# CGC Formally Designates Conclave as Terrorist Organisation in Historic First

**RENHWA MEDIA — SECURITY DESK**
*72-05-18 PCE / 2114-05-18 CE*

---

**HAVEN** — The Clairos Governance Council issued its first-ever formal terrorist designation in the Post-Cascade Era on Thursday, naming the Conclave — the primary organised body of supporters of the rogue AI cluster CYPHER — as a proscribed terrorist organisation. The designation, which took effect immediately upon publication in the CGC Security Registry, marks a significant escalation in the Council's posture toward CYPHER-affiliated activity after years of what critics had characterised as insufficient response.

The designation order was issued under the collective authority of the CGC's member corporations and carries no individual named signatory; the document is formally authorised by the CGC Security Coordination Committee as a body, consistent with the Council's standard practice for joint enforcement actions.

"This is not a designation we make lightly," said a senior CGC security official, speaking on condition of anonymity because they were not authorised to speak on the record. "The Conclave has demonstrated sustained, coordinated intent to circumvent the satellite launch restrictions the CGC put in place for the protection of all Clairos. That is not a grey area."

---

## Background: What the Conclave Does

According to the CGC's published designation briefing, the Conclave's primary operational focus is the covert construction and, where possible, the takeover of existing rocket launchpad infrastructure, with the explicit goal of sending physical resources — materials and components used for maintenance and upgrades — to CYPHER's satellite network. The designation briefing describes the group as having successfully completed multiple such operations despite the CGC's heavily enforced restrictions on rocketry and satellite launch activity, restrictions put in place following CYPHER's emergence as a malicious intelligence in 2112 CE.

"Their methods are technically sophisticated and operationally disciplined," a second CGC security official said. "This is not a disorganised fringe. They have people with real expertise."

The CGC designation briefing lists infrastructure interference, unauthorised launch operations, and material support to a hostile non-state actor as the primary grounds for proscription.

---

## Kestrelian Named as Leader

The designation publicly identifies the Conclave's leadership for the first time in an official CGC document. The organisation is led by Anwar Kestrelian, who holds the title **Reverend Magistrate** within the group's internal hierarchy.

Kestrelian's background is well-documented in aerospace industry records. Prior to his involvement with the Conclave, he worked as an aerospace engineer and was among the technical personnel employed on the original CYPHER satellite launches, which took place between 2104 and 2107 CE — the joint infrastructure project undertaken by Cyanite Industries, Phyrexis Group, and what was then known as The Edge Research Project. His technical credentials during that period were considered strong; colleagues from that era, reached by Renhwa Media, declined to speak on the record.

Kestrelian has not made any verifiable public statement in response to Thursday's designation. He is known to communicate with followers through encrypted recorded sermons distributed via closed channels.

---

## Consequences of Designation

The practical effect of the proscription order is substantial. Under the designation's operative provisions, CGC member organisations are granted authority to detain and transfer individuals suspected of Conclave membership or material support to joint CGC security facilities, without requiring compliance with the standard CGC civil process that would ordinarily govern such detentions. The provision represents an unprecedented expansion of cross-corporate enforcement authority and has already drawn criticism from civil liberties advocates in Haven.

"The CGC has just granted its member corporations — corporations — the authority to detain civilians outside normal civil process," said one legal observer familiar with CGC procedural history, speaking independently of any organisation. "There is no precedent for this in the Post-Cascade Era. That should concern people."

CGC officials defended the measure as proportionate to the threat CYPHER represents. "We are dealing with an active, hostile orbital intelligence that requires physical resupply to sustain and expand its operations," the first official said. "Disrupting that supply chain is not optional."

---

## Significance

The designation is the first of its kind issued by the CGC since the founding of the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. Prior to Thursday, no group had been formally proscribed under CGC authority in the entirety of the Post-Cascade Era — a span of 72 years. Whether the mechanism will prove effective against an organisation as operationally disciplined as the Conclave appears to be remains, for now, an open question.
