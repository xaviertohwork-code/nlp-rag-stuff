# Phyrexis-Backed Infrastructure Bill Stalls as Genesis, TEC Demand Budget Concessions

**RENHWA MEDIA — POLITICAL DESK**
*Senior Political Correspondent Jared Eto-Kwesi | 77-05-03*

---

## SUMMARY: THE BILL AND ITS TROUBLES

A joint infrastructure funding proposal backed by the Old 3 Firms — Phyrexis Group, Cyanite Industries, and ONE Network Enterprises — has been stalled in the Clairos Governance Council since 77-03-22, with no resolution in sight. The proposal, initially valued at **14.2 billion Phi Credits**, was introduced as a broad Haven infrastructure modernization package, covering upgrades to port facilities, transit corridors, and shared utilities across multiple sectors. Months later, it sits effectively frozen, as the CGC's two newest members have transformed a routine appropriation vote into a protracted negotiation over who gets what.

It is, say those watching closely, an entirely predictable outcome from a body increasingly unable to conduct the basic business of governance.

---

## GENESIS LABS: THE SOMATIC CONDITION

Genesis Labs has made its position clear and costly. According to sources familiar with the deliberations, Genesis has formally conditioned its support on an earmark of **2.1 billion Phi Credits** for Somatic Clinic licensing expansion in Central Haven — a provision conspicuously absent from the Old 3's original drafting.

The demand reflects Genesis Labs' longstanding ambition to extend its Somatic Clinic footprint into Haven's centrally administered zone, which currently falls under collective CGC administration rather than any single megacorporation's sector control. Central Haven's mixed residential population represents a significant untapped market for Genesis's services, and its neutral status has historically made it a contested prize for every megacorp seeking expanded influence.

Founder and CEO Cary Mullin declined to comment for this article. Genesis Labs' official spokesperson offered only that the company "remains committed to ensuring that any CGC appropriation benefits the broadest possible range of Havenites through access to clinically supervised health services."

---

## TEC: THE QUIET VETO

The Edge Corporation has been characteristically less explicit in its opposition, but no less effective. TEC has raised concerns that the current draft does not adequately preserve **1.8 billion Phi Credits** in Edge network maintenance funding, framing its position as a technical observation rather than a formal demand. Observers have not been so generous in their interpretation.

"When TEC says the numbers 'concern' them, the Council has learned that is a condition," said one senior Havenite policy analyst, speaking without attribution. "They have not formally voted against the bill. But their acquiescence has a price, and they've named it."

For the record: TEC has not cast a vote against the infrastructure proposal. What it has done is make clear, through back-channel CGC deliberations, that 1.8 billion Phi Credits in Edge maintenance allocation is not a line item it views as negotiable.

---

## VEYANOVA: 'FISCALLY IRRESPONSIBLE'

The response from Phyrexis Group, which invested considerable political capital in assembling the original Old 3 coalition behind the bill, has been pointed.

Madame Anuntra Veyanova, Chair of Managing Partners and Phyrexis Group's representative in public-facing CGC commentary, did not mince words when reached for comment.

**"The demands being raised by the new members of this Council are fiscally irresponsible given the Council's current deficit position,"** she said, in a statement delivered to this correspondent in written form. "The proposal before the Council was developed responsibly, in consultation with the full range of infrastructure stakeholders. It does not require revision to accommodate the expansion ambitions of its newest members."

It is the kind of statement that signals a negotiating ceiling has been reached — or at least that Phyrexis wishes to project that it has.

---

## THE ARITHMETIC OF DYSFUNCTION

The numbers, as one unnamed CGC Secretariat official put it, are simply not adding up.

"If both sets of conditions were met as currently stated," the official told Renhwa Media, "the combined demands would push the total appropriation to **18.1 billion Phi Credits**." The official declined to speculate on whether any figure between 14.2 billion and 18.1 billion might prove acceptable to all parties.

That gap — and what it represents about the CGC's capacity for functional governance — is the real story. The Council has not passed a major discretionary spending resolution since **75 PCE**, a fact its own members acknowledge with varying degrees of embarrassment. The one recent exception is the routine biennial Floodwall Maintenance Levy appropriation approved on **77-03-04**, a vote that carries no meaningful political controversy because no one wishes to be on record opposing the infrastructure keeping Haven above water.

Everything else, it seems, is subject to the mathematics of mutual leverage.
