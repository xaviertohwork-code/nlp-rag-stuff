## SECTION 1: INCIDENT SUMMARY

On 76-05-14, CGC Counter-Terrorism Division forces successfully interdicted an unauthorized rocket launch attempt by operatives affiliated with the Conclave at a converted industrial site located 47 kilometers northwest of Sarento. The site, a former Cyanite Industries manufacturing facility, had been covertly repurposed by Conclave operatives in advance of a planned resupply launch targeting CYPHER's orbital infrastructure network. The interdiction prevented the launch and resulted in the detention of seven Conclave operatives. Three additional operatives escaped the interdiction perimeter.

No CGC personnel sustained life-threatening injuries during the operation. The site has been secured and placed under joint CGC-Cyanite Industries custodial authority pending full forensic processing.

---

## SECTION 2: SITE ANALYSIS

The launch site occupied the footprint of a Cyanite Industries heavy-component manufacturing facility that was formally decommissioned eight months prior to the interdiction operation following Cyanite's consolidation of production capacity in Sarento proper. The facility's decommissioning was publicly recorded in Cyanite's quarterly asset disposition filings.

Conclave operatives had exploited the site's vacancy with considerable operational investment. External modifications were minimal and deliberately consistent with the visual profile of an inactive industrial property, a finding that accounts in part for the delay between the facility's repurposing and its detection by CGC intelligence channels. Internally, however, operatives had installed a portable launch gantry assembly, fuel management infrastructure, and communications equipment. Structural reinforcements had been made to the facility's primary loading bay to accommodate vertical launch alignment.

The sophistication of the internal modifications is consistent with Conclave operational planning attributed to Reverend Magistrate Anwar Kestrelian's insider knowledge of launch infrastructure, though Kestrelian's personal involvement in this site's preparation cannot be confirmed (see Section 6: Intelligence Assessment).

## SECTION 3: TACTICAL TIMELINE

**76-05-11:** Initial intelligence reporting flags anomalous vehicle movement patterns at the target coordinates, consistent with heavy-equipment transfer.

**76-05-13:** Surveillance assets confirm the presence of a portable launch assembly at the site. Counter-terrorism interdiction authorization is requested and granted by CGC Secretary-General's office under standing Resolution 77-[REDACTED] authority.

**76-05-14, 02:17:** CGC Counter-Terrorism Division ground units deploy from the Sarento staging facility.

**76-05-14, 04:38:** CGC forces breach the facility perimeter and initiate interdiction. The breach occurs 22 minutes before the Conclave's scheduled launch window, as confirmed by recovered countdown documentation. The rocket was fueled and in final pre-launch configuration at the time of entry.

**76-05-14, 04:52:** Site secured. Seven operatives detained. Three operatives breach the eastern perimeter gap during the initial entry phase; pursuit operations fail to recover these individuals.

---

## SECTION 4: PAYLOAD ANALYSIS

The rocket payload was partially encased and partially staged for loading at time of interdiction. Forensic inventory of the payload manifest, cross-referenced against physical contents, identifies approximately 1,400 kilograms of electronics components and replacement parts. Component categorization indicates materials consistent with satellite maintenance and systems-upgrade requirements for a network of the scale and complexity attributed to CYPHER's orbital array infrastructure.

No biological materials, weaponized substances, or anomalous technology were identified within the payload. The electronic components themselves represent no independent threat; their significance is exclusively in their intended destination.

---

## SECTION 5: PERSONNEL SUMMARY

**Operatives detained:** Seven (7). All seven are in CGC custody. Identifications are pending; initial processing indicates at least four individuals with prior Conclave-affiliated records in CGC intelligence databases. Interrogation is ongoing; preliminary sessions have yielded limited cooperative disclosure.

**Operatives escaped:** Three (3). Based on trajectory analysis from perimeter sensor data, the three escaped individuals departed via watercraft staged on the facility's northwest river access point — a route not covered by the initial perimeter deployment. Their current whereabouts are unknown. Facial recognition data from site surveillance is being processed; results are pending.

---

## SECTION 6: INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT

The operational planning quality of this attempt — site selection, modification discipline, launch window timing, and pre-positioned exfiltration assets — represents a significant capability step relative to the Conclave attempts documented in 74 PCE. The Division assesses this as consistent with an operation benefiting from experienced infrastructure and logistics guidance.

**Notably, Reverend Magistrate Anwar Kestrelian was not present at the site and is not believed to have been physically involved in coordinating the launch attempt.** This absence is assessed as operationally significant rather than incidental. Kestrelian's deliberate non-presence at a site bearing the hallmarks of his technical knowledge suggests an intentional compartmentalization strategy — Kestrelian is functioning as a remote knowledge source and planning authority, insulating himself from capture by maintaining physical separation from active launch operations.

This represents a maturation of Conclave operational security doctrine that poses significant implications for future interdiction strategies. Prior analysis had assumed Kestrelian's personal presence at technically complex operations; this assumption must now be revised. Kestrelian's current location is unknown.

The three escaped operatives are assessed as a priority recovery target. Their exfiltration's pre-positioning strongly suggests that at least one senior Conclave figure with knowledge of the site's layout had contingency planning authority — raising the possibility that one of the three escapes was deliberate and planned, rather than opportunistic.

---

## SECTION 7: RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Immediate expansion of satellite surveillance coverage over decommissioned Cyanite Industries facilities across the northwest Sarento corridor and analogous sites in other CGC industrial zones.

2. Revision of Conclave threat-modeling frameworks to account for a remote-command operational structure in which Kestrelian does not physically attend operations.

3. Accelerated processing of captured operatives' identifications through cross-referenced Conclave intelligence archives.

4. Formal request to Cyanite Industries for advance notification of future facility decommissioning events to enable proactive CGC monitoring.

5. Review of eastern perimeter gap exposure in standard CGC breach deployment doctrine to eliminate analogous exfiltration vulnerabilities in future operations.
