## I. STATEMENT OF CONCERN

The CGC Infrastructure Committee convened in emergency session on 73-09-12, three days following the declaration of a Category Amber emergency at Cyanite Industries Floodwall Reactor 4. Having reviewed the incident timeline, contemporaneous communications logs, and written statements submitted by Cyanite Industries' designated liaison, this Committee records the following formal finding:

The Committee expresses **grave concern** at the 11-hour delay between Cyanite Industries' internal Category Amber declaration and the formal notification of the CGC Infrastructure Committee. The Floodwall reactor network is not merely a Cyanite Industries commercial asset. It is the singular physical infrastructure upon which the continued habitability of Haven Island depends. The Committee's existing notification framework, codified under Resolution 46-IR-003, was designed precisely to ensure that disruptions of this magnitude are communicated to member representatives without delay. An 11-hour gap between declaration and notification is not a procedural irregularity. It is a failure of the intercorporate accountability this Committee exists to enforce.

The Committee further notes that the Category Amber designation, as defined in Resolution 46-IR-003 Annex B, constitutes a threshold event requiring immediate cross-institutional awareness. No interpretation of existing policy supports an 11-hour internal review window before external notification.

---

## II. RECORD OF COMMITTEE DISCUSSION

### Opening Remarks and Member Positions

The session was opened by the Committee Chair, who invited each member representative to register their position on the incident prior to deliberation.

**Renhwa Media** provided the most direct and unambiguous criticism of any member in attendance. Renhwa Media's representative described the notification failure as *"an unacceptable breach of intercorporate trust,"* arguing that any megacorporation entrusted with operating critical shared infrastructure must be held to a higher standard of transparency than would apply to purely commercial operations. The representative further stated that Renhwa Media reserved the right to escalate formally to the full CGC if the Committee's response was found to be insufficient.

**Genesis Labs** echoed these concerns in formal written remarks submitted to the record: *"Genesis Labs views with the utmost seriousness any incident in which the integrity of Haven's foundational infrastructure may have been compromised and not communicated to fellow CGC members in a timely manner. We expect full accountability."* Genesis Labs' representative additionally requested that the resulting audit extend to all Category Amber-capable systems across the Floodwall network, not only Reactor 4.

**Phyrexis Group** did not offer substantive criticism of Cyanite's conduct during open session. Phyrexis Group's representative indicated in preliminary terms that the Group would be unlikely to oppose well-drafted amendments to notification procedures, and encouraged the Committee to proceed with measured deliberation. The representative did not commit a formal position at this stage.

**ONE Network Enterprises** similarly offered a measured preliminary response, noting the operational importance of timely notifications and indicating preliminary support for strengthening existing provisions. ONE's representative did not formally commit to a specific amendment framework.

### Cyanite Industries' Response

Cyanite Industries' representative formally objected to the characterization of the delay as a notification failure, stating that the 11-hour interval was caused by *"standard internal verification procedures"* and was not the result of intentional withholding. The representative argued that Cyanite's existing protocols require engineering staff to complete a preliminary diagnostic confirmation before escalating an alert externally, in order to prevent unnecessary alarm in response to transient sensor anomalies.

The Committee found this explanation inadequate. Regardless of the internal rationale, Resolution 46-IR-003 imposes no carve-out for internal verification windows of this duration. The Committee notes further that Cyanite Industries' representative formally objected to the proposed threshold increase discussed during this session, citing increased Levy costs as a constraining factor — an argument the Committee found insufficient justification for delaying external notification of a Category Amber event.

The Committee records that Cyanite's defense, as presented, does not constitute grounds for treating the delay as compliant with existing obligations.

## III. PROPOSED AMENDMENTS TO RESOLUTION 46-IR-003

The Committee has drafted the following proposed amendments for referral to the full CGC for a vote.

**Proposed Amendment A — Mandatory Notification Window:**
Resolution 46-IR-003 shall be amended to require that any megacorporation operating CGC-designated critical infrastructure notify the CGC Infrastructure Committee **within 60 minutes** of any internal Category Amber or higher emergency declaration, regardless of the status of internal verification or diagnostic procedures. Completion of internal verification shall not be a prerequisite for external notification; subsequent updates to the notification may reflect verification outcomes as they become available.

**Proposed Amendment B — Escalation Acknowledgement Protocol:**
Any notification submitted under the amended window requirement must receive written acknowledgement from the Committee Secretariat within 30 minutes of receipt. Failure to receive acknowledgement shall require the notifying entity to escalate directly to the CGC Chair on duty.

**Proposed Amendment C — Maintenance Scheduling Disclosure:**
Operators of Category Amber-capable systems shall submit annual maintenance scheduling summaries to the Infrastructure Committee no later than the first business session of each PCE calendar quarter, beginning in 74 PCE.

**Voting Outlook:**
The proposed amendments have been referred to the full CGC for a vote. Phyrexis Group and ONE Network Enterprises have both signaled preliminary support for the amendments as drafted, though neither has issued a formal commitment. Genesis Labs is expected to vote in favor. Cyanite Industries is expected to oppose. The Committee Chair notes that the referral carries the Committee's unanimous recommendation for passage.
