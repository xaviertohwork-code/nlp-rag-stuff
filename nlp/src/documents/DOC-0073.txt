## SECTION 1 — SITUATION

On 76-06-09, the CGC Security Directorate received an alert originating from a routine maritime overflight indicating anomalous thermal signatures consistent with pre-ignition launch vehicle fueling at a converted fishing dock located approximately **620 kilometers west of Haven Island**, on an uncharted atoll not previously flagged in the Directorate's Conclave Infrastructure Registry. Subsequent rapid assessment confirmed the presence of an unauthorized launch vehicle in advanced pre-launch configuration.

All available evidence at the time of interdiction indicated the vehicle was intended to deliver a resupply payload to the CYPHER orbital network, consistent with known Conclave logistics activity in the region.

---

## SECTION 2 — MISSION

CGC Rapid Response Unit 3 was tasked with the immediate interdiction and destruction of the launch vehicle prior to any orbital insertion attempt, the detention or neutralization of all personnel present at the site, and the preservation of intelligence materials for post-action exploitation.

---

## SECTION 3 — EXECUTION

**0342 hours, 76-06-09** — Thermal anomaly first registered by maritime overflight asset. Signature elevation above baseline consistent with cryogenic propellant loading. Alert transmitted to CGC Security Directorate Operations Center.

**0351 hours** — Operations Center confirms launch vehicle identification. CGC Rapid Response Unit 3 activated. Emergency tasking order issued.

**0417 hours** — Unit 3 assets airborne. Complement: **8 autonomous combat drones** (Type-III interdiction configuration) and a **12-person strike team** (mixed breacher and security roles).

**0509 hours** — Drone advance element reaches engagement envelope. Site reconnaissance confirms: one launch vehicle, vertical on pad, fueling in progress; eleven personnel identified on-site; no air defense assets detected.

**0514 hours** — Strike authorization granted by CGC Operations Center Duty Director.

**0516 hours** — Drone element engages launch vehicle propellant assembly and primary structural frame. **Launch vehicle destroyed on the pad. No payload reached orbit.** Vehicle did not achieve ignition; destruction occurred during fueling phase.

**0518 hours** — Ground strike team fast-ropes to site perimeter. Conclave personnel attempt dispersal toward two rigid-hull inflatables moored at the dock.

**0527 hours** — Site secured.

---

## SECTION 4 — RESULTS

### 4.1 Operational Outcome

The launch vehicle was destroyed in its entirety prior to ignition. No payload reached orbit or any sub-orbital trajectory. The atoll site sustained significant structural damage to dock and crane infrastructure; two storage buildings were destroyed in secondary detonations attributable to propellant reserves.

### 4.2 Casualty Summary

**Conclave Personnel:**
- Killed in action: **3**
- Detained: **5**
- Escaped: **3** (via water; pursuit assets redirected, recovery unsuccessful)

**CGC Personnel:**
- Killed in action: 0
- Injured: **2** (non-critical; one ballistic fragment wound, one blast concussion; both returned to duty within 72 hours)
- Missing: 0

### 4.3 Materials Exploited

Encrypted data storage devices (3), recovered from personnel and site office. Hard-copy logistics manifests. Partial launch vehicle technical schematics. Analysis ongoing.

---

## SECTION 5 — INTELLIGENCE ASSESSMENT

### 5.1 Identification of Detained Operatives

Post-detention biometric processing and cross-referencing against the Directorate's Conclave Personnel Registry identified all five detained operatives as members of **Cell Kestrel-7**, a known Conclave logistics cell previously associated with procurement and transport of launch-critical components across the Sharpsea region. Cell Kestrel-7 had not been positively located since 74 PCE. Its reactivation and operational capability at this scale represents a significant intelligence failure in cell-tracking continuity.

### 5.2 Intelligence Failure Analysis — Detection Delay

The central failure of this operation was not in its execution but in its latency. The Directorate's pre-existing monitoring architecture did not flag the atoll site during any period preceding 76-06-09. Post-action site inspection and detainee interviews confirm that **Conclave operatives had disguised the launch vehicle as a commercial fishing crane for a minimum of 14 days prior to detection.** The vehicle's structural profile, coloring, and partial jacketing were consistent with heavy-lift crane equipment of the type common throughout island-chain maritime operations. Absent thermal or spectroscopic interrogation at close range, the disguise was effective against passive optical and radar survey at standard overflight altitudes.

The 14-day preparation window represents a sustained, undetected operational posture within what the Directorate had implicitly treated as a lower-risk maritime zone. This assumption is no longer supportable.

It should additionally be noted, for the record, that one unauthorized launch site has been identified on an uncharted atoll approximately **800 kilometers northeast of Haven Island** in a separate intelligence stream — details of which fall outside the scope of this report but are referenced for Directorate awareness in evaluating regional coverage architecture.

---

## SECTION 6 — RECOMMENDATIONS

**R-01 (Commander Answorth, Priority: URGENT):** The CGC Security Directorate should immediately initiate procurement and deployment of upgraded remote sensor coverage across all converted maritime infrastructure — fishing facilities, repurposed cargo installations, decommissioned industrial platforms — within **1,000 kilometers of Haven Island**. Passive optical survey alone is insufficient against adversaries with demonstrated capability and willingness to execute 14-day covert disguise operations.

**R-02:** Cell Kestrel-7's remaining at-large members (estimated 4–7, per Registry) should be elevated to Priority Red tracking status. Exploitation of recovered data storage devices should be expedited to reconstruct the cell's supply chain.

**R-03:** The Directorate should review whether current Conclave Infrastructure Registry protocols adequately account for atoll and sub-classified maritime structures outside Haven's primary monitoring footprint, with particular attention to structures that would not require formal construction permits under any extant regulatory framework.
