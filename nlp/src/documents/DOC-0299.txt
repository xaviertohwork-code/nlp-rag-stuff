# WE THE OPTED
## Position Paper: Principles of Civic Autonomy in a Post-Edge Society

**Document Reference:** WTO-PP-001
**Date:** 76-03-12
**Classification:** OPEN
**Issued by:** We The Opted, Haven Island

---

### Opening Statement

We The Opted is a humanist political advocacy organization founded in 2113 by Mira Delacroix-Vey, and by Mira Delacroix-Vey alone. No co-founder shares that origin. What began as a gathering of individuals who had chosen to purge Edge nanobots from their bodies — and who found themselves stripped of economic and social standing for that choice — has grown into the most significant civic voice in Haven for those who refuse to surrender their personhood to corporate infrastructure.

This position paper sets forth the foundational principles upon which We The Opted stands: the Four Pillars of Civic Autonomy. These are not aspirations. They are non-negotiable conditions for a society that can be called free.

---

### The Four Pillars of Civic Autonomy

**Pillar I — Bodily Sovereignty**

No corporation, council, or governance body holds legitimate authority over what a citizen does or does not introduce into their own body. This applies to Edge nanobots. It applies to Somatic Augmentation products. It applies to every biological and technological intervention that the megacorporations of Haven have commercialized, normalized, and in many cases quietly made economically mandatory.

We The Opted maintains that Opting — the act of purging Edge nanobots and their influence from one's body — is a fundamental right that no megacorporation may restrict, penalize, or condition access to basic services upon. A Havenite who chooses to Opt must not find themselves denied housing, employment, medical care, or civic participation as a result. The current architecture of Haven makes such denial routine. We name it for what it is: coercion dressed as commerce.

**Pillar II — Informational Self-Determination**

Citizens have the right to know what is being done to their minds. Edge's nanobot infrastructure modifies behavior, shapes perception, and harvests behavioral data at a scale no Havenite fully comprehends — because The Edge Corporation has never been required to disclose the full scope of those modifications. We The Opted calls on the Clairos Governance Council to mandate complete public disclosure of all nanobot behavioral modification protocols currently active in Haven's population. Every algorithm. Every trigger. Every nudge. Citizens cannot consent to what they cannot see.

Informational self-determination extends beyond Edge. It includes the right to access, correct, and delete one's own data held by any CGC member corporation. It includes the right to communicate without corporate intermediation. It includes the right to receive news and analysis that has not passed through a corporate content review mechanism before reaching one's eyes.

**Pillar III — Economic Independence**

Phi Credits are denominated, tracked, and settled entirely through infrastructure controlled by Phyrexis Group. Food, shelter, medicine, and access to the Edge are all mediated by the megacorporations. The result is not an economy — it is a system of managed dependency in which no Havenite can fully exercise civic choice because every act of daily survival is entangled with corporate interest.

We The Opted advocates for the development and recognition of non-corporate economic mechanisms: mutual aid networks, community resource-sharing, and the formal recognition of barter and non-Phi exchange as legitimate economic activity that may not be penalized or taxed into inaccessibility. Economic independence is not a utopian demand. It is the minimum condition for meaningful political autonomy.

**Pillar IV — Cognitive Clarity**

The Edge is designed to feel like freedom. It is designed to make disconnection feel like loss. The nanobot architecture driving these effects operates on the human nervous system with mechanisms that TEC continues to designate a trade secret. We The Opted holds that no technology may be legally deployed on a citizen's cognition without that citizen's fully informed, revocable, and non-coerced consent.

Cognitive clarity means the right to think without engineered interference. It means the right to experience grief, boredom, uncertainty, and dissatisfaction without an algorithm interpreting those states as symptoms to be corrected. It means the right to be a full human being, not a user.

---

### On Organizational Principles

We The Opted is a non-violent organization. We state this clearly and without qualification: violence is not a legitimate political tool, and We The Opted does not endorse, coordinate with, or provide cover for organizations that employ it. Our power is the power of people who have chosen clarity over comfort, and that power does not require explosions to be real.

Since our founding, We The Opted has grown steadily because the conditions that gave rise to it have not improved — they have worsened. In 71 PCE, we counted 340 registered members: individuals who had Opted, or who had come to oppose the megacorporate stranglehold on Havenite life, and who were willing to attach their names to that opposition. As of the publication of this position paper in 76 PCE, that number stands at over 340,000 registered members. The growth reflects not our organizational brilliance but the spreading awareness that something in Haven has gone deeply wrong.

---

### Conclusion

The Four Pillars — bodily sovereignty, informational self-determination, economic independence, and cognitive clarity — are not a wish list. They are the architecture of a society in which people, rather than corporations, hold sovereign authority over their own lives. We The Opted exists to build that architecture, document by document, member by member, and protest by protest.

We ask every Havenite who reads this paper to consider what it would mean to live in a city built on these principles. Then we ask them to help us build it.
