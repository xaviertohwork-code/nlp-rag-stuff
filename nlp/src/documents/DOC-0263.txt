# CYPHER Infrastructure Project — Construction Delay Assessment Report, Phase II

**Document Reference:** CYI-PROJ-CYPHER-0263
**Classification:** YELLOW
**Authoring Organisation:** Cyanite Industries — Major Projects Division
**Author:** Project Manager, CYPHER Infrastructure Programme
**Date:** 57-08-22

---

## Executive Summary

This report documents the causes, financial implications, and corrective actions associated with the Phase II construction delays affecting the CYPHER Infrastructure Project. The launchpad complex and associated ground support infrastructure, originally planned for completion in 56 PCE (2098 CE), will not reach full readiness until 60 PCE (2102 CE), representing a four-year delay against the approved project baseline. Satellite launches remain projected to commence in 62 PCE (2104 CE) and to conclude in 65 PCE (2107 CE), consistent with the launch programme's dependency on ground infrastructure readiness.

The delay is attributable to three primary root causes: complications in coastal land reclamation, rare earth mineral supply chain disruption, and structural safety incidents requiring site remediation. Total cost overrun is estimated at 780 billion Phi Credits above the original approved budget of 3.2 trillion Phi Credits. Corrective actions are underway and the revised 60 PCE completion target is assessed as achievable with current resource commitments.

## Delay Root Causes

### 3.1 Land Reclamation Complications

Land reclamation for the launch site footprint proved materially more difficult than the baseline survey data anticipated. Rising sea levels at the reclamation zones, which had accelerated beyond the projections used in the original feasibility study, resulted in repeated inundation of partially consolidated fill areas, requiring remediation and re-consolidation work that was not included in the original scope.

The project's geotechnical assessment noted that **Senior Survey Engineer Dorel Prochazka**, who served as the responsible authority for the original land stability sign-off in 51 PCE, formally approved the reclamation zone boundaries based on sea-level projections that are now considered outdated. Internal review has determined that the projections, while consistent with published data available at the time of approval, did not incorporate the accelerated melt-rate modelling that became available in 52 PCE. This finding is documented for programme record purposes; it does not constitute a disciplinary or liability determination. The Prochazka approval decision is cited here solely to establish the point at which the reclamation scope baseline was fixed.

Remediation added approximately eleven months to this work stream.

### 3.2 Rare Earth Mineral Supply Chain Disruptions

Procurement of rare earth minerals required for the fabrication of launch cradle components experienced significant supply chain disruption over the 54–56 PCE period. Shortfalls in available rare earth mineral supply led to a **14-month delay in the fabrication of launch cradle components** across all three launchpad complexes. Fabrication contractors were unable to source materials to specification within the contracted delivery windows; substitution with alternative-grade materials was assessed and rejected on grounds of structural tolerance requirements.

The procurement record identifies multiple upstream sources of supply constraint, including extraction yield variability and competing demand from concurrent large-scale fabrication programmes. This report does not attribute primary causation to any single supply node; a separate commercial assessment is under preparation by the Procurement Division.

### 3.3 Workforce and Equipment Shortfalls

Turnover among specialist groundworks crews, combined with delays in delivery of heavy compaction equipment, contributed an estimated six months of additional slippage at the Alpha and Beta complexes. Retention incentive measures implemented in 55 PCE partially mitigated further attrition.

## Safety Incidents

### Incident Summary — Launchpad Complex Gamma

**Date:** 56-11-03
**Incident Type:** Structural Collapse
**Severity:** Fatal

During preparatory works at Launchpad Complex Gamma, a partially completed launch support gantry section experienced a structural failure, resulting in the collapse of an estimated 400 square metres of elevated formwork. **Three construction workers were killed** in the incident. A further seven personnel sustained injuries of varying severity; two required extended medical treatment.

The incident triggered a full-site suspension at Complex Gamma lasting 47 days, pending structural investigation and remediation. The investigation determined that the failure originated in a connection assembly that had been installed during a period of degraded material quality oversight. Revised inspection protocols have been implemented across all active construction zones.

The deaths of the three workers are recorded with formal acknowledgement in this report. Full incident documentation is retained under reference CYI-SAF-0263-GAMMA.

---

## Financial Impact

The total cost overrun associated with the Phase II delay is estimated at **780 billion Phi Credits** above the original approved project budget of **3.2 trillion Phi Credits**. The overrun reflects remediation expenditure, extended contract durations, workforce retention measures, materials re-procurement, and delay penalties. Full cost certification will follow infrastructure completion.

---

## Corrective Actions

1. Revised geotechnical monitoring protocols have been implemented at all active reclamation zones, incorporating updated sea-level projection datasets.
2. Rare earth procurement has been placed under a multi-supplier framework with increased strategic reserve requirements to reduce single-source exposure.
3. Enhanced structural inspection procedures, implemented following the Complex Gamma incident, are now applied across all active construction zones.
4. Programme management has been restructured to consolidate reporting lines and improve early identification of compounding delay risks.

The revised completion target of 60 PCE (2102 CE) is assessed as achievable under current resourcing.
