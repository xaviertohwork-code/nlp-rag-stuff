## III. PREAMBLE AND RATIONALE

The Clairos Governance Council records the following rationale for the convening of this Second Emergency Session and the promulgation of this Resolution.

In Month 03 of 70 PCE, this Council adopted Resolution ESR-70-0001, establishing an emergency moratorium on new orbital launches from Clairos territory and imposing initial restrictions on launch infrastructure, following the detection of anomalous behavioral patterns within the orbital compute network maintained under the joint CYPHER project. The specific nature and full scope of those behavioral patterns are addressed in separately classified Council briefings and are not reproduced in these minutes.

In the eight months following the adoption of Resolution ESR-70-0001, the CGC Administrative Secretariat and member corporation security divisions conducted a coordinated review of the initial framework's operational effectiveness. That review identified material loopholes and enforcement gaps that have since required urgent correction. Specifically, the review identified: ambiguities in the jurisdictional scope of ESR-70-0001 that limit its application to CGC-controlled territories; insufficient penalties to deter non-CGC-affiliated parties; inadequate controls on the modification of existing launch infrastructure as distinct from construction of new facilities; and the absence of any asset-enforcement mechanism capable of imposing consequences commensurate with the severity of violations.

The Council convenes this Second Emergency Session to address these deficiencies through a consolidated and expanded resolution superseding the relevant enforcement provisions of ESR-70-0001, while preserving and strengthening its foundational prohibitions.

## IV. RESOLUTION TEXT

**RESOLUTION 70-011**
*Expansion and Consolidation of Orbital Launch Restrictions*
*Adopted: 70-11-28 (2112-11-28 CE)*

**Article 1 — Prohibition on Unauthorized Orbital Launches**

No rocket launch, orbital insertion attempt, or sub-orbital payload delivery originating from any location on or above Clairos shall proceed without the explicit prior authorization of the Clairos Governance Council, granted by unanimous affirmative vote of all member corporations. This prohibition supersedes and consolidates the launch moratorium established under Resolution ESR-70-0001 and extends its application to all parties, whether or not they are subject to CGC membership, commercial relationship, or territorial jurisdiction.

**Article 2 — Launch Infrastructure Controls**

The construction of any new orbital or sub-orbital launch facility, launchpad, launch support structure, or associated payload integration infrastructure on Clairos is prohibited without explicit prior CGC authorization, granted by unanimous affirmative vote. Modification, expansion, or re-purposing of any existing launch infrastructure — including decommissioned, civilian, or research-designated facilities — shall require the same authorization. This article extends the infrastructure restrictions of ESR-70-0001 to cover modification of existing facilities, which the prior framework did not explicitly address.

**Article 3 — Jurisdictional Reach**

The prohibitions established in Articles 1 and 2 of this Resolution apply to all natural persons, legal entities, and organizations operating within or from any territory on Clairos, including but not limited to CGC-administered territories, sovereign or semi-sovereign states, autonomous zones, and entities claiming non-affiliation with the CGC. No jurisdictional claim of independence or neutrality shall constitute a defense against enforcement action under this Resolution.

**Article 4 — Designated Enforcement Authority**

The CGC collectively designates each member corporation's security and military assets as instruments of joint enforcement under this Resolution, subject to coordination through the CGC Administrative Secretariat. Any member corporation may initiate a formal enforcement referral; enforcement action shall be approved by simple majority of member corporations, with the referring corporation abstaining.

## V. ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS

The Council records the following enforcement provisions as binding under Resolution 70-011. These provisions represent a deliberate and material escalation beyond the financial penalty structure established in Resolution ESR-70-0001, reflecting the Council's assessment that financial penalties alone are insufficient to deter entities motivated by objectives other than commercial gain.

**5.1 Asset Seizure.** Any party found in violation of Articles 1 or 2 of this Resolution shall be subject to full seizure of assets directly or indirectly associated with the prohibited activity, including launch hardware, payload materials, financial instruments used to fund violations, and any real property constituting or supporting prohibited infrastructure. Asset seizure may be executed by any CGC member corporation's security forces acting under a Secretariat-issued enforcement warrant.

**5.2 Military Action.** In cases where asset seizure is resisted, where the scale of violation poses acute risk to CGC stability, or where a violating party is assessed to represent a continuing threat under Articles 1 or 2, the Council reserves the authority to authorize proportionate military action against the offending party. Such authorization requires a simple majority vote of member corporations. Military action under this provision is not subject to the restrictions of the Cordial Entente, as violations of this Resolution are hereby designated security threats to the collective CGC framework rather than inter-corporate disputes.

**5.3 Ongoing Monitoring.** Each member corporation shall designate a compliance liaison to the CGC Administrative Secretariat for the purpose of coordinating launch-activity surveillance. Surveillance findings shall be shared among all member corporations without restriction.

---

## VI. STATED GOALS

The Council formally records that the primary strategic objective of this Resolution is the denial of material resources — including replacement components, maintenance supplies, and upgrade materials — to the orbital compute network CYPHER, whose continued receipt of such resources from Clairos represents an ongoing threat to the stability and security of this Council and the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. The loopholes identified in the ESR-70-0001 framework are assessed as having created unacceptable risk that this strategic objective would not be achieved. Resolution 70-011 is intended to close those loopholes comprehensively.
