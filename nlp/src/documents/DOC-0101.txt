# Central Haven Arts Collective Announces 77 PCE Neon Tides Festival Lineup

**Haven Municipal Bulletin | 77-02-14 | Civic & Community Affairs**

---

The Central Haven Arts Collective is pleased to announce the full lineup for the 77 PCE Neon Tides Festival, now entering its **14th consecutive year** as Haven's premier public celebration of light, sound, and human expression. First held in 64 PCE (2106 CE), Neon Tides grew from a modest two-day gathering of street muralists in the Civic Plaza's eastern colonnade into the sprawling multi-disciplinary event Havenites know and love today. What began as an informal exchange between a handful of visual artists and a single projection crew has grown, year by year, into one of the few occasions on which all of Central Haven seems to exhale at once.

---

## Event Details

This year's festival runs from **77-03-08 through 77-03-10** in the **Central Haven Civic Plaza**, spanning all three public terraces and the connecting walkways between the Fountain District and the Meridian Steps. Gates open each day at 14:00 and remain open until 02:00 the following morning, with the main stage programming concentrated in the evening hours.

**Entry is free for all Havenites** — no registration, no ticket, no barrier. Simply arrive. For those wishing to access premium Edge-enhanced viewing experiences at designated installations, optional viewing passes are available for **150 Phi Credits** per person for the full three-day run. These passes unlock layered digital overlays, curated audio environments, and priority positioning at select venues, but are entirely optional; every main stage performance and the majority of installations are fully accessible without them.

---

## This Year's Theme: Layered Realities

The 77 PCE festival takes **"Layered Realities"** as its organizing theme, inviting artists to interrogate the invisible architectures that shape how Havenites perceive their city, their bodies, and each other. The Collective has assembled a lineup that deliberately bridges the full spectrum of sensory experience — **both Edge-integrated and Opted-accessible installations** are central to this year's programming, a curatorial choice that festival director Ouma Thiess-Rallard describes as "not a compromise, but the point."

Headlining the main stage are returning fan favourite **Cassian Drouet & the Interference Collective**, whose live sound-sculpting performances have closed Neon Tides twice before, and first-time main stage act **Yeva Malorek**, a movement and projection artist whose recent residency in the northern residential districts drew considerable community attention. Spoken word, percussion ensemble, and mixed-media performance slots fill the supporting stages across all three evenings.

---

## Installations

This year's installation programme is among the most ambitious in the festival's history, with twenty-three commissioned works spread across the Civic Plaza's terraces and alcoves. Among the highlights:

**"Tidal Memory"** by Sorrel Onkendi occupies the full length of the Lower Terrace with a flowing bioluminescent textile installation responsive to ambient sound — no Edge connection required, drawing on touch and proximity sensors alone. At the opposite end of the spectrum, **"Threshold Bloom"** by the Duo Halverstam-Cress is designed exclusively for Edge-enhanced viewing pass holders, presenting a layered urban-ecology simulation visible only through Edge-integrated perspective.

Several installations have been specifically designed to offer meaningfully different but equally complete experiences depending on whether or not the viewer is connected to the Edge. Notably, **"Adjacent Frequencies"** by Pale Maren, which occupies the Meridian Courtyard, presents one visual composition to Fullwalkers through Edge-layer overlays, and an entirely distinct — though thematically parallel — composition in the physical medium of suspended glass and ambient light for those who are Opted or otherwise Edge-unconnected. The Collective has emphasized in its programming notes that neither version is considered primary.

---

## Attendance and Planning

Last year's festival saw **approximately 340,000 visitors across the three days of programming**, a record at the time and a figure that tested the Civic Plaza's crowd-management infrastructure. In response, the Municipal Transit Authority has confirmed extended service hours on all Central Haven loop routes throughout the festival weekend, and temporary pedestrian flow corridors have been agreed with the adjoining sector administrations.

The Arts Collective encourages early arrival on all three evenings. Food and drink vendors will be licensed across the Lower and Mid Terraces, with a dedicated non-commercial artisan market running throughout the daytime hours on 77-03-09.

For further information, installation maps, and the full performance schedule, visit the Central Haven Civic Notice boards or contact the Arts Collective liaison office directly.

*The Neon Tides Festival is a Central Haven Arts Collective programme, supported by the Haven Municipal Cultural Development Fund.*
