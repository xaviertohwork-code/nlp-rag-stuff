## SECTION I: OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE

The coordinated multi-corporate action against Wampa Robotics, conducted at the 25th CGC anniversary ceremony and its immediate operational aftermath, proceeded according to the phased assault timeline established in pre-operation planning. The initial coordinated assault on Wampa facilities across Haven was completed within 7 hours of first contact, representing a successful execution of the suppression and seizure phase.

It must be noted, however, that the successful completion of the initial assault did not constitute the end of required military effort. The operational assumption that Wampa's loyalist workforce would capitulate rapidly following the primary facility seizures proved to be an underestimate. Elements of Wampa personnel — primarily drawn from their security divisions and automated systems maintenance corps — dispersed into Haven's broader residential and industrial districts rather than surrendering. It required several months after the initial assault for the combined forces of the CGC to suppress the majority of these guerrilla remnants operating across Haven. This timeline, while longer than initially projected, falls within acceptable operational bounds and should not be characterized as a failure of the primary action. The pacification effort concluded substantially within the 28 PCE calendar year, confirming that the operation achieved its primary strategic objective.

**Assessment:** Operational performance on the primary seizure phase rates as SATISFACTORY. Extended pacification requirements rate as ANTICIPATED COMPLICATION, not OPERATIONAL FAILURE.

---

## SECTION II: ASSET ACQUISITION OUTCOMES

Asset redistribution was conducted pursuant to the proportional distribution formula agreed among participating CGC members prior to the operation. This document formally rejects any characterization of the redistribution as having proceeded on an equal-distribution basis — the formula was explicitly proportional, calibrated to reflect each member corporation's operational contribution to the action and their pre-existing industrial footprint in the relevant manufacturing sectors. Those who contributed more received more.

Under this proportional formula, **Cyanite Industries acquired approximately 31% of Wampa's physical manufacturing assets**, representing the largest single-member allocation in the redistribution. This outcome is assessed as materially favorable. The acquired asset portfolio includes primary robotic fabrication infrastructure from three of Wampa's Haven facilities, a portion of Wampa's materials processing equipment (which presents strong synergy with Cyanite's existing extraction and heavy manufacturing operations), and a selection of specialized tooling installations from the Wampa North Haven assembly complex.

The Strategic Analysis Group considers the 31% acquisition figure a positive outcome given the scale of the distributed redistribution and the number of participating members. Cyanite's proportional contribution to the operation warranted this allocation, and we assess that it was appropriately recognized in the final distribution. This acquisition meaningfully strengthens Cyanite's manufacturing base and positions the company to absorb significant portions of the former Wampa production capacity once integration work is completed.

## SECTION III: ROBOTICS TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION CHALLENGES

Translating physical asset acquisition into productive operational capacity is the central challenge of the post-seizure period. The Strategic Analysis Group has conducted a preliminary technical assessment of the acquired Wampa automated production lines, drawing on evaluations by Cyanite's senior engineering teams who were granted access to the assets in the immediate post-redistribution window.

**Integration timeline estimate:** Cyanite assesses that full integration of Wampa's automated production lines into Cyanite's operational framework will require an estimated 14 months from the redistribution completion date. This estimate accounts for the following factors:

- Wampa's proprietary control architecture is not natively compatible with Cyanite's existing production management systems. Significant software re-engineering will be required, and Wampa's technical documentation — while largely intact following the seizure — reflects an internal engineering culture that prioritized modularity for Wampa's own expansion rather than interoperability with external systems.
- Several of the acquired fabrication units sustained minor physical damage during the extended pacification operations, requiring repair prior to operational activation.
- Training requirements for Cyanite supervisory and maintenance personnel to operate the new automated systems are non-trivial. Wampa's workforce expertise has been lost; institutional knowledge must be rebuilt internally.

The 14-month estimate is contingent on uninterrupted access to acquired facilities and on the continued suppression of disruption attempts targeting Cyanite-controlled assets. Risks to this timeline are addressed in Section IV below.

## SECTION IV: RESIDUAL PACIFICATION OPERATIONS AND ONGOING RISKS

As noted in Section I, the majority of Wampa guerrilla remnants were suppressed within several months of the initial assault. However, the Strategic Analysis Group assesses that isolated loyalist cells remained operationally active into late 28 PCE. The persistence of these cells beyond the main pacification window represents a manageable but non-negligible ongoing security concern.

Of particular operational relevance: Wampa loyalist cells conducted sporadic attacks on redistribution convoys through late 28 PCE. These attacks have not succeeded in reversing any material redistribution outcome, but they have introduced friction into logistics operations and created localized security expenditures that were not anticipated in original operational planning. The attacks appear to target Cyanite and other member convoys without significant discrimination, suggesting that residual loyalists are motivated by generalized opposition to the redistribution rather than by targeted intelligence about specific asset movements.

**Recommended posture:** Continued elevated security protocols on all asset transfer and logistics operations connected to Wampa redistribution should remain in force through at least mid-29 PCE. The Strategic Analysis Group recommends a formal review of residual threat levels at 29-06-00 PCE to assess whether standing-down elevated protocols is advisable.

---

## SECTION V: SUMMARY ASSESSMENT

The Wampa action has delivered material strategic benefit to Cyanite Industries. The 31% asset acquisition under the proportional distribution formula is the largest single-member share and constitutes a meaningful industrial expansion. Integration challenges are real but manageable within the estimated 14-month window. Residual loyalist activity is an irritant, not a strategic threat.

The Strategic Analysis Group assesses overall operation outcome as: **FAVORABLE.**
