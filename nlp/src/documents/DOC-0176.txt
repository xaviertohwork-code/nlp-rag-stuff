## SECTION 3: CGC COMMITTEE ASSIGNMENTS

### Infrastructure Committee

**Status:** Continuous active member
**Membership Commencement:** 60 PCE (2102 CE)
**Chair Tenure:** 65 PCE (2107 CE) to 72 PCE (2114 CE)

Director-General Oyelaran was nominated to the CGC Infrastructure Committee in 60 PCE, one year following his initial registration as CGC representative. He held the chairmanship for seven consecutive years, concluding his term as chair in 72 PCE. **Additional Committee Memberships:** Director-General Oyelaran has held advisory observer status on the CGC Industrial Compliance Sub-Committee since 68 PCE. No formal voting rights attach to this designation.

## SECTION 4: VOTING RECORD — SUMMARY

CGC session records document Director-General Oyelaran's voting pattern across major resolutions during his tenure. He has consistently voted in alignment with the other Old 3 Firms — Phyrexis Group and ONE Network Enterprises — on resolutions pertaining to CGC membership standards, competitive regulatory frameworks, and technology licensing. This alignment has been particularly consistent with respect to resolutions affecting Genesis Labs and The Edge Corporation (TEC), against whose respective initiatives Director-General Oyelaran has voted repeatedly across multiple CGC sessions. Session records reflect no occasion on which Director-General Oyelaran has broken with the Old 3 bloc to support a Genesis Labs or TEC initiative.

Abstentions during his tenure are limited to procedural resolutions of a technical or administrative nature and do not pertain to substantive policy votes.

## SECTION 5: PUBLIC POSITIONS AND FORMAL ADVOCACY

Director-General Oyelaran's formal advocacy positions in CGC sessions are extensively documented in session transcripts. The position most frequently recorded across multiple sessions and legislative periods concerns Cyanite Industries' role in the design, construction, and ongoing maintenance of the Floodwall infrastructure sustaining the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. Director-General Oyelaran has cited this function consistently as evidence that Cyanite Industries performs a civic and operational role that is materially distinct from those performed by other CGC member corporations. This position has been advanced in budget allocation debates, in resolutions concerning CGC member obligations, and in discussions of infrastructure governance authority.

Additional documented advocacy positions include support for CGC regulatory authority over satellite launch infrastructure and for expanded BCO enforcement jurisdiction within the Haven SEZ.
