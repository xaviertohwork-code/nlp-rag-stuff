## PREAMBLE

WHEREAS the Clairos Governance Council (hereinafter "the Council") was constituted under the Blackshore Accords of 4-02-17 (17th Day of the Second Month, Year 4 PCE) for the purpose of ensuring the stability, continuity, and equitable development of the Haven Island Special Economic Zone and the broader economic order of Clairos;

WHEREAS the Council retains the authority, pursuant to Article 11(c) of the Blackshore Accords as amended, to act against any member entity whose conduct, operations, or strategic posture is determined by supermajority vote of the remaining members to constitute a systemic threat to the Council's governance capacity and the stability of the corporate order it upholds;

WHEREAS the Council has conducted a review of the conduct and strategic posture of Wampa Robotics, Incorporated (hereinafter "the Subject Entity"), the findings of which are summarized in Annex A (CLASSIFIED — [REDACTED]) to this Resolution;

WHEREAS the Council has determined, upon the basis of findings documented in Annex A and the supplementary intelligence assessments referenced in Annex B ([REDACTED]), that the Subject Entity constitutes a systemic threat to Council stability within the meaning of Article 11(c);

NOW, THEREFORE, the Council hereby resolves as follows.

## ARTICLE III — ORDER OF DISSOLUTION

**Section 3.1 — Immediate Effect.**
Effective upon the passage of this Resolution, Wampa Robotics, Incorporated is hereby dissolved as a legal entity operating within the Haven Island Special Economic Zone and within any territory subject to CGC regulatory authority. Its membership in the Council is terminated with immediate effect.

**Section 3.2 — Cessation of Operations.**
All commercial, manufacturing, research, and administrative operations of the Subject Entity within CGC-regulated territory shall cease immediately upon passage of this Resolution. [REDACTED] shall be responsible for coordinating the physical enforcement of this cessation order in accordance with the operational plan filed separately as Annex D (classification level [REDACTED]).

## ARTICLE IV — ASSET DISTRIBUTION

**Section 4.1 — General Principle of Proportional Distribution.**
The assets of the dissolved Subject Entity, enumerated in the Asset Schedule filed as Annex E (RESTRICTED), shall be distributed proportionally among the remaining member entities of the Council — Phyrexis Group, Cyanite Industries, ONE Network Enterprises, and Renhwa Media — according to the proportional weighting formula set out in Section 4.2 below. No consideration, payment, or compensation of any kind shall be owed by any receiving member entity to any other party in respect of assets received under this Article.

**Section 4.2 — Proportional Weighting Formula.**
Asset distribution shall be calculated according to each member entity's proportional share of aggregate audited Council-member revenue for the most recently completed fiscal year (Year 27 PCE), as certified by the CGC Secretariat's Financial Compliance Office. For the avoidance of doubt:

> **(a)** Physical assets, including but not limited to manufacturing equipment, facilities, robotics inventory, and [REDACTED], shall be distributed in accordance with the proportional shares certified under this Section, subject to logistical allocation adjustments as approved by a simple majority of receiving members.

> **(b)** Financial assets, including cash holdings, receivables, and contracted revenue streams of the Subject Entity, shall be distributed in strict numerical proportion without adjustment.

> **(c)** In the event that any physical asset category is not divisible, the receiving member entity whose proportional entitlement most nearly corresponds to the full value of that asset shall receive it in whole, with compensating adjustments applied to subsequent asset categories.

**Section 4.3 — Intellectual Property: Declassification and Free Distribution.**
Notwithstanding the proportional distribution formula set out in Section 4.2, all patents, registered designs, trade secrets, technical specifications, source materials, and other intellectual property held by Wampa Robotics, Incorporated at the time of dissolution (hereinafter collectively "the IP Portfolio") shall be treated as follows:

> **(a)** The entirety of the IP Portfolio is hereby **declassified** and shall be made available to all four remaining CGC member entities simultaneously and without condition. No member entity shall be required to pay any fee, royalty, acquisition cost, licensing charge, or other consideration of any kind to obtain access to or rights over any element of the IP Portfolio. The IP Portfolio is distributed to all receiving members at no cost.

> **(b)** Each receiving CGC member entity shall hold full rights to exploit any element of the IP Portfolio for internal commercial purposes without restriction.

> **(c)** Each receiving CGC member entity shall further hold the right to sublicense any element of the IP Portfolio to external parties, including entities not members of the Council, for commercial gain, subject only to the notification requirement set out in Section 4.3(d). No revenue generated by a CGC member entity through such sublicensing shall be subject to Council revenue-sharing obligations.

> **(d)** A receiving CGC member entity that enters into a sublicensing agreement with an external non-CGC party shall notify the CGC Secretariat of the existence (though not necessarily the terms) of such agreement within thirty days of its execution.

**Section 4.4 — Encumbered Assets.**
Assets subject to existing third-party liens, security interests, or contractual obligations are listed in Annex E, Schedule 2. Resolution of encumbrances shall be the responsibility of [REDACTED] on behalf of the receiving membership collectively. [REDACTED].

## ARTICLE V — PROHIBITIONS AND SUCCESSOR ENTITIES

**Section 5.1 — Prohibition on Reconstitution.**
No entity constituted in whole or in substantial part from the personnel, assets, or intellectual property of the dissolved Subject Entity may apply for CGC membership or operate within CGC-regulated territory under a successor name or reformed corporate structure without the unanimous consent of all then-current CGC members.

**Section 5.2 — Prohibition on Monopoly Accumulation.**
In recognition of the harms associated with the Subject Entity's historical monopoly position in labour automation and autonomous robotics, no single CGC member entity shall be permitted to acquire through this dissolution more than [REDACTED]% of the total assessed value of the physical asset categories enumerated in Annex E, Schedule 1(a) through 1(f). Distribution adjustments required to enforce this cap shall be resolved by the CGC Secretariat's Financial Compliance Office in consultation with receiving members.
