## I. STATEMENT OF PROTEST

The Republic of Zonnon, acting in its own capacity and in recognition of the shared interests of all constituent nations of the Sharpsea Bloc, hereby lodges this formal diplomatic protest against CGC Resolution 77-014. The Republic categorically rejects the authority of the Clairos Governance Council to impose mandatory tracking and transponder requirements upon vessels registered under the sovereign flag of any Bloc nation, and demands the immediate withdrawal of this Resolution.

This protest is lodged in accordance with established principles of sovereign jurisdiction and in the name of the people of Zonnon Island, whose economic and civic life depends upon the freedom of navigation that this Resolution would fundamentally compromise.

---

## II. BACKGROUND AND PASSAGE OF RESOLUTION 77-014

CGC Resolution 77-014 was passed on 17 January 77 PCE (17 January 2119 CE) by a vote of four in favor to one against. The sole dissenting vote was cast by Genesis Labs. No other member of the Clairos Governance Council joined that dissent; the Resolution passed with the affirmative support of all remaining CGC members.

The Resolution mandates the installation of CGC-standard transponders aboard all Bloc-registered commercial vessels transiting within two hundred nautical miles of Haven Island, with transmission of positional and cargo data routed continuously to CGC satellite tracking infrastructure during such transit. Non-compliant vessels are subject to denial of access to Haven-adjacent shipping lanes and associated port facilities.

The Republic of Zonnon notes Genesis Labs' dissent for the record.

---

## III. LEGAL ARGUMENTS: EXTRATERRITORIAL OVERREACH

The Republic of Zonnon submits that CGC Resolution 77-014 constitutes unlawful extraterritorial overreach and is void as against the Republic and all Bloc-registered vessels sailing under Zonnon's flag.

The Sharpsea Bloc — comprising the Republic of Zonnon, the Kashikari Hegemony, and the Nyari Confederation — is not a signatory to any CGC jurisdictional agreement. No treaty, accord, memorandum of understanding, or instrument of adhesion has ever been concluded between the CGC and any Bloc nation that would extend CGC regulatory or enforcement jurisdiction to Bloc-registered vessels operating in international waters. The Haven Island Special Economic Zone and its immediately adjacent maritime space may fall within CGC administrative competence; the international shipping lanes traversed by Bloc commercial vessels do not. The CGC possesses no lawful basis to impose equipment installation requirements, data transmission obligations, or compliance conditions upon vessels registered under the sovereign authority of a non-member state.

The Republic further submits that the two-hundred-nautical-mile threshold specified in Resolution 77-014 is drawn so broadly as to encompass substantial portions of open international waters routinely transited by Bloc commercial fleets. To require transponder installation as a precondition of such transit is to assert prescriptive jurisdiction over foreign vessels in international waters — a power the CGC has never been granted by any instrument to which the Bloc is party.

---

## IV. PRECEDENT ANALYSIS: A PATTERN OF UNILATERAL APPLICATION

The Republic of Zonnon regrets that Resolution 77-014 does not arise in isolation. This protest formally cites three prior instances in which CGC resolutions were applied unilaterally to non-CGC territories and non-member entities without legal basis:

The first such instance occurred in 2109 CE, when CGC regulatory standards were imposed upon shipping manifests originating from Bloc ports without any Bloc government's consent or negotiation.

The second occurred in 2112 CE, when CGC broadcast and communications restrictions were extended to non-Haven media infrastructure operating within nominal range of Haven-controlled satellite networks.

The third occurred in 2116 CE, when CGC accession standards, developed and enacted as internal CGC membership criteria, were selectively cited as a basis for restricting the access of non-CGC commercial entities to Haven port facilities.

In each of these three instances, the CGC acted unilaterally, without consultation, without consent, and without any governing jurisdictional instrument authorizing the application of its internal resolutions to sovereign non-member entities. Resolution 77-014 represents a fourth such act, and the Republic of Zonnon submits that this pattern constitutes a systematic posture of overreach that this protest places formally on the international record.

---

## V. IMPACT ASSESSMENT

The practical consequences of Resolution 77-014 for Zonnon and the broader Bloc are severe. The Republic's Maritime Commerce Secretariat estimates that approximately **1,400 Bloc-registered commercial vessels** conduct transits within the Resolution's two-hundred-nautical-mile zone on an annual basis. This figure encompasses the full range of Zonnon's export trade fleet, including the offshore aquaculture transport vessels that carry protein-farming exports to Haven markets — trade upon which Zonnon's export economy substantially depends.

Mandatory transponder installation at CGC-specified standards would impose significant capital costs upon vessel operators, the majority of whom are small to mid-size Zonnon commercial enterprises without the financial infrastructure to absorb regulatory compliance burdens imposed by a body to whose authority they have never consented. The ongoing transmission of positional and cargo data to CGC satellite infrastructure raises acute concerns regarding commercial confidentiality, competitive intelligence, and the integrity of Bloc shipping operations more broadly.

The cumulative effect of compliance, were it to be accepted, would be the practical subordination of Bloc maritime commerce to CGC surveillance and control — precisely the form of economic subjugation that the nations of the Sharpsea Bloc have consistently and formally rejected.

---

## VI. DEMAND FOR WITHDRAWAL

The Republic of Zonnon demands the following:

**First**, that the Clairos Governance Council formally withdraw CGC Resolution 77-014 in its entirety.

**Second**, that the CGC Secretariat issue a written acknowledgment that Resolution 77-014 does not apply to, and shall not be enforced against, vessels registered under the sovereign flag of any Sharpsea Bloc nation.

**Third**, that the CGC commit to consultative engagement with Bloc governments prior to the passage of any future resolution bearing upon Bloc-registered vessels, Bloc-adjacent waters, or Bloc commercial infrastructure.

The Republic of Zonnon reserves all rights under applicable principles of sovereign jurisdiction and international maritime law, and reserves the right to take such further action as the circumstances require should the CGC fail to respond substantively to this protest within sixty days of its submission.
