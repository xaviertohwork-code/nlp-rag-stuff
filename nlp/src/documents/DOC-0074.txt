# CGC Marks 70th Anniversary of Blackshore Accords with Subdued Ceremony

**RENHWA MEDIA — HAVEN CORRESPONDENT DESK**
*By Correspondent Eleni Vasquez | 74-04-04 PCE (2116-04-04 CE) | OPEN*

---

CENTRAL HAVEN — The Clairos Governance Council marked the 70th anniversary of the Blackshore Accords on Thursday with a ceremony that, by almost any measure, reflected the current state of the body it was meant to celebrate: technically functional, visibly strained, and notably short on enthusiasm.

The event was held at the CGC Assembly Chamber in Central Haven — a space that, in the ordinary course of CGC business, collects dust between formal sessions while the megacorporations conduct their real negotiations elsewhere. Thursday's occasion gave the Chamber its most prominent use in recent memory, and the result was, at best, a reminder of why the Quicksilver Speakeasy has become the preferred venue.

---

## Outside the Chamber: The More Energetic Gathering

Whatever the ceremony lacked in vitality, the scene outside the Assembly Chamber more than compensated. Approximately 300 We The Opted demonstrators gathered on the plaza surrounding the venue, waving placards bearing slogans that have become familiar fixtures of any CGC public occasion — "OUR BODIES, NOT YOUR MARGINS" and "70 YEARS OF WHAT, EXACTLY?" among the more printable ones.

The protesters were organized with the discipline that has come to characterize WTO's public actions: marshals in orange armbands maintained crowd order, medic stations were positioned at the plaza's corners, and a sound system carried rotating speakers through a programme of testimonials from Opted citizens whose words carried across the square even through the Assembly Chamber's reinforced windows. The demonstration remained peaceful throughout. CGC security forces, deployed in numbers that suggested anticipation of something larger, found themselves with little to do.

---

## A Historical Background Note

The Blackshore Accords were signed in 4 PCE — 2046 CE — at the coastal city of Blackshore, then still recognizable as the tourist destination it had once been before the Cascade remade the world's coastlines. That document formally created the Haven Island Special Economic Zone, launching the land reclamation and Floodwall construction that would eventually produce the city of Haven as it exists today. Whatever one makes of the seventy years that followed, the Accords were unquestionably consequential. The CGC's official remarks on Thursday made sure to say so, at length.

---

## Inside the Chamber: Attendance Worth Noting

The attendance roster for Thursday's ceremony will be studied more carefully than the speeches delivered within it.

**In-Person Attendance:** Of the CGC's seated megacorporation Director-Generals, only Cyanite Industries' Takeshi Oyelaran attended in person. Oyelaran delivered the ceremony's keynote address, speaking in the plainspoken register he reliably deploys for public occasions — crediting Cyanite's engineering work on the Floodwalls and the "physical foundations on which Haven rests" as the Accords' most durable legacy. It was not a subtle framing.

**Remote Participation:** ONE Network Enterprises CEO Nguyen Anh Mai participated via holographic projection, her image rendered with the polished transmission quality that has become standard for her CGC appearances. Anh Mai's remarks were brief and focused on Haven's logistics infrastructure; she did not take questions.

Oyelaran was the only seated Director-General to attend the ceremony in person. Representation from Genesis Labs, the Edge Corporation, and Phyrexis Group was handled by delegation — senior officials, not principals, occupying the member seats. No explanation was offered publicly for the principals' absences, which is itself a kind of explanation.

---

## The Names Not Spoken

Perhaps the most pointed omission of Thursday's proceedings was one that will surprise no one familiar with CGC commemorative conventions: the ceremony included no acknowledgment of Wampa Robotics or Renhwa Media, both founding or former members of the CGC and both parties to the history that the occasion was ostensibly marking.

Wampa Robotics was among the Original 5 Firms that signed the Blackshore Accords in 2046 CE. Renhwa Media was as well. Their contributions to the CGC's founding — and the rather different ways in which their memberships concluded — apparently did not merit a mention in the formal programme. A CGC spokesperson did not respond to a request for comment on the omission before publication.

Readers may draw their own conclusions about whose history is being commemorated, and whose is being quietly set aside.
