**Issuing Authority:** CGC Security Directorate

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This assessment evaluates the threat of deliberate sabotage to Haven Island Floodwall infrastructure as of the third month of 76 PCE. The Floodwall system — maintained exclusively by Cyanite Industries and representing the sole barrier between Haven's 100 million residents and permanent inundation — constitutes the highest-consequence single-point dependency in Clairosian civic infrastructure. Any successful degradation of pumping capacity, even temporary, would constitute an existential emergency.

The assessment concludes that non-state threat actors collectively present a **moderate-to-low** aggregate sabotage risk at this time, with meaningful variance across individual actor profiles. The Spirit Liberation Front is assessed as the most credible near-term threat. The Conclave presents limited direct concern in this operational domain. No other organized threat actor has demonstrated capability or intent sufficient to warrant an individual rating above low.

Physical security gaps, concentrated at the northern shore reactor installations, represent the most significant addressable vulnerability in the near term.

---

## SECTION 1: THREAT ACTOR PROFILES

### 1.1 Spirit Liberation Front (SLF)
**Direct Floodwall Sabotage Threat Rating: MODERATE**

The SLF is assessed as the most credible sabotage threat to Floodwall infrastructure among currently monitored non-state actors. This rating reflects two compounding factors.

First, demonstrated technical capability: SLF attacks against TEC systems since 76 PCE have shown an ability to plan and execute coordinated infrastructure disruption operations requiring insider knowledge, multi-team deployment, and precise timing. The tactical sophistication evidenced against TEC's Black Box peripheral systems in 75 PCE and the recurring pattern of convoy disruptions is directly transferable to industrial infrastructure targeting.

Second, ideological scope: SLF's publicly stated opposition to **all megacorporation operations** — not merely those of TEC or Genesis Labs — logically encompasses Cyanite Industries' Floodwall mandate. Although SLF's recent operational focus has been primarily against Edge-related infrastructure, the Directorate assesses that this reflects prioritization rather than doctrinal constraint.

**Raw Intelligence Item — SLF Communiqué, 76 PCE (Early Quarter):**
> *"The walls that cage us were never built for us."*

*Analyst Commentary:* The phrase "walls that cage us" was flagged by automated monitoring as potentially referencing Floodwall infrastructure. Upon full-text review, the communiqué contextualizes the phrase as rhetorical commentary on CGC governance broadly — specifically, corporate control over Havenite movement and civic life. No specific operational language, infrastructure coordinates, or target indicators are present. **This communiqué does not indicate active operational planning against Floodwall infrastructure.** It is retained as a watch-item and should inform future intercept tasking. Its ideological framing is nonetheless consistent with eventual target escalation.

---

### 1.2 The Conclave
**Direct Floodwall Sabotage Threat Rating: LOW**

The Conclave presents a **low** direct threat to Floodwall infrastructure at this time. This rating is not a reflection of the Conclave's organizational capability — which the Directorate continues to assess as significant in narrow operational domains — but rather of a fundamental mismatch between their established operational focus and Haven's civic infrastructure as a target set.

Conclave operations are oriented overwhelmingly toward securing materials and launching-infrastructure access for CYPHER's maintenance and resupply pipeline. Their demonstrated activities — covert launchpad construction, cargo smuggling operations, exploitation of legacy satellite-launch infrastructure — serve a single strategic objective: sustaining CYPHER's orbital network. The Floodwall system offers no operational utility toward that objective. Disrupting Haven's civic infrastructure would, if anything, undermine the supply chains on which CYPHER's resupply operations depend.

The Directorate assesses that the Conclave's operational doctrine treats Haven stability as instrumentally useful, not as a target. Direct Floodwall sabotage is not consistent with Reverend Magistrate Anwar Kestrelian's known strategic posture.

*The Conclave is NOT rated high for direct Floodwall sabotage threat. Any intelligence product characterizing the Conclave as a high-priority Floodwall sabotage actor should be considered inconsistent with current assessment and referred to this Directorate for review.*

### 1.3 Other Non-State Actors
**We The Opted / Spirit Liberation Front (Adjacent):** WTO is assessed as posing zero sabotage threat; their operational doctrine explicitly rejects infrastructure violence. The possibility of SLF attempting to use WTO's public protest activity as cover for reconnaissance near Floodwall perimeters is a secondary watch concern, not a primary threat.

**Unaffiliated Criminal Networks:** Assessed low. No intelligence indicating criminal interest in sabotage as distinct from theft of Cyanite-managed industrial materials.

## SECTION 2: VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT

### 2.1 Primary Vulnerability: Nuclear Reactor Coolant Intake Channels

The Directorate identifies the **nuclear reactor coolant intake channels** as the single most vulnerable point in the Floodwall system. This assessment is grounded in operational history as well as structural analysis.

In 73 PCE, a bioaccumulation event — the uncontrolled proliferation of biological matter within the northern shore coolant intake infrastructure — caused a reduction in reactor thermal regulation efficiency significant enough to require a partial operational shutdown for remediation over a seventeen-day period. This incident was not the result of deliberate sabotage; it arose from inadequate monitoring of the intake filtration systems. Nevertheless, the 73 PCE bioaccumulation incident demonstrated unambiguously that the coolant intake channels represent a pathway through which a relatively modest external intervention can produce disproportionate effects on reactor output and, by extension, on pumping capacity.

A deliberate sabotage operation targeting the coolant intake channels — whether through introduction of contaminants, physical obstruction of filtration infrastructure, or targeted degradation of intake valve systems — would require less technical sophistication than a direct attack on the reactor cores themselves while producing comparable operational disruption.

No secondary Floodwall component approaches the coolant intake channels in assessed vulnerability.

---

## SECTION 3: CONSEQUENCE ANALYSIS

In the event of a successful coordinated attack disabling two or more reactor installations simultaneously, modeling indicates that pumping capacity would fall **below safe thresholds within hours**. This scenario does not require the destruction of reactor infrastructure — sustained interference with coolant intake or power distribution at multiple nodes would be sufficient to produce the same outcome over a compressed timeline.

The Directorate assesses this multi-reactor scenario as the planning contingency requiring the most urgent attention. Single-reactor disruption, while operationally significant, falls within Cyanite's established redundancy tolerance. Simultaneous multi-reactor disruption does not.

---

## SECTION 4: RECOMMENDATIONS

**4.1 Northern Shore Reactor Counter-Sabotage Patrols**

Physical security at the northern shore reactor installations is currently limited to **standard Cyanite industrial security protocols** — perimeter monitoring, access credentialing, and reactive response capacity. This posture is assessed as insufficient given the vulnerability profile of the coolant intake channels and the SLF's demonstrated capability for coordinated infrastructure targeting.

The Directorate recommends the **immediate deployment of dedicated counter-sabotage patrols along the northern shore reactor installations**, to be resourced from CGC Security Directorate personnel rather than Cyanite industrial security exclusively. Patrol schedules should be randomized and should specifically include underwater inspection cycles covering coolant intake approaches.

**4.2 Intake Channel Monitoring Upgrade**

Automated biological and chemical detection at coolant intake filtration stages should be upgraded to continuous real-time monitoring. The 73 PCE incident represents a documented exploitation pathway that has not been fully closed.

**4.3 SLF Intercept Tasking**

The 76 PCE communiqué referencing "the walls that cage us" should be used to calibrate future intercept collection against SLF internal communications for escalation indicators. Specific watch terms and infrastructure-adjacent language patterns are appended to the classified annex.
