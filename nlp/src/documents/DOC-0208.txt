### 1. OPENING FLEET SUMMARY

At the close of Q4 76 PCE, ONE Network Enterprises' combined commercial fleet numbered **1,247 active vessels**. This figure reflects the full operational inventory excluding units formally decommissioned or transferred during the quarter. Against an internal availability target of 85%, the overall fleet availability rate closed the quarter at **81.3%**, representing a shortfall of 3.7 percentage points relative to target and a decline of approximately 1.1 points from the Q3 76 PCE close. The availability shortfall is attributable to a combination of extended drydock cycles, supply chain disruption affecting maintenance throughput, and an above-average concentration of fuel system service orders. Each of these contributing factors is addressed in the relevant sections below.

---

### 2. COMMERCIAL FLEET MAINTENANCE POSTURE

Maintenance order volume for the quarter was materially shaped by the continued aging of the Transoceanic-class cargo carrier segment. Fuel cell replacements for Transoceanic-class vessels accounted for **40% of all maintenance orders** processed during Q4, placing sustained pressure on service bay allocation across ONE's primary port facilities. Throughput in those bays was further constrained by supply chain conditions detailed in Section 4. The elevated proportion of fuel cell orders is expected to persist through at least Q2 77 PCE pending a fleet-wide replacement cadence review currently underway within the Directorate.

---

### 3. MILITARY FLEET READINESS

The military fleet contingent comprised **196 vessels** at the end of Q4 76 PCE. Of these, **22 were in drydock for scheduled maintenance** at the quarter close. The remaining 174 vessels are assessed as operationally available, though eight carry conditional readiness flags pending minor repairs not requiring drydock cycling. No mission-critical capability gaps attributable to the drydock figure have been identified in this assessment period. The 22-vessel drydock count is within the range of historical quarterly norms for the military contingent and does not represent a departure from planned maintenance scheduling.

---

### 4. DRYDOCK BACKLOG AND SUPPLY CHAIN STATUS

At ONE's primary Haven shipyard, the drydock backlog stood at **34 vessels awaiting scheduled maintenance** at quarter close. This figure represents demand ahead of current throughput capacity and has increased from 28 vessels at the Q3 close, a trend the Directorate attributes primarily to the throughput drag described below rather than to a spike in unplanned maintenance events.

Supply chain conditions during Q4 contributed meaningfully to the backlog accumulation. Hull plating shipments from Cyanite Industries — it should be noted that the delayed material was hull plating specifically, not titanium alloy sheets or any other Cyanite product line — experienced average delivery delays of **18 days** across the quarter. These delays interrupted scheduled plating work on vessels mid-cycle, in several cases extending individual drydock durations and preventing the clearance of earlier-queued vessels. Coordination with Cyanite's logistics office has been documented; no commitment to improved delivery cadence for Q1 77 PCE has been received as of this memorandum's date.

---

### 5. SUMMARY OBSERVATIONS AND FORWARD INDICATORS

Fleet availability at 81.3% against an 85% target, a drydock backlog of 34 vessels at Haven, and a maintenance order profile dominated by Transoceanic-class fuel cell work collectively define a readiness posture that is functional but operating below standard. The Directorate will issue Q1 77 PCE interim tracking no later than 77-02-28. COO review of backlog reduction options, including temporary capacity agreements with third-party facilities, is recommended before end of 77-01.
