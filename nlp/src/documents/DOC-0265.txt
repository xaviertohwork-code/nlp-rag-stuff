# First CYPHER Satellites Launch From Haven Complex — 'A New Computational Age,' Say Project Leaders

**RENHWA MEDIA — TECHNOLOGY DESK**
*Filed 62-03-08 | Haven Island Special Economic Zone*

---

Haven Island erupted in celebration early this morning as the first batch of CYPHER satellites climbed from the launch complex on Haven's northern shore, streaking skyward in a column of white light that drew tens of thousands of spectators to rooftops, waterfronts, and the open promenades of central Haven. The launches mark the long-anticipated opening of what project leaders from the joint venture — **Cyanite Industries**, **Phyrexis Group**, and **Edge Research** — are calling a transformational moment for the whole of Clairos.

The project, a rare and ambitious collaboration between three of the Clairos Governance Council's most powerful members, has been years in the making. No outside organization — including, contrary to some pre-launch speculation in the trade press, any aerospace contractor — holds a stake in the venture. The partnership is, and has always been, Cyanite, Phyrexis, and Edge Research, a point that all three organizations were quick to emphasize in the run-up to this morning's event.

---

## Launch Day

The first payload lifted at 06:14 local time, with crowds already massed along the waterfront barriers of north Haven, their faces tilted upward in the grey morning light. Cheers rolled across the sector with each successive launch, vendors working the crowd with hot food and commemorative broadsheets, children perched on the shoulders of parents who had, in many cases, waited through the night.

**The first batch comprises 64 modules, launched over a three-day window beginning today**, with the remaining payloads staggered across the schedule to allow ground crews to verify telemetry between launches. The modules will take up their designated orbital positions over the coming weeks, with full network integration proceeding in phases thereafter.

Security cordons kept the immediate launch infrastructure restricted, but a designated public viewing zone in north Haven's coastal park was packed well beyond its intended capacity by mid-morning, and Haven Transit reported record early-morning ridership for the third consecutive day. Office towers across north and central Haven offered improvised viewing platforms from upper floors; at least two corporate communications teams posted footage to the civic networks within minutes of liftoff.

---

## 'A Computational Age Restored'

The mood among the project's leadership was one of barely contained pride. Speaking at a joint press briefing held at Cyanite Industries' north Haven facility following the first successful payload deployment confirmation, representatives of all three partner organizations addressed the assembled press.

"What you witnessed this morning is the beginning of a new computational age for Clairos," said a senior Cyanite Industries project executive, to sustained applause from the gathered correspondents and invited guests. "We did not simply build a satellite network. We rebuilt the foundation that the Cascade took from us."

That context — the Cascade and its aftermath — was woven throughout the briefing. The catastrophic flooding of 2042 had devastated coastal infrastructure across Clairos, with datacenters, many of which had been sited near the water to exploit cooling resources, suffering particularly severe losses. The near-total destruction of that compute capacity had, in the decades since, imposed a ceiling on the technological and economic development of Clairos from which it had never fully recovered.

A representative from Phyrexis Group struck a similarly expansive tone. "The Cascade took something from every person on Clairos — not just shorelines and cities, but the invisible infrastructure that let civilization function at scale. What Cyanite, Phyrexis, and Edge Research have built together is the answer to that loss. We're giving Clairos back its mind."

Edge Research's delegation, the smallest of the three but visibly energized, offered technical commentary on the phased integration schedule, confirming that the full launch sequence is expected to be completed by **65 PCE**, at which point the complete CYPHER network will be operational and available to support the next generation of computing-dependent technologies across Haven and beyond.

---

## What Comes Next

The completion of today's first launches opens what analysts are already calling an inflection point. With sustained orbital compute capacity available for the first time since the Cascade wiped out terrestrial equivalents, research teams across Haven — in materials science, biotechnology, logistics modeling, and communications infrastructure — have been waiting for precisely this moment to begin projects that have been on hold, sometimes for years, for want of processing power.

A full celebratory reception hosted by the three joint venture partners is scheduled for this evening at venues across west and north Haven. The public viewing zone in north Haven's coastal park will remain open through the evening, with civic lighting activated along the waterfront.

Renhwa Media will provide full coverage of remaining launches across the three-day window.
