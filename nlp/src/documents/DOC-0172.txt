# CYANITE INDUSTRIES
## INTERNAL MEMORANDUM

**CLASSIFICATION: YELLOW**
**TO:** Sarento Facility Supervisors, All Divisions
**FROM:** Regional Operations Manager, Sarento District
**DATE:** 77-01-05
**RE:** Q1 77 PCE Shift Scheduling Updates — Rotation Changes, Overtime Protocols, and Planned Maintenance Downtime

---

### 1. SHIFT STRUCTURE — Q1 77 PCE ROTATION

Effective 77-01-08, the Sarento primary smelting complex will continue its standard operating schedule of three eight-hour shifts per day. Shift assignments for Q1 are attached as Appendix A. Supervisors are responsible for ensuring all positions are filled at changeover; unplanned gaps at the smelting floor will not be tolerated and must be escalated immediately to the relevant division lead.

Across all divisions, the Sarento facility employs approximately 6,800 workers. Headcount allocations by division are reflected in the Q1 roster. Supervisors noting discrepancies between attached rosters and actual floor counts must report these to the Sarento District HR Coordination Office within five business days of receiving this memo.

There are no changes to break schedules or changeover windows from Q4 76 PCE. Supervisors should nonetheless re-brief their sections on the procedures at the start of Q1 to ensure compliance.

---

### 2. OVERTIME PROTOCOLS

All overtime requests must be submitted through the **Cyanite Operations Portal** a minimum of **48 hours in advance** of the requested overtime period. Requests submitted outside this window will not be processed and will be returned to the submitting supervisor without exception. Plan accordingly, particularly around the maintenance window outlined in Section 3.

Supervisors anticipating elevated manpower requirements for the post-maintenance ramp-up period in late February are strongly advised to submit overtime requests early. Demand on the Portal during that period is expected to be high, and late submissions will not receive expedited handling.

---

### 3. SCHEDULED REACTOR MAINTENANCE — OUTPUT REDUCTION NOTICE

A scheduled reactor maintenance window of **72 hours** is confirmed for the period **77-02-10 through 77-02-13**. This is a planned, routine maintenance cycle coordinated with the facility engineering team. To be unambiguous: the duration is 72 hours, not 96 hours or any other figure that may have circulated informally — supervisors should correct any such misunderstandings within their sections immediately.

During this 72-hour window, facility output across the primary smelting complex and associated processing lines will be reduced by approximately **40%**. Shift scheduling during the maintenance window will remain at three eight-hour shifts per day; however, active workstations and line throughput will be curtailed in accordance with engineering directives to be distributed separately under cover of a RED-classified operations brief no later than 77-01-20.

Division supervisors should begin adjusting production targets and client-facing delivery commitments accordingly. Coordination with the Sarento logistics desk on downstream shipment scheduling is the responsibility of individual division leads.

---

### 4. ACTION ITEMS

Supervisors are required to:

- Confirm Q1 roster receipt and discrepancies by **77-01-13**
- Pre-submit anticipated overtime requests for the maintenance ramp-up period **no later than 77-02-06**
- Brief sections on the 72-hour maintenance window timeline and output reduction expectations by **77-01-31**

Questions regarding this memo should be directed to the Regional Operations Coordination Desk. Do not route operational queries through this office directly.
