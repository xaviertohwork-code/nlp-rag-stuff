## SITE BACKGROUND

Blackshore: formerly a coastal tourist destination, pre-Cascade. Distinguished by rare black sand beaches produced by local volcanic basaltic erosion. **Not an industrial port settlement.** Site is historically notable as the symbolic location where the Blackshore Accords were signed in 4 PCE (2046 CE), formally establishing the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. Cascade impact on Blackshore was severe — among the most complete coastal devastation on record in this region; prior urban and resort infrastructure almost entirely obliterated. Site subsequently transferred to Cyanite Industries operational jurisdiction for resource development purposes. Present survey purpose: systematic assessment of mineral deposit concentrations for extraction feasibility.

**54-05-17 / 09:55**
Transect BLS-B (ref: 44.73°S / 112.31°E). Coastal sediment layer notably thicker than projected — approx. 6.8m before bedrock contact. Visual ID of metallic mineral inclusions throughout. Significant magnetite banding evident at 2.1m and 4.4m depth. Multiple samples collected.

**54-05-17 / 11:18**
Transect BLS-C (ref: 44.76°S / 112.35°E). Spectral scanner readings: anomalously elevated titanium signature across full 40m transect width. Confirmed via portable XRF unit — titanium dioxide concentrations well above commercial extraction threshold. Vanadium co-occurrence detected in same layer; concentrations significant. Both minerals consistent with ilmenite-hosted and titanomagnetite-hosted ore assemblages typical of this volcanic geology.

**54-05-17 / 13:42**
Transect BLS-D (ref: 44.79°S / 112.38°E). Repeat XRF confirmations. Magnetite concentrations in coastal sediments: high-grade throughout sampled column. Titanium and vanadium readings maintain consistency with BLS-C findings. No anomalous readings suggesting contamination or instrument error.

## MINERAL SURVEY FINDINGS

Significant concentrations confirmed across sampled coastal sediment corridor:

- **Magnetite (Fe₃O₄):** High-grade banding at multiple depth horizons; consistent across all transects.
- **Titanium (as ilmenite / titanomagnetite):** Elevated TiO₂ values throughout BLS-C to BLS-F corridor; exceeds commercial extraction threshold at all sampled points.
- **Vanadium (V):** Co-hosted with titanium-bearing minerals; concentration levels warrant dedicated extraction planning.

All three identified as primary extraction targets. Additional trace mineral assemblages noted — detail in supplementary report CI-GEO-54-0441-S.
