## SUBJECT: Assessment of CGC Economic Coercion Vectors Against the Republic of Zonnon and Sharpsea Bloc, 2117

---

### SECTION I — THREAT OVERVIEW

This assessment catalogues identified and suspected economic coercion mechanisms deployed by the Clairos Governance Council against the Republic of Zonnon and, where applicable, against the Sharpsea Bloc as a collective. Analysis draws on signals intelligence, maritime monitoring data, and interagency reporting from Bloc partner nations. The period under primary review is 2114 to the date of this document, with particular attention to developments in the first half of 2117 CE. The CGC's coercive strategy appears increasingly deliberate and multi-vector in character, coordinating external trade pressure with internal political destabilization rather than relying on either mechanism in isolation.

---

### SECTION II — ECONOMIC COERCION VECTORS

#### II.A — Tariff and Sanctions Pressure

CGC-aligned commercial entities have in 2116 and 2117 progressively expanded the application of informal non-tariff barriers against Zonnon-origin aquaculture exports to Haven. These measures, applied through licensing delays at Haven's West Haven port authority rather than via formal CGC resolution, are designed to impose economic friction while preserving plausible deniability. Formal CGC sanction resolutions remain absent, likely reflecting the deadlock within the Council rather than any restraint of intent. This Division assesses that formal sanction instruments remain a credible escalatory option should CGC internal coordination improve.

#### II.B — Shipping Route Interdiction

Maritime data collected by the Zonnon Naval Monitoring Station through May 2117 CE confirms that ONE Network Enterprises redirected 23% of non-essential shipping away from Bloc-adjacent routes between January and May 2117. The diversion pattern is consistent with deliberate economic isolation rather than commercially motivated rerouting: alternative routes selected by ONE vessels are in several documented instances operationally longer and costlier, indicating that route selection reflects strategic intent rather than logistical efficiency. The effect on Bloc inter-island commerce has been measurable, with Kashikari transit revenues reporting a confirmed 8–11% contraction for the period. Zonnon's own deep-water port traffic at Tavenport is down approximately 14% year-on-year through the same window. This Division regards ONE's routing decisions as the most immediately quantifiable CGC coercive instrument currently active against Bloc interests.

#### II.C — Internal Influence Operations and Astroturfing

This Division has identified seven suspected CGC-funded astroturfing operations within the Republic since 2114. The operations vary substantially in mechanism, target audience, and apparent funding pathway. Two operations focused on amplifying labor grievances within Zonnon Island's aquaculture sector, seeding disinformation about export revenue misallocation to erode public confidence in the current parliamentary administration. One operation targeted Zonnon's university networks, funding a series of academic events framed as independent policy forums but structured to promote dependency arguments favorable to CGC alignment. One operation involved the placement of synthetic editorial content in three regional media publications through shell-company advertising intermediaries, promoting narratives of Zonnon's economic isolation as self-inflicted. Three of the seven operations targeted the Accommodationist political faction directly, providing coordinated financial and logistical support designed to amplify Accommodationist calls for trade normalization with Haven and to give that faction disproportionate visibility in domestic political coverage. One operation remains under active investigation; its target and mechanism have not yet been fully characterized. The breadth of this campaign — spanning economic, academic, media, and direct partisan vectors simultaneously — indicates a degree of operational resourcing inconsistent with opportunistic interference. This Division assesses these operations as a coordinated program rather than discrete unrelated incidents.

---

### SECTION III — FORCE POSTURE CONSIDERATIONS

Zonnon's standing naval force comprises approximately 40,000 active personnel, constituting the largest uniformed military capacity within the Sharpsea Bloc. The force is structured primarily for coastal defense and counter-interdiction operations consistent with the Republic's defensive military doctrine, which treats CGC economic and political destabilization as the primary threat category. It is the assessment of this Division that the current force posture remains appropriate to that doctrine.

The officer corps of the Zonnon Maritime Defense Command skews Isolationist in political orientation, a fact that must be acknowledged in any honest assessment of how this Command's analytical conclusions are likely to be received by Accommodationist civilian government elements. This Division notes this structural reality without prejudice and recommends that assessments of this nature be distributed to civilian government counterparts with accompanying methodological documentation to permit independent review.

---

### SECTION IV — THREAT LEVEL ASSESSMENT

Having reviewed all identified coercion vectors in aggregate — tariff friction, confirmed shipping diversion, and a coordinated seven-operation internal influence campaign — this Division rates the overall CGC economic coercion threat level as **ELEVATED** on the Command's four-tier scale (GUARDED / MODERATE / ELEVATED / SEVERE). The current rating reflects active, measurable harm to Zonnon's external trade position and confirmed interference in domestic political processes, without yet crossing thresholds associated with direct economic blockade or military posturing.

---

### SECTION V — RECOMMENDATIONS

1. This Command recommends immediate coordination with Nyari Confederation signals intelligence services to cross-reference internally identified astroturfing patterns with any corresponding financial or communications data held by Nyari.
2. This Command recommends the Prime Minister's Security Council formally brief the Kashikari Hegemony Council on the ONE shipping diversion data, with a view to collective Bloc documentation of the pattern for potential external diplomatic use.
3. This Command recommends review of domestic counterintelligence resourcing in light of the identified seven-operation internal influence campaign, with particular attention to academic and media sector vulnerabilities.
4. This Command recommends that the civilian government treat any acceleration of Accommodationist policy proposals with heightened scrutiny pending further investigation of the three operations identified as targeting that faction.
