## Sector Transit Delays Frustrate Central Haven Commuters

*By Kiran Dasari | 77-03-12*

---

For the past two weeks, the daily commute between central Haven and downtown south Haven has become a test of patience for hundreds of thousands of residents. The transit line connecting the two districts has logged delays averaging **22 minutes per trip** — a figure that has transformed what should be a routine journey into a grinding daily ordeal.

"I've started waking up forty-five minutes earlier just to account for it," said Petra Solvay, 34, a logistics coordinator who makes the journey five days a week. "And I'm still late. My supervisor thinks I'm making excuses."

The line in question is one of Haven's busiest arterial routes. According to transit authority ridership data, it carries an estimated **340,000 daily passengers**, making the sustained disruption one of the more consequential infrastructure failures to hit Haven this quarter. Delays have been reported across multiple time windows, with morning and evening peak hours drawing the worst of it.

When contacted for comment, a spokesperson for the Phyrexis Group sector administration office offered a characteristically terse response.

"The disruptions are the result of scheduled infrastructure maintenance," the spokesperson said, declining to provide a timeline for completion or further detail on the nature of the work being carried out.

Commuters on the line have been less terse.

"Scheduled?" said Olek Mravna, 51, a warehouse shift supervisor who has used the line for over a decade. "If it's scheduled, someone forgot to schedule an end date. We've been standing on platforms for the better part of a month."

One recurring complaint among riders centers on a disconnect between the real world and what they see on the Edge. Several commuters noted that Edge-based transit scheduling displays show no delays whatsoever — trains listed as on time even as passengers wait on overcrowded platforms watching those same trains fail to appear.

"You check the Edge board and it tells you everything is running fine," said Imani Ferrel, 27, a data entry clerk commuting from central Haven. "Then you look up from your headspace and there's a crowd of two hundred people who've apparently all been imagining the same ghost train. At some point it stops being funny."

The gap between the Edge's reported scheduling and the actual lived experience of commuters is one that transit advocates have flagged before, though it has yet to prompt any formal CGC inquiry.

The Phyrexis Group sector administration office did not respond to follow-up questions regarding the Edge scheduling discrepancy.

The delays come at a time when commuter patience with sector-administered transit services is already strained. Several riders interviewed by the Herald described the maintenance explanation as implausible given the duration and consistency of the disruptions.

"If this were truly scheduled, you'd think they'd have mentioned it," Solvay said. "A notice. An update. Anything. Instead we just wait."

The Haven Herald has submitted a formal information request to the Phyrexis sector administration office. As of publication, no response beyond the initial statement has been received.
