Three priorities define our infrastructure posture for 77 PCE. Each is addressed below. I expect section leads to return written position statements within ten days.

**I. Black Box Security Hardening**

Following the SLF-attributed surveillance incidents in late 76 PCE, I have directed Facility Security to conduct a full perimeter audit of the Black Box. The audit identified three external approach vectors requiring physical reinforcement and two anomalous electromagnetic signatures on the building's eastern face consistent with passive monitoring equipment. Both signatures have been neutralized. Facility Security is now implementing a revised protocol that mandates weekly active sweeps of all below-grade corridors and a restructured access credentialing cycle for all personnel with L1 clearance or above.

This is not a precautionary exercise. The SLF has demonstrated that it has accurate knowledge of our operational footprint in south Haven. Treat all external contact zones as surveilled until the full hardening program is certified complete. Target completion: 77-03-15.

---

**II. CYPHER Compute Allocation Negotiations**

TEC currently leases approximately 11% of CYPHER's total compute capacity for Edge processing. This corresponds to 37 designated E-cluster satellite nodes — our allocated portion of the operational constellation — supporting Tier 3 services: avatar persistence, identity management, and cross-user synchronization. These services constitute the backbone of Edge's immersive layer; degradation in E-cluster throughput directly maps to user-perceptible quality loss.

I want to be unambiguous about the scope of our current arrangement: beyond the designated 37-node E-cluster, TEC does not lease significant additional non-E-cluster CYPHER compute for Edge operations. What we have is what we have. There is no informal buffer, no secondary allocation, no arrangement that has not been put in front of this leadership team.

The constraint on growth is structural. CYPHER's satellite constellation exceeds 340 operational nodes in total. Of those, 37 are ours. Allocation of CYPHER satellite resources is governed collectively by the CGC, and any expansion of TEC's E-cluster designation requires CGC approval. Given our current position within the CGC, pursuing that approval in 77 PCE is not straightforward. Nonetheless, I am directing our CGC liaison team to open preliminary allocation discussions in Q1. The request will be framed around demonstrated service demand — provide them the Q4 76 PCE usage data, scrubbed and formatted for the Secretariat's intake template, by 77-01-20.

---

**III. Edge Nanobot Distribution Density Improvements**

Current Haven-wide active nanobot coverage sits at 94.3% of the estimated Fullwalker-eligible population. The remaining 5.7% falls into three documented categories: Opted individuals who have successfully expelled nanobot integration (approximately 3.1%), individuals residing in structurally anomalous zones with suppressed signal propagation (approximately 1.8%), and unresolved technical outliers requiring individual diagnosis (approximately 0.8%).

The 1.8% propagation-gap population is the actionable target for 77 PCE. Engineering has identified eleven specific micro-zones in North Haven's industrial fringe and two in Central Haven's sub-basement residential corridors where structural density creates a persistent coverage shadow. Remediation requires physical dispersal supplement nodes — seventeen total. Procurement authorization is approved. Installation is to be sequenced around the BCO enforcement operations currently scheduled for West and North Haven to avoid conflicting operational footprints.
