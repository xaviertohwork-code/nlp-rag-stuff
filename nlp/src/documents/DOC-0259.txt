# INTERNAL MEMORANDUM
**Classification:** L3 — RESTRICTED DISTRIBUTION
**Entity:** Edge Research (Independent Legal Entity, Effective 41-06-03 PCE)
**To:** All Staff
**From:** Sim Jiahong, Chief Executive Officer
**Date:** 41-06-03 PCE
**Subject:** Post-Separation Operational Directive — Effective Immediately

---

As of today, Edge Research stands as a formally constituted independent legal entity. That is not a formality. That is a fact, and I want every person on this team to feel the weight of it before reading another line of this memo.

We were built inside the machinery of Phyrexis Group and Cyanite Industries. Their resources, their infrastructure, their decisions about what we were allowed to become. That era is over. What begins today is ours to define.

Let me be plain about what changes, and what does not.

---

**What Changes**

Our corporate structure. Edge Research now operates under its own charter, its own legal identity, and its own executive authority. I am that authority. Decisions about product direction, personnel, and competitive posture no longer require referral upstream. We move when I say we move.

Our address. We have relocated to our new accommodations courtesy of our friends across the corridor — space on land in south Haven granted to us by Phyrexis Group, and I expect it to be treated as a permanent home, not a temporary arrangement. Build here. Think here. This is where Edge is made.

Our mandate. We are no longer a subsidiary skunkworks. We are a company. Behave accordingly.

---

**What Does Not Change**

Our financial arrangements remain intact. All financial reporting continues to run directly to the Phyrexis Group Managing Partners through the established channels. This is not up for internal discussion, and it is not a subject for external communication. The terms are the terms. We operate within them, and we perform well enough that no one has cause to revisit them. That is all any of us need to understand on this point.

---

**Strategic Priorities**

I will not waste your time with a list of fifteen objectives.

There is one primary target: Renhwa Media.

They are the dominant entertainment and information platform in Haven. They have used that position to shape narrative, suppress competitors, and make themselves indispensable to the megacorporations that tolerate them. They are also slow, complacent, and structurally unable to imagine what we are building.

We are going to take their market. Not gradually. Not politely. We will eat into their margins, we will capture their audience, and we will make the question of Renhwa's relevance one that people in this city stop even bothering to ask.

Everything we build from this point forward is built with that competition in mind. Every product decision, every user engagement metric, every partnership conversation. If it does not advance our position against Renhwa, deprioritize it.

---

**Interim Reporting Structure**

Until further notice:

- All technical leads report directly to me on a weekly cycle.
- All external communications — press, partnership, regulatory — are cleared through my office before release. No exceptions.
- Financial documentation for the Managing Partners is to be submitted on the existing timetable. Accuracy is not optional. Neither is punctuality.

---

We are small. We are new, in the formal sense. We are not, in any other sense, starting from scratch.

We have a technology that no one else has. We have the will to deploy it. We have a competitor that has grown fat on the assumption that nothing like us was coming.

That assumption ends today.

Get to work.

— **Sim Jiahong**
Chief Executive Officer, Edge Research
