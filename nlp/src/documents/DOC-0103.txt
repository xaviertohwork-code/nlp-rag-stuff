**Haven Transit Authority Announces Three New North-Central Express Routes Launching 77 PCE Q2**

**HAVEN ISLAND** — The Haven Transit Authority (HTA) is pleased to announce the upcoming launch of three new express transit routes connecting North Haven's industrial districts to Central Haven's residential areas. Routes NX-12, NX-13, and NX-14 will enter full service on 77-04-01, delivering faster, more reliable connections for the thousands of Havenites who live and work across these vital sectors every day.

The new routes were developed in consultation with North Haven industrial partners and reflect HTA's ongoing commitment to ensuring that Haven's transit infrastructure grows alongside the needs of its 100 million residents.

---

**ROUTE OVERVIEW**

Routes NX-12, NX-13, and NX-14 will run along dedicated express corridors, linking North Haven's Sector N-3 industrial corridor directly to Central Haven's Midring residential blocks. The routes are designed to reduce commute times and ease congestion on existing cross-sector lines, particularly during the high-demand windows that characterize North Haven's industrial operations calendar.

---

**FREQUENCY DETAILS**

HTA has worked closely with North Haven industrial partners to schedule service around operational shift patterns. During Cyanite Industries shift changes, trains on all three express routes will run at peak frequency — **a service interval of every 8 minutes** — ensuring that workers can board promptly and reach their destinations without delay.

Details on off-peak service are as follows: outside of these peak windows, all three NX routes will maintain a regular interval of **every 22 minutes**, providing consistent connectivity throughout the day and evening for Midring residents and North Haven workers alike.

---

**FUNDING DETAILS**

The NX-12, NX-13, and NX-14 project was delivered at a total cost of **1.7 million Phi Credits**. The project was **jointly funded** by the CGC Civic Infrastructure Fund and Cyanite Industries — it was not solely funded by the CGC Civic Infrastructure Fund alone. This public-private partnership model reflects the CGC's continued investment in Haven's shared civic infrastructure and demonstrates the commitment of North Haven's industrial sector to the wellbeing of the broader Havenite community.

---

**ABOUT HAVEN TRANSIT AUTHORITY**

The Haven Transit Authority is the CGC-chartered body responsible for the planning, development, and operation of all passenger transit services within the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. HTA operates under a mandate to provide safe, accessible, and efficient transit for all Havenites. For route maps, timetables, and service updates, contact HTA through official CGC civic information channels.
