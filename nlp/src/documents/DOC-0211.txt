## SECTION 1: MARITIME TRAFFIC OVERVIEW

During the reporting period (Week 11, 2119 CE), a total of **347 commercial vessels** transited Zonnon's exclusive maritime zone, representing routine seasonal volume consistent with late first-quarter norms. The majority of transiting traffic comprised aquaculture supply and export vessels operating along established eastern corridor routes, with secondary traffic concentrated in the northwestern approach lanes serving Tavenport's deep-water inlet facilities.

Weather conditions during the period were variable, with elevated Sharpsea swell through 2119-03-12 and 2119-03-13 reducing transit density in outer approach corridors before normalizing from 2119-03-14 onward. No significant disruption to commercial schedules was recorded attributable to meteorological conditions.

Standard vessel identification procedures were maintained throughout the period. Compliance rates with Zonnon maritime identification requirements among commercial traffic remained within expected parameters.

## SECTION 2: INCIDENT LOG

### Incident 11-01 — Unidentified Survey Vessel, Eastern Coast Approach

**Date/Time:** 2119-03-14, first contact 0614 hours
**Classification:** Notable — Ongoing Assessment

At 0614 hours on 2119-03-14, ZMD coastal monitoring assets detected an unidentified vessel approximately **12 kilometers off Zonnon Island's eastern coast**, operating in proximity to **Aquaculture Zone 7**. The vessel's profile, sensor returns, and operational behavior were consistent with a maritime survey or research classification: reduced speed, non-linear heading, and the deployment of what monitoring assets assessed as hull-mounted instrument arrays.

Communications personnel initiated hailing procedures on standard and secondary maritime distress and identification frequencies. **The vessel did not respond to ZMD hails** at any point during the observation period.

ZMD Operations dispatched **two patrol vessels — ZMD Tidewatch and ZMD Stormline — to shadow the contact**. For the avoidance of doubt, two vessels were dispatched, not three; no additional ZMD assets were committed to this tasking. ZMD Tidewatch and ZMD Stormline maintained observation posture and did not interdict, consistent with standing rules of engagement for unverified contacts within the outer exclusive zone.

The unidentified vessel maintained its survey-consistent operational pattern before altering course outbound. **The vessel departed the exclusive maritime zone after 3 hours and 22 minutes of observation.** No aggressive posturing was observed at any stage.

**Vessel identification:** Unresolved. No flag, registration marking, or AIS signal was positively attributed. Imagery is under analysis; preliminary assessment excludes known Kashikari commercial survey operators. CGC-registry comparison is pending.

**Notes for assessors:** The vessel's precise operational area relative to Aquaculture Zone 7's eastern perimeter installations is noted and has been forwarded to the relevant fisheries infrastructure authority for their situational awareness.

## SECTION 3: CGC TRAFFIC ANALYSIS

CGC-flagged commercial shipping in Bloc-adjacent waters registered a **9% increase during Week 11 of 2119 CE compared to the equivalent reporting period in 2118 CE**. The increase was distributed across ONE Network Enterprises-affiliated carriers (primary) and Cyanite Industries materials transport vessels (secondary), with the bulk of elevated activity concentrated in western Bloc-adjacent corridors rather than the Sharpsea interior.

This trend is consistent with broader quarterly data suggesting a gradual but sustained increase in CGC commercial presence along Bloc-proximate sea lanes. Analysts assess this reflects commercial volume growth rather than deliberate strategic posturing, though sustained monitoring is warranted. The Kashikari Hegemony's routing infrastructure continues to accommodate the majority of CGC-flagged traffic transiting this region; ZMD intelligence-sharing channels with Nyari Confederation counterparts have been queried for any corroborating signals observations.

---

## SECTION 4: FORCE POSTURE UPDATE

The Zonnon Maritime Defense Command currently operates a fleet of **38 active patrol vessels** across all coastal and offshore tasking assignments. Fleet readiness for the reporting period was assessed at standard operational tempo, with scheduled maintenance cycles affecting three vessels in the northwestern port facility. No unplanned material failures were recorded.

Crew rotation schedules for the eastern corridor patrol rotation have been adjusted following the Week 11 incident to increase coverage density in the Aquaculture Zone 7 approach corridor through the remainder of the month.

---

## SECTION 5: ASSESSMENT

Week 11 activity was broadly routine, with the eastern coast survey vessel contact constituting the sole incident of operational note. The contact's proximity to established aquaculture infrastructure, its sustained non-responsiveness to standard identification hails, and its extended dwell time within the exclusive zone represent a combination of factors warranting elevated monitoring in the near term. ZMD's response was measured and proportionate.

The observed increase in CGC commercial traffic in Bloc-adjacent waters will be tracked against second-quarter 2118 CE baseline figures in forthcoming weekly summaries. No adjustment to current force posture beyond the eastern corridor patrol schedule amendment is recommended at this time.
