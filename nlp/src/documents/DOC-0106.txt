# South Haven Waterfront League: 76 PCE Season Recap and Championship Results

**Haven Municipal Bulletin | Sports & Community | 76-12-04**

*By Our South Haven Sports Correspondent*

---

## Season Overview

What a ride it has been. The South Haven Waterfront League wrapped up its **9th completed season** in 76 PCE with some of the most electric racing and diving the dockside crowds have witnessed since the league's founding, and if the energy in the stands was any indication, the sport is only growing stronger.

This season saw all **12 amateur teams** — fielding a combined roster of approximately **480 registered athletes** — compete across three months of small-craft sprints, open-water relays, and the always-spectacular deep-dive events that have become the league's signature draw. **Total spectator attendance across the season reached approximately 215,000**, a modest uptick from 75 PCE that league administrators say signals consistent, healthy growth for amateur maritime sport in Haven.

"The waterfront has always been ours," said one volunteer race marshal before the championship weekend. "The league just gave it a name."

---

## Championship Results

**The Dockside Currents are your 76 PCE South Haven Waterfront League Champions.**

In a championship final that had spectators on the edge of every available railing, the Dockside Currents defeated the Floodwall Runners **47 points to 39**, claiming their first title in four seasons of trying. The Runners, who had dominated the regular-season standings with an almost mechanical consistency, found themselves undone by the Currents' tactically aggressive small-craft sprint strategy in the morning sessions, which opened a gap that the afternoon deep-dive relay could not fully close.

Standout performer of the championship weekend was Currents sprint captain **Yeva Molnar**, whose back-to-back sprint finals wins in the quarter and semi earned her the judges' commendation for individual excellence — the first time a sprint specialist has taken that distinction since 73 PCE. For the Floodwall Runners, deep-dive relay anchor **Osei Tamboli** put in a performance that drew audible appreciation from a crowd that was, by that point, largely rooting against him. Tamboli's final relay leg, a forty-meter descent-and-recovery, is already making the rounds on word-of-mouth as one of the dives of the season.

The Dockside Currents will receive the league's traditional brass-and-driftwood trophy at the annual banquet, scheduled for 76-12-20.

---

## Sponsorship Details

The league's smooth operation this season owed no small debt to its title sponsor, the **South Haven Merchants' Association**, which contributed **85,000 Phi Credits** to the 76 PCE season. Those funds supported event marshaling, safety vessel deployment, and the new floating marker system used in this year's deep-dive relay finals. League treasurer **Pata Neves** was emphatic in her acknowledgment: "The Merchants' Association has been a genuine partner in building this league's infrastructure, and the quality of this season reflects that investment directly." To be clear, the title sponsorship was held by the South Haven Merchants' Association — not, as has occasionally been misreported in informal channels, the South Haven Fishing Cooperative, which participates in the league in an entirely separate capacity as a team patron.

---

## Expansion Announcement

The league saved perhaps its biggest news for the closing ceremony: beginning in **77 PCE**, the South Haven Waterfront League will formally expand to include a **North Haven division**, welcoming **four new teams** to the competition structure. The North Haven expansion has been discussed informally for several seasons, and league administrators confirmed that venue agreements and team registration frameworks are already in place.

The structural details of how the North and South divisions will interact — whether through a unified points table, divisional playoffs, or a combined championship format — are still being finalized, and the league has promised a full announcement ahead of the 77 PCE season opener.

For now, though, that can wait. Tonight belongs to the Dockside Currents.
