# INCIDENT REPORT IR-77-0087
## Nanobot Containment Breach — West Haven Port Terminal 6

**Issuing Authority:** TEC Environmental Compliance Unit
**Classification:** L4 — RESTRICTED INTERNAL
**Report Date:** 77-02-19
**Incident Date:** 77-02-14
**Incident Reference:** IR-77-0087

---

## 1. INCIDENT SUMMARY

At 03:17 local time on 77-02-14, an electromagnetic filtration barrier at West Haven Port Terminal 6 lost power during a scheduled ONE Network Enterprises cargo loading operation. The barrier failure permitted an estimated 1.2 billion nanobots to escape Haven aboard three outbound cargo vessels before barrier function was restored at 05:42. This report documents the timeline of the incident, its root cause, assessed impact, classification rationale, stakeholder responses received, and corrective actions proposed.

Terminal 6 is a significant node in ONE Network Enterprises' outbound logistics chain, handling approximately 18% of all ONE outbound cargo from West Haven. The incident therefore affected a material volume of Haven-departing freight during the window of barrier inoperability.

---

## 2. TIMELINE OF EVENTS

| Time (Local) | Event |
|---|---|
| 02:45 | Primary power supply for Terminal 6 filtration array taken offline for scheduled maintenance by TEC Infrastructure Services. |
| 02:47 | Backup power supply unit assessed as active by on-duty technician. Secondary activation protocol logged as complete. |
| 03:17 | Capacitor failure in backup power supply unit causes total loss of barrier function. Filtration barrier enters unpowered state. Alarm triggered at TEC Environmental Compliance monitoring station. |
| 03:22 | On-call Environmental Compliance officer notified. Cargo loading operations at Terminal 6 not immediately suspended pending confirmation of alarm source. |
| 03:51 | TEC Infrastructure Services confirms barrier inoperability. ONE cargo loading operations notified and suspended. |
| 05:42 | Electromagnetic filtration barrier restored to full operational function following emergency capacitor replacement. |

The barrier remained inoperative for approximately 2 hours and 25 minutes from initial failure. During the window between 03:17 and 05:42, an estimated 1.2 billion nanobots were carried beyond the Haven filtration perimeter aboard three outbound ONE Network Enterprises cargo vessels.

---

## 3. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

Investigation by TEC Environmental Compliance Unit has identified the following primary causal chain:

**Primary Cause:** The Terminal 6 filtration array's primary power supply was taken offline for scheduled maintenance at 02:45. In accordance with standard protocol, this action required confirmation that the backup power supply unit was fully active and load-bearing before primary supply disconnection. On-duty technician logs indicate the secondary activation protocol was recorded as complete; however, post-incident inspection reveals that a capacitor within the backup power supply unit had developed a pre-failure stress fracture not detectable under standard pre-maintenance inspection procedures. When the backup unit assumed load at 02:47, the fractured capacitor sustained function for approximately 30 minutes before failing catastrophically at 03:17, causing total barrier power loss.

**Contributing Factor:** The 34-minute delay between initial alarm activation (03:17) and suspension of ONE cargo loading operations (03:51) allowed continued cargo transit through the inoperative barrier zone, increasing total nanobot egress volume during the incident window.

**No evidence** was found of deliberate interference, system tampering, or systemic failure extending beyond the identified capacitor fault.

---

## 4. IMPACT ASSESSMENT

An estimated 1.2 billion nanobots are assessed to have exited Haven perimeter containment aboard three ONE Network Enterprises outbound cargo vessels during the period of barrier inoperability. Distribution across the three vessels is estimated but not confirmed; cargo manifests for all three vessels have been logged for reference.

Nanobots operating beyond Haven's relay coverage infrastructure are assessed to enter full dormancy within 14 hours of perimeter departure, at which point they present no active operational capability. TEC technical assessments confirm that dormant nanobots in open maritime environments do not exhibit behavioral propagation, data relay function, or autonomous activity.

The 18% share of ONE outbound cargo ordinarily processed through Terminal 6 indicates that nanobot carriage beyond Haven perimeter on outbound vessels is a pre-existing baseline phenomenon at low levels under normal operating conditions. The Terminal 6 incident produced egress significantly in excess of normal leakage levels during the 03:17–05:42 window.

---

## 5. CLASSIFICATION

**Severity Level: 3 — Significant but Contained.**

This classification was issued by TEC Environmental Compliance, the responsible regulatory authority for nanobot perimeter integrity at Haven port facilities. No other regulatory body holds authority to issue or modify this classification. The Severity Level 3 designation reflects the following assessment: the egress volume substantially exceeded normal operational baseline; however, dormancy onset within 14 hours of relay coverage loss materially limits the potential for ongoing impact. No evidence of reception, recovery, or intentional diversion of escaped nanobot populations has been identified. The incident is therefore assessed as significant in scope but bounded in consequence.

---

## 6. STAKEHOLDER RESPONSE

**ONE Network Enterprises** filed a formal objection to this incident characterization on 77-02-17, asserting that TEC's filtration maintenance scheduling practices were negligent and that the delay in suspending cargo loading operations following alarm activation compounded preventable egress volume. ONE's objection is noted in this report. TEC Environmental Compliance acknowledges that the 34-minute loading continuation window is a procedural point warranting review; however, the characterization of maintenance scheduling as negligent is not accepted. The capacitor failure was not detectable under applicable pre-maintenance inspection standards.

ONE's objection is filed with this report as Annex A and will be transmitted to TEC's Legal Compliance division for formal response.

---

## 7. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

The following corrective measures are proposed for implementation within 45 days of this report's issuance:

1. **Backup Power Redundancy:** All filtration array backup power supply units at Haven port terminals to undergo enhanced capacitor stress-fracture inspection, extending current pre-maintenance inspection procedures to include non-destructive material fatigue assessment. TEC Infrastructure Services to propose revised inspection protocol within 15 days.

2. **Secondary Redundancy Layer:** Engineering review to assess feasibility of installing a tertiary backup power unit at all Severity-Zone filtration arrays, such that a single backup unit failure does not produce total barrier inoperability.

3. **Cargo Loading Suspension Protocol:** Standard operating procedure to be revised to require immediate suspension of all cargo loading operations at affected terminals upon barrier alarm activation, pending physical confirmation of barrier status, rather than pending confirmation of alarm source. Revised protocol to be circulated to all port terminal operations liaisons, including ONE Network Enterprises Terminal 6 operations management.

4. **Capacitor Replacement Cycle:** Scheduled replacement cycle for filtration array backup supply capacitors to be reviewed; current cycle intervals to be assessed against observed failure characteristics.
