## 1. Budget Overview

This report presents the consolidated fiscal summary for the CGC operational budget for Fiscal Year 76 PCE (01-01-76 through 12-31-76). All figures are denominated in Phi Credits. Figures contained herein cover CGC directorate operations only. Capital expenditure accounts, including infrastructure contracts such as the Cyanite Industries Floodwall maintenance contract, are reported separately in the CGC Capital Accounts Annual Statement and are not consolidated here.

| Metric | Amount (Phi Credits) |
|---|---|
| Total Assessed Operational Budget, FY 76 PCE | 14,200,000,000 |
| Total Actual Collected Contributions | 13,700,000,000 |
| Net Collection Variance | (500,000,000) |

The total assessed CGC operational budget for FY 76 PCE was **14.2 billion Phi Credits**, covering CGC directorate operations and excluding direct infrastructure contracts paid separately through the CGC capital accounts. Actual collected contributions toward the operational budget totaled **13.7 billion Phi Credits**. The nature and origin of the resulting shortfall of 500 million Phi Credits is addressed in Section 4, Collection Variance Notes.

## 4. Collection Variance Notes

### Note A — Genesis Labs Disputed Assessment

**Genesis Labs** remitted 2.12 billion Phi Credits against a total assessed contribution of 2.62 billion Phi Credits for FY 76 PCE, resulting in an uncollected balance of **500 million Phi Credits**. Genesis Labs formally filed a Contribution Dispute Notice pursuant to CGC Procedural Rule 22(f) on 76-09-14, citing methodology disagreements over subsidiary revenue classification — specifically, the appropriate treatment of revenue generated by Genesis Labs' Opting rehabilitation clinic network in the revenue base used for proportionate assessment calculation. Genesis Labs' position holds that this revenue stream does not constitute core megacorporation operating revenue for CGC assessment purposes. The Office of the Comptroller has referred the dispute to the CGC Office of Member Relations & Legal Affairs for adjudication in accordance with standard dispute resolution procedures. Pending resolution, the uncollected 500 million Phi Credits is recorded as a receivable. **No other member corporation filed a contribution dispute for FY 76 PCE.** Cyanite Industries, ONE Network Enterprises, Phyrexis Group, and The Edge Corporation each settled their assessed contributions in full and without contest.

---

## 5. Forward Notes

Resolution of the Genesis Labs contribution dispute is anticipated within the first half of 77 PCE. Should the adjudication find in favor of the CGC's assessed methodology, the 500 million Phi Credit receivable will be collected pursuant to the standard late-payment schedule established in CGC Procedural Rule 22(g). The Reserve and Contingency Fund carries forward a substantial uncommitted balance, providing adequate liquidity buffer against the disputed receivable for the duration of the adjudication period. The Office of the Comptroller does not recommend any adjustment to the FY 77 PCE assessment methodology pending the outcome of the dispute proceeding.
