## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY AND PRIORITY OBJECTIVES

This memorandum establishes Genesis Labs' strategic posture for Clairos Governance Council sessions throughout 77 PCE. The following objectives are listed in descending priority order.

**Priority 1 — Defense of Somatic Augmentation Regulatory Autonomy.**
Genesis Labs' top CGC priority for the 77 PCE sessions is blocking any resolution that would subject Somatic Augmentation to CGC regulatory oversight. Helix Tower leadership has made clear that this line is non-negotiable. Any regulatory framework administered through the CGC collective would expose our clinical standards, retroviral licensing terms, and growth-enhancer protocols to scrutiny by parties with direct commercial interests in undermining them. We will deploy the full weight of our CGC representation capacity against any motion — whether introduced directly or through procedural amendment — that advances this outcome. Subsidiary objectives include opposing any BCO scope expansion that might serve as a precursor mechanism toward Somatic oversight.

**Priority 2 — TEC Surveillance Capability Containment.**
Cary Mullin has issued a standing directive that Genesis Labs is to vote against any resolution expanding TEC's surveillance capabilities within the CGC framework. This position is both principled and strategic: the Opting rehabilitation clinic network operated by Genesis Labs represents a direct point of vulnerability if TEC's nanobot infrastructure gains any CGC-sanctioned data-sharing authority. Our vote on all relevant resolutions should be cast consistently and without qualification.

**Priority 3 — Budget Contribution Dispute Resolution.**
Genesis Labs continues to dispute the 500 million Phi Credits assessed contribution from the 76 PCE budget cycle. We have not agreed to pay this sum, we have not settled this assessment, and we do not intend to do so under current terms. Our position remains that the underlying methodology misclassified subsidiary revenue streams in a manner that artificially inflated our assessed share. Separately, our auditors have flagged 47 billion Phi Credits in Genesis Labs subsidiary revenue as potentially misclassified in the CGC's consolidated accounting framework — a figure whose implications for our assessed contributions across multiple budget cycles we are still evaluating. Legal Affairs is coordinating with CGC Affairs on the dispute posture; no concessions are to be communicated to CGC Secretariat without Director-level authorization.

## II. BUDGET AUTHORIZATION — 77 PCE

Genesis Labs' government relations budget for 77 PCE is authorized at **280 million Phi Credits**. This allocation covers direct CGC representation costs, external advisory retainers, regulatory monitoring, and stakeholder engagement across all CGC-adjacent bodies. Given the elevated priority environment described in Section I, any reallocation between line items above 5 million Phi Credits requires approval from this office. No budget surplus from the disputed 76 PCE contribution assessment is to be anticipated or forecasted pending resolution.

## III. ALLIANCE ASSESSMENT — TEC

The growing nanobot footprint of The Edge Corporation presents an evolving dynamic within the CGC. TEC's expanding nanobot presence creates a structural basis for alliance-of-convenience with Genesis Labs on privacy-related votes — specifically, resolutions that would authorize broader data collection, inter-corporate nanobot data sharing, or CGC-sanctioned behavioral surveillance infrastructure. 

---

## IV. RISK WARNINGS

**Old 3 Retaliation Risk.**
The memo's most significant near-term political risk is the following: any appearance of coordination with TEC that might provoke unified Old 3 retaliation must be avoided. Phyrexis, Cyanite, and ONE have demonstrated consistent willingness to act collectively against entities they perceive as aligned challengers. If our independent votes on surveillance-adjacent resolutions are characterized publicly as a Genesis-TEC axis, the Old 3's backroom coordination mechanisms will activate against both of us simultaneously. The value of any individual vote alignment does not approach the cost of that scenario.

All public-facing CGC communications should emphasize the independent basis for Genesis Labs' positions. No joint statements with TEC. No shared procedural motions. No pre-vote signaling through back channels that can be documented.

**Somatic Oversight — Incremental Encroachment.**
Monitor for resolutions framed around "consumer safety" or "bioregulatory harmonization" that do not name Somatic Augmentation directly but create enforcement frameworks applicable to it. These are more dangerous than direct proposals.
