# CYANITE INDUSTRIES
## CLASSIFICATION: YELLOW
### Director-General's Quarterly Address — Q1 77 PCE
**Distributed to: All Division Heads, Senior Operations Staff**
**From: Takeshi Oyelaran, Director-General**
**Date: 77-04-02**

---

*The following is the transcript of the Director-General's recorded quarterly address, distributed via secure internal channel to division heads across all Cyanite facilities. Acknowledge receipt through standard YELLOW-channel confirmation.*

---

Division heads,

Q1 is behind us. Before you go back to doing what Cyanite does — which is building things, moving things, and keeping the lights on for everyone else in this city — I want to take a few minutes to walk through where we stand.

I'll be direct, as I always am. Overall: this was a solid quarter. Not a perfect one, but a solid one. I'll take that.

---

**SARENTO OPERATIONS AND RAW MATERIALS**

The Sarento facilities processed **9.2 million metric tonnes of raw materials** through Q1 77 PCE. That number should mean something to everyone in this room — or listening to this recording. That is the volume of extraction, refinement, and processing that makes everything else downstream possible. Not just for Cyanite. For Haven. For Clairos.

The facility directors at Sarento deserve to hear that acknowledged. They ran a tight operation through weather delays in the first six weeks of the quarter and still hit the throughput numbers. That is what Cyanite people do.

Shipyard output from Sarento held to schedule. Under our standing supply agreements with ONE Network Enterprises, we delivered **three patrol vessels** in Q1. On time, to specification. That relationship works because we hold to our commitments. We expect ONE to do the same with theirs.

---

**FLOODWALL MAINTENANCE**

I want to address the Floodwall maintenance budget directly, because I know some of you have seen the figure and I want to frame it the right way.

Floodwall maintenance consumed **34% of Cyanite's Q1 operational budget**. Thirty-four percent. I know what some of you are thinking. That is a large share of our operational resources committed to infrastructure that does not generate revenue.

Here is how I want you to think about it instead: that 34% is the reason Haven is still above sea level. That is not a figure I apologize for. That is a figure I point to with pride. We are the company that keeps Clairos above water — literally. No other megacorporation in the CGC can say that. No other organization on this planet can say that. That cost is not a burden. That cost is what separates us from everyone else.

The pumping systems and retaining wall inspections on the northern shore came in on budget. Reactor maintenance cycles were completed within the planned downtime window. Good work by the Infrastructure Division.

---

**MUNITIONS AND HEAVY PRODUCTION**

Q1 munitions production **exceeded targets by 8%**. I will not belabor the point — that is the number, it is good, and I expect the relevant teams to be recognized for it at the divisional level.

Armoured vehicle output was on target. Automated systems production remains steady. The war robots coming off the Sarento line are meeting all procurement specifications.

We stay ahead of our production benchmarks because Cyanite's strength has always been simple: if you destroy one of our machines, we replace it with two. That is not a boast. That is the operational reality we build toward every quarter.

---

**MINING OPERATIONS — A NOTE**

On the mining side: there are **ongoing labor efficiency concerns at two outposts** which I am aware of and which are being addressed through the appropriate divisional channels. I will not name the outposts in this communication, and I am not inviting further discussion of the specifics here. Division heads with direct oversight responsibilities know what they need to know. If you are uncertain whether that includes you, it does not.

This is not a material operational risk at this time. It will be managed.

---

**CLOSING**

Cyanite's job is to keep Clairos functional. Not to be flashy about it. Not to hold press events or launch spectacles. We mine the ground, we build the walls, we ship the materials, and we keep the water out.

That is what we did in Q1. That is what we will do in Q2.

Back to work.

— **Takeshi Oyelaran**
*Director-General, Cyanite Industries*
*77-04-02*
