## 1. Scope

This bulletin establishes revised mandatory standards for the storage, handling, viability testing, and disposal of Genesis Labs somatic retroviral vectors and associated biological growth enhancers distributed through all certified Genesis Labs Somatic Clinics. Revision 4 supersedes all previous editions of GL-TB-2119-003 and amends applicable provisions of the Genesis Labs Somatic Product Handling Compendium (GL-SPHC-2117). Compliance with this bulletin is mandatory for all clinic and logistics personnel handling classified somatic product lines. Non-compliance constitutes a deviation event under Genesis Labs Quality Governance Framework GL-QGF-009 and must be reported accordingly.

---

## 2. Applicable Products

This bulletin applies to the following product categories:

**2.1 Standard Somatic Retroviral Vectors (Category SRV-S)**
Mass-manufactured retroviral vectors formulated for general population somatic augmentation. Distributed in sealed cryogenic unit packaging under GL-SPHC Appendix C cold-chain specifications.

**2.2 Second-Generation Biological Growth Enhancers (Category BGA-2)**
Second-generation activator compound series (development year 84 PCE), supplied as adjunct therapy to SRV-S vectors. Distributed in hermetically sealed secondary packaging under GL-SPHC Appendix D conditions.

**2.3 Personalized Retroviral Vectors (Category SRV-P)**
Genome-specific retroviral constructs produced at certified Somatic Clinics using patient-submitted genomic data. Subject to supplementary handling provisions specified in Section 7 of this bulletin.

*Note on Discontinued Products:* First-generation biological growth enhancers (internal designation BGA-1; historically referenced under the informal street designation "Gleam") are no longer manufactured or distributed by Genesis Labs. Their historical shelf stability parameters are retained in this bulletin for comparative reference only (see Section 4.2).

---

## 3. Storage Requirements

**3.1 Standard Temperature Range**
All Category SRV-S and BGA-2 products must be stored continuously at **2–8°C** from point of production through point of clinical dispensing. Cold-chain integrity must be verified at each logistics handoff using Genesis Labs Cold-Chain Verification Form GL-CCVF-12.

**3.2 Mandatory Minimum Viability Standard**
Standard somatic retroviral vectors (Category SRV-S) must maintain a **minimum 92% viability rate** following **120 days of storage at 2–8°C**. Lots failing to meet this threshold at any scheduled testing interval must be quarantined and reported immediately to the Somatic Product Integrity Division pending disposition review. This standard is established as an absolute floor; lots consistently performing below 95% viability at the 90-day interval should be flagged for enhanced monitoring per GL-QGF-009 Section 3.4, even where they remain above the 92% minimum.

**3.3 Temperature Exceedance — Reclassification Threshold**
Vectors or enhancers stored **above 28°C for more than 72 continuous hours** must be reclassified as non-viable and disposed of in full accordance with **Genesis Labs Biohazard Protocol BH-7**. For the avoidance of any doubt: the mandatory reclassification and disposal threshold is **72 continuous hours**, not 48 continuous hours or any shorter interval. Exceedances of 24–72 hours above 28°C require enhanced viability testing per Section 6.2 before lot release may proceed. Exceedances of fewer than 24 hours above 28°C must be logged and flagged on the accompanying Cold-Chain Verification Form but do not independently require reclassification absent confirmed viability failure.

---

## 4. Viability Standards and Shelf Life

**4.1 Category SRV-S — Standard Retroviral Vectors**
As specified in Section 3.2, Category SRV-S lots must maintain a minimum 92% viability rate at 120 days under recommended 2–8°C storage. Lot-level viability is assessed by fluorescence-based transduction efficiency assay (GL-ASSAY-VE-4) at intervals of 30, 60, 90, and 120 days post-production. Any lot falling below the 92% threshold at any scheduled interval is ineligible for clinical dispensing.

**4.2 Category BGA-2 — Second-Generation Biological Growth Enhancers**
Category BGA-2 compounds carry a **shelf life of 18 months** under recommended storage conditions (2–8°C, humidity ≤ 35%, protected from direct light exposure per GL-SPHC Appendix D). Lot expiration dates are printed on primary and secondary packaging; no dispensing is permitted beyond the printed expiry date regardless of apparent product integrity.

*Comparative Reference — Discontinued BGA-1:* First-generation enhancers (BGA-1 / "Gleam") carried a shelf life of **45 days** under their former storage specifications, a substantially shorter viable window that contributed to cold-chain compliance difficulties in early distribution cycles. This parameter is recorded here for historical reference only. BGA-1 is not subject to the provisions of this bulletin.

**4.3 Viability Decay Profile — SRV-S**
Acceptable viability decay curves for Category SRV-S are tabulated in **GL-SPHC Appendix C, Table C-3**. Lots exhibiting decay rates exceeding 1.5 percentage points per 30-day interval below the projected curve midpoint must be escalated to the Somatic Product Integrity Division for root-cause analysis, irrespective of whether the absolute 92% threshold has been breached.

## 5. Quality Control Protocols

**5.1 Batch Sampling Requirement**
Quality control batch testing is mandatory for all production lots of Category SRV-S and BGA-2 products. Testing must sample **no fewer than 5% of units per production lot**, drawn via stratified random selection per GL-QC-SAMPLING-006. Sampling below this threshold constitutes a major deviation under GL-QGF-009 and requires immediate corrective action and deviation reporting.

**5.2 Testing Methodology**
Sampled SRV-S units are assessed by transduction efficiency assay (GL-ASSAY-VE-4) as described in Section 4.1. Sampled BGA-2 units are assessed by high-performance liquid chromatography potency profiling (GL-ASSAY-BGA-2-09) with acceptance criteria specified in GL-SPHC Appendix D, Table D-2.

**5.3 Documentation**
All batch testing records must be retained in the Genesis Labs Product Integrity Archive for a minimum of seven years post-lot expiry and must be made available to Somatic Product Integrity Division auditors upon request within 48 hours.

**5.4 Non-Conforming Lots**
Lots failing batch testing must be quarantined under restricted access conditions (see GL-SPHC Section 8.3) and disposed of in accordance with Biohazard Protocol BH-7 following written disposition authorization from the Somatic Product Integrity Division Director.

## 6. Temperature Exceedance Procedures

**6.1 Detection and Logging**
Temperature exceedance events must be logged within two hours of detection using Cold-Chain Incident Form GL-CCIF-03. The log must record: time and date of confirmed exceedance onset, maximum recorded temperature, duration of exceedance, product lots affected, and the name of the responsible logistics or clinic officer.

**6.2 Exceedance Response by Duration**
- **Fewer than 24 continuous hours above 28°C:** Log and flag per Section 3.3; enhanced viability spot-check at next scheduled interval.
- **24 to 72 continuous hours above 28°C:** Mandatory enhanced viability testing of the full affected lot prior to any clinical release; release prohibited until testing confirms viability above threshold.
- **More than 72 continuous hours above 28°C:** Immediate reclassification as non-viable; full lot disposal per Biohazard Protocol BH-7; mandatory deviation report to Somatic Product Integrity Division within 24 hours of reclassification.

**6.3 Escalation**
Temperature exceedances affecting more than one product category simultaneously, or affecting more than one clinic location within a 14-day window, must be escalated to the Somatic Product Integrity Division Director and the Regional Logistics Compliance Officer within 24 hours.

## 7. Personalized Retroviral Vector Handling (Category SRV-P)

Personalized retroviral vectors are produced at certified Genesis Labs Somatic Clinics using genome-specific tailoring derived from individual patient genomic profiles. Due to the genome-specific nature of the retroviral construct and the absence of large-batch stabilization capacity, **Category SRV-P vectors carry a reduced shelf life of 30 days** from the date of final production sign-off, irrespective of storage conditions.

SRV-P vectors must be stored at 2–8°C throughout their usable window. The minimum viability standard of 92% at 120 days applicable to Category SRV-S does not apply to SRV-P products; instead, SRV-P lots must meet a minimum 95% viability standard at 14 days post-production, assessed by GL-ASSAY-VE-4 prior to patient administration. Any SRV-P lot failing the 14-day assessment or reaching its 30-day expiry must be disposed of per Biohazard Protocol BH-7.

SRV-P production, testing, and disposal records must be maintained per Somatic Clinic Personalized Vector Registry GL-PVR-02 and cross-referenced with individual patient treatment records in compliance with Genesis Labs Patient Data Governance Protocol GL-PDG-005.
