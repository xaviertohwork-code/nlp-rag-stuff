## I. Service History

Dr. Sekara joined the Nyari Federal Intelligence Service on 2105-09-01, following successful completion of the standard analytical intake examination and a full security vetting process. ## III. Role Description

Dr. Sekara's primary function within the Communications Protocol Division is the detection and analysis of anomalous routing patterns within encrypted and semi-encrypted signal traffic passing through or originating from Nyari Confederation communications infrastructure. Her specialization in communications protocol analysis encompasses both passive monitoring of signal traffic and the development of detection frameworks used by junior analysts across the division. She is additionally responsible for periodic audit of the Confederation's secure routing architecture and has served as lead technical contributor on three inter-agency protocol review panels since 2108.

## V. Supervisor Notes — Late 2113 Conduct

*"During discussion of the proposed expansion of the centralized signal monitoring protocol to additional outer-island relay nodes, Dr. Sekara raised what she described as 'philosophical reservations about the scalability of centralized monitoring in a decentralized society.' The comment was not accompanied by a formal objection or procedural challenge. Other analysts present did not engage with the remark substantively. No formal disciplinary notation was made at the time. The supervisor has requested this observation be entered into the annual review record for institutional continuity.

## VI. Concerns — Independent Research Leave Requests

Between 2113 and early 2114, Dr. Sekara submitted **three formal requests for independent research leave**, each proposing a period of between four and eight weeks for self-directed cryptographic protocol study outside the NFIS institutional framework. The requests cite, variously, personal academic development, protocol research gaps not currently addressed by the division's mandate, and the need for what she terms "methodological distance." Two requests were declined on operational grounds; the third remains under review.

Personnel policy permits independent research leave at supervisory discretion, and the submission of such requests is not irregular in isolation. However, the frequency — three requests within approximately fourteen months — is atypical for an analyst at Dr. Sekara's seniority level and has been flagged by her supervisor as warranting monitoring.

---

## VII. Recommendations

Following review of the above record, this office offers the following assessment:

Dr. Sekara's technical contributions to the Communications Protocol Division remain of high institutional value and her continued employment is **recommended without reservation**.

However, given the combination of observations noted above, this office further recommends the application of **enhanced supervisory oversight** during the forthcoming review cycle. This is to be understood as a precautionary and supportive measure — not a disciplinary action — intended to ensure that Dr. Sekara's professional development remains aligned with divisional priorities.

A follow-up assessment is recommended no later than 2115-04-01.
