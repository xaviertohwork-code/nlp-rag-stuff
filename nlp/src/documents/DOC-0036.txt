### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This position paper, jointly prepared in advance of the annual CGC-Bloc trade review, sets out the formal objections of the Sharpsea Bloc to recently imposed Clairos Governance Council trade measures targeting Bloc-origin exports. It assesses the economic impact of these measures, draws upon relevant historical precedent to frame the Bloc's negotiating posture, and recommends a coordinated set of countermeasures designed to signal collective resolve. All three constituent nations are invited to formally adopt this posture prior to the commencement of review proceedings.

---

### BACKGROUND

The Clairos Governance Council has, with increasing frequency, employed tariff escalation as an instrument of political leverage against non-aligned states. The most recent and most consequential such action directly affects the Sharpsea Bloc: the CGC formally imposed a fourteen percent (14%) surcharge on all Bloc-origin aquaculture exports effective the fourth quarter of 2116 CE. This measure was introduced without prior consultation with any Bloc government, was not accompanied by any stated regulatory justification, and applies selectively to Bloc producers while exempting Haven-origin and CGC-affiliated producers operating in the same product categories.

The surcharge represents a material escalation beyond previous CGC trade friction and must be understood in the context of the CGC's longstanding effort to create economic dependency relationships that erode the political autonomy of non-aligned states. This Ministry regards the measure as a deliberate act of economic coercion rather than a legitimate trade-regulatory decision.

---

### ECONOMIC IMPACT ASSESSMENT

The immediate economic impact falls most heavily upon the Republic of Zonnon, whose aquaculture sector bears the greatest exposure to CGC-controlled Haven markets. In 2116 CE, Zonnon's aquaculture exports to Haven totalled approximately 28 billion Phi Credits — a figure that represents the largest single export stream of any Bloc nation and reflects decades of investment in Zonnon's offshore protein-farming infrastructure. The application of a fourteen percent surcharge to this volume constitutes an annual levy in the order of 3.9 billion Phi Credits on Zonnon producers alone, a burden that will, if sustained, drive significant margin compression across the sector and may accelerate consolidation toward the very CGC-affiliated distribution intermediaries that Zonnon has historically sought to limit.

Kashikari and Nyari face more diffuse but nonetheless material impacts, primarily through downstream effects on maritime logistics volumes and associated service revenues. Reduced Zonnon export throughput translates directly to reduced Kashikari shipping activity on the principal Zonnon-Haven routes.

For context, the Sharpsea Bloc's combined gross domestic product, across all three constituent nations, is estimated at 684 billion Phi Credits as of 2116 CE. The proportional significance of the aquaculture sector to the Bloc's aggregate economic base underscores why targeted pressure on this sector constitutes leverage disproportionate to the apparent narrowness of the measure.

---

### HISTORICAL PRECEDENT

The CGC has previously employed selective tariff measures as precursors to broader economic campaigns against non-compliant governments, a pattern well-documented in the pre-Cascade period and replicated with regularity since the establishment of the Haven Island Special Economic Zone. The Bloc has not always responded with full cohesion, and it is important to acknowledge this frankly.

In 2115 CE, the Bloc attempted to file a joint trade protest against an earlier, more limited round of CGC market-access restrictions. That protest failed to achieve the status of a fully co-signed Bloc document. The Kashikari Hegemony did not ultimately co-sign the protest; the Patara family's intervention within the Hegemony Council produced a consensus blockage that resulted in Kashikari's abstention from the filing. The protest was consequently submitted under Zonnon and Nyari signatures only, materially weakening its standing as a representation of collective Bloc will. The CGC noted the absence of a unified signature in its formal response, and subsequent communications from CGC representatives have cited the 2115 document's incomplete endorsement as evidence of intra-Bloc disagreement.

This Ministry regards that episode as a cautionary precedent. The current surcharge measure is of a scale and directness that demands a unified response. A repeat of the 2115 outcome would be interpreted by the CGC as confirmation that the Bloc cannot maintain collective discipline under economic pressure — precisely the signal that would encourage further escalation.

---

### RECOMMENDED COUNTERMEASURES

Following consultations among participating governments, this position paper recommends the following coordinated countermeasures, to be adopted simultaneously upon formal acceptance of this joint posture:

**i.** A formal retaliatory restriction on Cyanite Industries mineral survey vessels operating in proximity to Bloc waters. Cyanite has maintained an ongoing program of exploratory survey activity along the outer Bloc maritime zones, the commercial benefit of which accrues exclusively to Cyanite while the operational externalities — interference with Bloc fishing and aquaculture activity, navigational hazard, and surveillance risk — are borne by Bloc communities. Restricting survey vessel access to a defined exclusion zone would impose a direct and proportionate cost on a CGC member whose material interests are most legible to the other CGC parties.

**ii.** A coordinated deferral of Bloc participation in CGC-convened trade-review working groups pending withdrawal of the aquaculture surcharge, signaling that procedural cooperation is contingent on good-faith conduct.

**iii.** An expedited review of existing preferential routing arrangements with CGC-affiliated logistics operators, with a view to renegotiating terms on a more reciprocal basis.

---

### NEXT STEPS

This Ministry requests formal written endorsement of this joint posture from the Kashikari Hegemony Council and the Nyari Confederation Federal Leadership no later than 2117-04-01 CE, in advance of the scheduled opening of the annual CGC-Bloc trade review. All three governments are further invited to designate senior representatives to a coordinating session to be convened in New Mewan at a date to be agreed.

The Republic of Zonnon stands ready to lead formal presentation of the joint posture at the trade review, subject to confirmation of full Bloc endorsement.
