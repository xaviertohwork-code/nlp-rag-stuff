# Haven Public Library Network
## 76 PCE Annual Usage Statistics and New Branch Announcement

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
**77-02-01**
**Haven Island Special Economic Zone**

---

**HAVEN PUBLIC LIBRARY NETWORK REPORTS 76 PCE USAGE FIGURES; ANNOUNCES NEW PORTSIDE BRANCH**

*HAVEN* — The Haven Public Library Network (HPLN) today released its annual usage statistics for 76 PCE and announced the planned opening of a new branch facility in west Haven's Portside district later this year.

---

### Network Overview

The Haven Public Library Network currently operates **23 branches across all five Haven sectors** — North, East, West, South, and Central — serving Havenites of every background and affiliation. The Network's mission remains unchanged: to provide free, open access to information, cultural materials, and community space for all residents of Haven, regardless of corporate affiliation, Edge status, or economic circumstance.

---

### Physical Visits

Total physical visits across all 23 HPLN branches in 76 PCE reached **6.8 million**, representing a **3% decline compared to 75 PCE**. The Network acknowledges this modest dip with candour. Director of Public Services Yolanda Fertsch noted that foot traffic patterns across Haven have shifted considerably over the past several years, and that the Network is actively reviewing branch programming and facility design to respond to evolving community needs. Several branches reported increased attendance at in-person events — lectures, archival exhibitions, and skills workshops — even as general browsing visits declined. The Network does not regard this trend as cause for alarm, but it does inform the design priorities of future branch development.

---

### Edge-Accessible Catalog Usage

On the digital side, the picture is markedly different. **Edge-accessible catalog queries rose to 41 million in 76 PCE**, an increase of **18% over 75 PCE** figures. This growth reflects both expanded catalog digitization efforts completed during 75–76 PCE and the Network's investment in Edge-optimized search and retrieval interfaces, which received a significant usability upgrade in mid-76 PCE. The HPLN Edge catalog is accessible to any Haven resident with active nanobot integration at no cost and without a physical branch visit. The Network considers robust Edge access a core service, not a supplementary one, and plans further catalog expansion through 77 PCE.

---

### New Branch Announcement: West Haven Portside

The HPLN is pleased to announce that its **24th branch will open in west Haven's Portside district in the sixth month of 77 PCE**. The Portside district has been underserved by the Network for several years — the nearest existing branches require transit connections that present a meaningful barrier for residents in the port-adjacent neighborhoods. The new facility will offer the Network's full range of services, including physical collections, community event space, and public access terminals.

The branch's defining feature at opening will be a **dedicated Opted reading room offering no Edge connectivity** — a quiet, fully analog space designed for Havenites who prefer to access materials outside the Edge environment. This is the first such dedicated space in any HPLN facility. The room will offer an expanded physical periodicals collection and carrel-style individual study seating. While it has attracted some attention during the planning process, the HPLN regards it simply as one amenity among many in a branch designed to serve the full diversity of the Portside community.

---

### Looking Ahead

The HPLN thanks the residents of Haven for their continued engagement with the Network and looks forward to welcoming the Portside community in 77-06.
