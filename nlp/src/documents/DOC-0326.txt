# HAVEN HERALD
## East Haven's Somatic Boom: New Genesis Labs Clinics Draw Lines Around the Block

*Haven Herald Lifestyle Desk | 76-08-22*

---

East Haven has a new landmark queue. Since the opening of Genesis Labs' latest pair of Somatic Clinics in the shadow of Helix Tower, the footpaths running along the eastern edge of the Genesis campus have taken on the unmistakable character of a popular venue on launch day — early risers clutching paper cups of stimulant tea, regulars trading tips on which appointment window opens first, and the occasional bewildered passerby stopping to ask what everyone is waiting for.

The answer, increasingly, is a better body.

**Wait Times and Walk-Ins**

Demand at the new east Haven location has outpaced even Genesis Labs' own projections. As of this week, prospective patients booking a standard personalized consultation are looking at an average wait time of approximately two weeks — a figure that would have seemed unremarkable for a specialty medical service a decade ago, but which stands out precisely because both new clinic sites had initially advertised same-week availability at launch.

"Two weeks felt like a long time when I first looked at the calendar," said Dae-Woo, a twenty-six-year-old logistics coordinator who joined the queue outside the Helix Tower-adjacent clinic on a recent afternoon. "But I've been thinking about this for months. I need the visual acuity enhancement — my work involves reading cargo manifests across three simultaneous display feeds, and my eyes are just not keeping up. Two weeks is nothing if it actually delivers." Dae-Woo emphasized that he has not yet undergone the procedure and is currently still in the consultation-booking stage, a point he made with what seemed like equal parts patience and anticipation.

Stories like his are common in the queue. First-timers with vocational reasons for Augmentation — precision trades, extended-screen roles, physical labor — make up a substantial portion of prospective patients outside the new clinics.

---

**What You're Paying For**

### Clinic Pricing

A standard personalized Somatic Augmentation at a Genesis Labs Somatic Clinic carries a price tag of approximately **22,000 Phi Credits** — a figure that includes the full intake assessment, genome sequencing required for retrovirus personalization, the tailored retroviral administration, and the several-months course of second-generation biological growth enhancers. That cost also covers the follow-up appointments and, crucially, the bespoke calibration that Genesis Labs markets as the cornerstone of its service.

Genesis Labs has long held the distinction of operating the most refined and safe Somatic Clinics in Clairos, a reputation built over decades of development and clinical oversight since Somatic Augmentation was first offered as a service. The personalized retrovirus approach — custom-designed around each individual patient's genome — is what Genesis Labs' marketing teams consistently point to when justifying the premium.

"You're not buying a general procedure," a Genesis Labs clinic advisor told the Herald. "You're buying a procedure designed for you, which is a fundamentally different thing."

---

### Street Alternatives

For those who find 22,000 Phi Credits well beyond reach, the conversation inevitably turns to the other option. Street biodealers across Haven — concentrated, as ever, around port-adjacent neighborhoods and the industrial fringe — offer non-personalized Somatics for as little as **400 to 600 Phi Credits**, a fraction of the clinic cost and a fact that is hardly a secret to anyone who has spent time in the city's less polished corridors.

The trade-off, such as it is, comes down to what you don't get: no genome assessment, no personalized retrovirus, no clinical follow-up, and no guarantee of what exactly is in the vial. Biodealers operate without oversight, and their product lines vary considerably in quality and origin.

---

**More Space, Same Standard**

Genesis Labs representatives confirmed to the Herald that both new east Haven locations, situated near Helix Tower, are fully staffed and operating under the same clinical protocols as the existing Haven flagship. Appointment slots for the coming weeks are already filled; the clinic's digital booking system reportedly logged over four thousand registrations in its first seventy-two hours online.

For East Haven's newest queue-dwellers, the wait, apparently, is worth it.
