**I. BACKGROUND AND ASSET ALLOCATION STATUS**

Following the coordinated CGC action of 25-01-28 (28 PCE), ONE Network Enterprises received approximately 22% of Wampa Robotics' physical assets under the proportional distribution formula applied across participating CGC members. It should be noted for the record that this allocation was not the largest among CGC participants; Cyanite Industries received a greater share of Wampa's physical manufacturing stock, reflecting their respective role and standing in the distribution proceedings. ONE's 22% stake nonetheless represents a substantial volume of logistics automation hardware, robotic handling equipment, and proprietary fleet-management terminal infrastructure, the integration of which is the subject of this memorandum.

Physical asset transfer is substantially complete. The challenge confronting this Directorate is not possession but operationalization.

---

**II. TIMELINE REVISION**

The original integration timeline of 10 months, submitted to the Executive Leadership Council in 28-02-12 (28 PCE), has been revised. Full integration of Wampa automation systems into ONE's existing shipyard and port infrastructure is now projected to require **18 months** from the date of this memorandum, yielding a revised completion target of approximately 30-11 (late 30 PCE).

This revision — extending the timeline by 8 months — is driven primarily by software incompatibilities that were not anticipated at the time of initial projection. Wampa's automation systems operate on a proprietary firmware stack, designated internally as WampaOS-Aegis (versions 4.1 through 4.7), which communicates via a closed-loop machine-control protocol incompatible with ONE's existing NAVCOM-7 port management architecture. Attempts to run Aegis terminals within NAVCOM-7 environments produce persistent handshake failures at the hardware abstraction layer; interim bridging software developed by ONE's Engineering Division has achieved only partial resolution, with an estimated 61% command-execution fidelity under test conditions — below the 95% threshold required for live operational deployment.

To be direct: we cannot deploy what we cannot make speak to our existing infrastructure. The revised 18-month figure reflects a realistic assessment of the reverse-engineering and middleware development effort required. A 24-month timeline was modeled and rejected as operationally unnecessary given current engineering capacity; 18 months remains the committed projection.

Engineering has flagged three additional areas of concern: (a) Wampa's robotic cargo-handling units use a positional-calibration standard incompatible with ONE's dock-floor sensor grids; (b) Wampa's fleet management terminals rely on a deprecated encryption handshake unsupported by ONE's current cybersecurity standards; and (c) Wampa's maintenance and diagnostic software is partially undocumented, requiring engineering reverse-analysis before it can be safely deployed in live environments.
