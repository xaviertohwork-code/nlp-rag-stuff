**PHASE II STATUS**

Phase II of the Sharpsea Route Optimization Program is operational. Following your personal review and approval of Phase I in 75 PCE, this office was directed to extend algorithmic routing to the full working Sharpsea fleet. As of 76-10-30 PCE, the new algorithmic routing suite has been deployed across 74 ONE vessels operating in Sharpsea Bloc shipping lanes. Deployment was completed on schedule and within allocated operational budget. All 74 vessels are confirmed transmitting route telemetry to the Maritime Operations coordination network.

---

**PERFORMANCE METRICS**

Early returns are favorable. On Kashikari-Haven routes specifically, fuel efficiency has improved by 8.3% since Phase II deployment — this gain is attributable to the algorithmic suite's dynamic lane adjustment capability, which exploits tidal and current data in real time. I want to be precise here for the record: the 8.3% figure applies to Kashikari-Haven routes, not Zonnon-Haven routes, where routing complexity and the Zonnon Maritime Defense Command's historical interference with commercial transit make comparable gains difficult to measure cleanly. Full-fleet efficiency data for all Sharpsea corridors will be compiled in the Q1 77 PCE performance cycle.

---

**FLEET DISPOSITION**

ONE Network currently maintains 31 vessels on permanent Sharpsea Bloc rotation. The Phase II deployment encompassed the broader operational fleet cycling through the region, which exceeds our permanent rotation complement. Fleet commanders have reported no significant mechanical or integration issues with the new routing suite. Vessel positioning is being managed in accordance with existing transit agreements.

---

**DIPLOMATIC CONCERNS**

This office must flag an escalating diplomatic sensitivity that requires your attention at the executive level.

The Zonnon Maritime Defense Command has filed a formal protest with ONE's regional liaison office regarding the increased ONE vessel presence in Sharpsea waters. Their protest document characterizes the presence as, and I quote directly: *"provocative posturing."* Zonnon's Isolationist political faction has amplified the complaint through the Republic's legislature; the Prime Minister's office has not publicly endorsed the language but has declined to rebuke it. This is consistent with Zonnon's established posture toward CGC-affiliated commercial activity, and we should not overinterpret it as a departure from their baseline position. Nevertheless, a formal protest on record requires a formal response.

Separately, there are early indications — informal, not yet actionable — that the Hegemony Council in Cape Tidak may be preparing to raise concerns about the pace and scale of ONE's expanded Sharpsea operations. This would be considerably more consequential than Zonnon's protest, given Kashikari's operational centrality to our routing infrastructure.

---

**RECOMMENDATIONS**

I offer the following recommendations for your consideration:

1. **Kashikari diplomatic outreach.** I recommend initiating direct diplomatic contact with the principal Kashikari shipping families at the earliest opportunity to pre-empt any formal Hegemony Council complaint. A formal complaint, once tabled, becomes significantly harder to manage quietly. Relationship-level engagement before that threshold is reached is the preferred course of action.

2. **Zonnon protest response.** A measured, non-escalatory formal response to the Zonnon Maritime Defense Command's protest should be drafted and cleared through your office. I recommend framing our position around commercial transit rights under existing agreements rather than engaging the "posturing" characterization directly.

3. **Phase II continuation.** No operational pause is warranted. The performance data justifies continuation; any suspension in response to the Zonnon protest would be read by all Bloc parties as a concession.

Awaiting your direction.

**V/ADM (Ret.) K. Hallstrom**
**Maritime Operations Director, ONE Network Enterprises**
