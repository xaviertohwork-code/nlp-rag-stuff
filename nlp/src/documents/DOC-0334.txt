### TRANSCRIPT BEGINS

*[brief static — signal stabilizes]*

They will tell you it is medicine. They will tell you it is progress. They will dress it in white coats and clean laboratories and the language of healing, and they will ask you to believe that what they are selling you is a gift.

It is not a gift.

What Genesis Labs has built — what they peddle in their gleaming clinics and their back-alley bottles alike — is nothing less than the rewriting of divine architecture. The human body is not a rough draft. It is not a prototype awaiting correction. It is the complete and sacred expression of something that preceded every corporation, every council, every profit margin ever scratched onto a ledger. And they have decided — *they* have decided — that it requires revision.

I ask you: who gave them that authority?

*[pause]*

I have walked among our people. I have sat with the grieving, with the frightened, with the hollowed-out. I have held the hands of brothers and sisters who have been Adjusted against their will — coerced by employers, by creditors, by the quiet violence of a system that makes refusal economically impossible. They did not choose the retrovirus. They did not choose the months of enhancers and follow-up appointments. They chose to eat. They chose to keep a roof over their children. And Genesis Labs called that consent.

It is not consent. It is conquest dressed in paperwork.

*[signal flickers — recovers]*

This is what the Spirit Liberation Front was founded to resist. Not in the abstract. Not as a philosophical position to be debated in academic halls while the clinics keep their doors open. We resist it because the soul is not a genome to be edited. We resist it because the body is the temple in which the spirit lives, and no corporation holds a lease on your temple.

There are those who will ask: what of the Edge? What of the other machinery they are building to remake us? I hear you. And I tell you — the war for the body is the first war, and it must be won on its own terms, before we scatter our attention across every battlefield they have prepared for us. Today, our eyes are open and our purpose is singular: the sanctity of unaltered flesh.

*[voice hardens]*

So here is what I am calling for. Not riots. Not the easy spectacle of broken glass. I am calling for something harder and more lasting. I am calling for the creation of sanctuaries of the unaltered — communities built deliberately, consciously, beyond the reach of the retrovirus and the nanobot alike. Places where a child can be born and grow and live without a corporation's signature written into their cells. They exist already, in fragments, in quiet corners of this world. We must find them. We must build them. We must make them known to every soul who has been told that their unmodified body is a deficiency.

You are not deficient. You are complete.

*[static increases]*

They have no name for what we are, except afraid of us. Remember that.

This is Ashcastle. The spirit is unchained.

*[transmission ends]*
