# EDGE TAKES HAVEN BY STORM: TEC'S DRAMATIC LAUNCH EVENT DISRUPTS CITY SYSTEMS

**Renhwa Media Technology Desk | 68-04-22**

---

## BREAKING: EDGE RESEARCH SEIZES HAVEN'S SCREENS IN UNPRECEDENTED STUNT

HAVEN — In what sources across the CGC are already calling the most brazen and irresponsible corporate stunt in recent memory, Edge Research yesterday launched its so-called "Edge" platform across Haven in a display that transformed every networked screen, device, and terminal in the city into an involuntary stage.

At approximately 19:00 hours, screens flickered. Displays in lobbies, transit stations, and residential blocks stuttered and died before erupting into cascading neon overlays. Personal devices locked, rebooted, and reoriented themselves toward a single broadcast: Edge Research's launch event, delivered without invitation into the hands and homes of every Havenite connected to the city's network infrastructure. Virtually every networked device across Haven was commandeered simultaneously, a feat of technical aggression that left ordinary citizens bewildered and corporate operations grinding to a halt.

The Edge, as Edge Research markets it, is an immersive augmented reality overlay powered by nanobots — microscopic machines drawn to the visual cortex, augmenting the user's perception of the world around them. Whatever its technical merits, the manner of its arrival was anything but subtle.

---

## DISRUPTION ACROSS HAVEN

The impact on other CGC member corporations' operations was immediate and severe. Financial processing systems experienced forced interruption. Logistics coordination terminals, manufacturing oversight dashboards, and communications networks all suffered cascading failures during the broadcast period. One senior figure familiar with the disruption to port operations described the incident as "an act of corporate sabotage dressed up as a product launch."

This publication can confirm that several CGC member corporations suffered measurable operational losses in the hours following the event, as technical teams scrambled to reassert control over hijacked infrastructure. The full accounting of damages has not yet been disclosed, but internal sources across multiple megacorporations describe the disruption as "massive."

Edge Research offered no prior warning to other CGC members, no regulatory filing, and no contingency coordination. The launch was, in the words of one internal communications document obtained by this publication, designed for maximum impact — and that it certainly achieved.

---

## BLAME FALLS ON PHYREXIS — AND DENIALS ARE NOT FORTHCOMING

The fury from the CGC directed at Edge Research has been matched, if not exceeded, by the fury directed at Phyrexis Group. Other CGC megacorporations have placed the blame for the incident squarely on Phyrexis, whose covert funding relationship with Edge Research has long been an open secret among Haven's corporate establishment — though never previously an acknowledged liability.

"Everyone in this building knows who bankrolled this," said one unnamed CGC representative, declining to be identified by name or affiliation. "This was not a rogue actor. This was a funded project, and the funder needs to be held accountable."

Critically, Phyrexis made no public claim of credit for the launch — indeed, sources describe the partnership as scrambling to distance themselves from the incident even as the neon overlays were still pulsing across Haven's skyline. The notion that Phyrexis proudly embraced this moment is directly contradicted by the frantic internal communications now leaking from the group's south Haven offices: what the other megacorporations witnessed was not pride, but a corporation caught in the wreckage of its own investment.

Multiple CGC representatives, speaking on condition of anonymity, stated that the incident had fundamentally altered the trust environment within the council. "Phyrexis enabled this," one said flatly. "Whatever they claim now, they enabled this."

---

## PHYREXIS RESPONSE: SEVERANCE IN 69 PCE

In the months following the launch, pressure on Phyrexis from the other CGC megacorporations has been unrelenting. Sources close to the partnership confirm that negotiations over the terms and timing of a formal separation from Edge Research have been ongoing since the night of the launch itself.

Phyrexis Group is expected to formally sever its funding relationship with Edge Research in 69 PCE — a reactionary measure, by all appearances, driven not by strategic intent but by the need to recover standing within the CGC. Whether this severance represents a genuine reckoning or merely a public relations exercise remains to be seen; this publication will continue to report on its implications.

For Havenites, the question is simpler. Last night, someone decided that every screen in Haven belonged to them. The bill for that assumption is only beginning to arrive.
