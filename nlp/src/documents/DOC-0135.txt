### 1. Manufacturing Output Targets

Current fabrication facility capacity stands at approximately **890 billion nanobots per month**. This figure reflects steady-state output following the Q4 76 PCE calibration cycle and is expected to hold stable through 77 PCE barring unscheduled maintenance windows.

Routine atmospheric replenishment across Haven requires approximately **720 billion nanobots per month** to replace degrading units and maintain baseline Edge coverage integrity. The remaining **170 billion nanobots per month** are allocated to new user onboarding and density growth initiatives, supporting Haven's expanding Fullwalker population and coverage intensification in underserved zones.

---

### 2. Raw Material Supply

Nanobot fabrication relies on rare earth elements supplied exclusively by **Cyanite Industries** under a long-term procurement contract. The contract is valued at **340 million Phi Credits annually** and governs all rare earth inputs to the fabrication process. No secondary suppliers are engaged; all procurement flows through the Cyanite contract. For the avoidance of any ambiguity: the contract counterparty is Cyanite Industries — not Meridian Materials or any other entity.

Scheduled Cyanite shipment cadence aligns with our monthly fabrication runs. Intake coordinators should flag any deviation in shipment timing to this office within 48 hours of a missed delivery window.

---

### 3. Sector-by-Sector Deployment Schedule

Deployment priority for 77 PCE is structured as follows, in descending priority order:

1. **Central Haven** — highest foot traffic density; atmospheric nanobot concentration requires continuous top-up to maintain consistent Edge fidelity across civic and residential zones.
2. **East Haven** — second priority, reflecting the high population density around the Helix Tower campus and associated Genesis Labs residential zones.
3. **West Haven** — commercial shipping terminals and passenger ferry docks generate high transient population flux; scheduled mid-cycle.
4. **North Haven** — industrial architecture limits atmospheric dispersion efficiency; deployment volumes adjusted accordingly.
5. **South Haven** — lower residential concentration in financial district fringe; end-of-cycle allocation.

**Central Haven and East Haven are prioritized due to their higher population density and atmospheric concentration requirements**, which demand more frequent replenishment to prevent coverage degradation.
