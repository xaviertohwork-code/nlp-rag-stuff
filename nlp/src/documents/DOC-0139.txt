# Edge Network Hits Record Concurrent Users — TEC Celebrates Milestone Amid Growing Pains

**By Jun Hara, Technology Correspondent | Renhwa Media | 77-03-28**

---

## Milestone: A New Peak for the Edge

The Edge network shattered its own concurrent usage record during the Haven Founders' Festival on 77-03-22, logging **19.1 million simultaneous avatar instances** across Haven — a figure that TEC's own communications division has since described as a landmark moment in the platform's history. The achievement arrived in a press release timed for maximum visibility, and TEC wasted little time distributing it to every networked device in the city.

Whether those same 19.1 million users felt like celebrating is a rather different question.

---

## The Record in Context

For comparison, the previous high-water mark stood at **18.3 million concurrent avatar instances**, set during the 76 PCE year-end celebrations. The Founders' Festival figure eclipsed that benchmark by a meaningful margin — the latest in a steady climb that has tracked Haven's growing dependence on Edge as its primary social and commercial infrastructure.

TEC's milestone framing, however, invited scrutiny about what exactly was being celebrated.

---

## User Complaints: Flickering, Lag, and Lost Identities

For many Havenites, the Founders' Festival evening was memorable for the wrong reasons. Reports flooded community boards within hours of peak load, describing a platform under visible strain.

Users documented **widespread avatar flickering**, in which rendered appearances stuttered or partially dissolved mid-interaction. Others reported **delayed interaction responses** — gestures, speech cues, and commercial transactions lagging several seconds behind real-time input, a disorienting experience in a platform designed to feel seamless. More alarming were accounts of **temporary identity glitches**: instances in which users' avatars briefly displayed corrupted or misassigned visual identities, a malfunction that some affected users described as deeply unsettling.

The complaints were not isolated. Independent tallies on community channels suggest tens of thousands of users experienced at least one of the three failure modes during the festival's peak hours.

---

## TEC's Response: An Unnamed Voice and No Timeline

Renhwa Media contacted TEC for comment. The company did not make any executive available. No named TEC official — not Sim Jiahong, not Chief Operating Officer Devika Oranyan, nor any other identified representative — issued a personal statement or offered a remediation timeline. Instead, **an unnamed TEC spokesperson** provided a written statement, attributing the performance degradation to "unprecedented demand exceeding our most optimistic projections."

The spokesperson added that TEC remains "fully committed to infrastructure investment that reflects the scale of Haven's ambitions."

What form that investment will take, when it will begin, or how much will be allocated was not specified. When asked directly whether TEC intended to seek additional CYPHER satellite allocation from the CGC to expand the Edge network's processing capacity, **TEC declined to comment**.

---

## Industry Analysis: A Structural Problem?

Not everyone finds TEC's framing persuasive. A Renhwa Media analyst, speaking on condition of anonymity given the sensitivity of CGC-adjacent commentary, offered a blunter assessment.

"This is not a story about a single festival overperforming expectations," the analyst said. "TEC's infrastructure is **fundamentally undersized for Haven's actual population dynamics**. The Edge wasn't built for a city of 100 million people living their entire economic and social lives on the platform. It was built for something considerably smaller, and the company has been papering over that gap with favorable timing and good press for years."

The analyst declined to speculate on the financial implications of a full infrastructure overhaul, noting only that "the cost would not be cosmetic."

---

## What Comes Next

TEC's refusal to address the CYPHER satellite question is conspicuous. Any meaningful expansion of Edge's processing headroom in the near term would almost certainly require negotiation with the CGC over orbital resource allocation — a politically complex conversation that would expose the platform's dependencies to the scrutiny of every megacorp on the Council.

For now, Havenites who spent their Founders' Festival watching their avatars flicker in and out of existence can take comfort in the knowledge that the network handling those failures has never been busier.
