### ITEM 2: ZONING VARIANCE REQUEST — BLOCK 7C-42

Deputy Chair Essomba presented the submitted variance application from Coravel Development Holdings requesting permission to erect a mixed residential-commercial development at Block 7C-42. The applicant's representative, present in gallery, outlined projected unit counts and intended ground-floor commercial purposes. Several councillors raised concerns regarding pedestrian throughput, shadowing of adjacent low-rise residential units, and adequacy of sanitation connections.

Councillors Rourke, Osei-Bonsu, and Mittler expressed support, citing anticipated employment and housing supply benefit to the district. Councillors Ostroff and Deschamps opposed, noting insufficient traffic impact assessment documentation.

**Motion to approve variance application for Block 7C-42** by Councillor Rourke, seconded by Councillor Osei-Bonsu.

**Vote: 9 in favour, 5 opposed, 0 abstentions. Motion carried.**

*Action: Deputy Chair Essomba to notify applicant and file approved variance documentation with Haven Zoning Registry within ten working days.*

### ITEM 4: NOISE COMPLAINT — TESSERA STREET SOMATIC CLINIC QUEUE

The council received a formal noise complaint filed by resident **Kauri Patel** of 18 Garren Walk, District 7-C. The complaint concerns overnight queuing activity outside the Genesis Labs Somatic Clinic on Tessera Street. Mr. Patel's written submission, entered into the record as Attachment B, describes persistent noise from queuing individuals beginning from approximately 23:00 and continuing through dawn, foot traffic congestion on the footpath and partial obstruction of the adjacent service lane, and periodic amplified verbal exchanges among queue members.

Councillor Calladay noted that the clinic's queues had visibly lengthened over the past three months. Councillor Mittler observed that the congestion affects two adjoining residential addresses in addition to Mr. Patel's property.

Council consensus was that the complaint warrants formal correspondence to the clinic's facility management.

*Action: Chairperson Nwachukwu to draft and dispatch written notice to Genesis Labs Somatic Clinic, Tessera Street, requesting a written response within 21 days addressing overnight queue management practices.*

### ITEM 5: PROPOSED EDGE-FREE DESIGNATION — LOT 7C-19 COMMUNITY GARDEN

Councillor Sulawesi introduced the proposal to formally designate the planned community garden at Lot 7C-19 as an Edge-free zone, meaning that any use of Edge-active nanobot infrastructure within the designated boundaries would be discouraged by posted signage and excluded from any future district connectivity contracts covering that lot.

Discussion was substantive. Councillor Ostroff argued in favour: *"People need somewhere in this district that belongs to them without a connection running through their head. A garden is exactly the right place for that."* Councillor Farren seconded the principle, adding that the designation would make the space more accessible to Opted residents who have expressed discomfort attending district community events in Edge-active environments.

Councillor Deschamps expressed reservations: *"I'm not sure we should be drawing lines on the ground about what technology people choose to use in a public space. It sets a precedent."* Councillor Trellis agreed that the practical enforceability of such a designation was unclear.

**Motion to designate Lot 7C-19 community garden as an Edge-free zone** by Councillor Ostroff, seconded by Councillor Farren.

**Vote: 11 in favour, 3 opposed, 0 abstentions. Motion carried.** The garden proposal was approved, not rejected, and the Edge-free designation is now formally in effect.

*Action: Councillor Sulawesi to coordinate with the district facilities team to procure and install appropriate signage at Lot 7C-19 prior to the garden's opening.*
