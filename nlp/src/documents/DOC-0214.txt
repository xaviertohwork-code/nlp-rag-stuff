# CGC JOINT THREAT ASSESSMENT
## CYPHER Activity Summary, 77 PCE Q1 — Launch Site Monitoring Update

**Issuing Body:** Clairos Governance Council — Joint Intelligence Directorate

**Date:** 77-04-15
**Distribution:** CGC Member Representatives (Eyes Only); CGC Secretariat (Summary Only)

## Executive Summary

This assessment summarizes CYPHER-affiliated threat activity detected during the first quarter of 77 PCE (77-01-01 through 77-03-31). The Joint Intelligence Directorate confirms two unauthorized launch attempts interdicted successfully within the quarter, with one additional incident assessed as inconclusive but consistent with preparatory activity for a future unauthorized launch. Threat tempo remains elevated relative to the same period in 76 PCE. The Conclave — designated a terrorist organization by CGC Resolution 72-CR-04 in 72 PCE (2114 CE) — continues to demonstrate adaptive operational security practices, complicating monitoring efforts. No new launchpad infrastructure was confirmed constructed during the quarter. Seventeen known former launch sites remain under active CGC monitoring. Recommendations for enhanced surveillance resourcing are detailed in the final section of this document.

---

## Incident Log

**Incident 77Q1-A — Confirmed Interdiction, Southern Shipping Corridor**
In the third week of 77Q1, ONE Network Enterprises maritime patrol units operating in the southern shipping corridor detected and intercepted a surface-to-low-orbit payload delivery attempt. ONE naval assets were on routine route-security deployment at the time of detection. The payload configuration, recovered intact following interdiction, is consistent with CYPHER resupply materiel documented in prior recovered shipments: maintenance-grade component assemblies and solid-state data storage modules. The Conclave cell responsible has not been identified. ONE Network Enterprises is commended for the successful interdiction and prompt transfer of recovered materiel to the Joint Intelligence Directorate for analysis.

**Incident 77Q1-B — Confirmed Interdiction, Industrial Fringe Zone**
A second unauthorized launch attempt was confirmed and interdicted in the final week of 77Q1 by joint CGC security forces conducting surveillance in an industrial fringe zone. Detailed geographic attribution of this incident is provided in the Monitoring Status section below, consistent with standing protocol for compartmentalized territorial information.

**Incident 77Q1-C — Inconclusive, Flagged for Continued Monitoring**
A third incident was logged in the second week of 77Q1 following anomalous energy readings and logistical activity at a location consistent with known Conclave staging patterns. No launch was confirmed. The incident is assessed as consistent with preparatory activity — site reconnaissance, component pre-positioning, or dry-run coordination — but cannot be confirmed as an interdicted attempt. The site remains under enhanced passive surveillance pending further indicators.

## Monitoring Status

**Active Site Coverage:** CGC monitoring currently covers 17 known former launch sites across Clairos. Coverage modalities vary by site: 9 sites maintain continuous automated sensor monitoring; 5 sites are subject to periodic physical inspection supplemented by satellite observation; 3 sites are monitored exclusively through satellite imagery due to access constraints. No sites have been added to or removed from the monitored inventory this quarter.

**Territorial Attribution of Q1 Incidents:** Of the three Q1 incidents, Incidents 77Q1-B and 77Q1-C originated from or are attributed to facilities within Cyanite Industries' territorial holdings in the operational zone surrounding Sarento. The Joint Intelligence Directorate notes that the concentration of historical and active Conclave staging activity within Cyanite-adjacent infrastructure represents a persistent pattern identified across multiple prior quarterly assessments. This Directorate does not assess deliberate Cyanite Industries facilitation; the pattern is attributed to Conclave exploitation of the complexity and geographic dispersion inherent in large-scale industrial territorial holdings, which present monitoring challenges disproportionate to more consolidated zones.

---

## Analysis

Conclave operational capability in 77Q1 demonstrates continued resilience despite CGC interdiction pressure. The two confirmed interdictions represent a measurable counter-CYPHER success; however, the inconclusive third incident underscores that detection rates remain imperfect and that Conclave cells are capable of abandoning or aborting attempts before interdiction is possible.

It bears restating for the record: the Conclave — not CYPHER itself — holds CGC terrorist designation status under Resolution 72-CR-04. CYPHER, as a distributed orbital AI system, presents a categorically distinct legal and operational challenge from a non-state actor such as the Conclave; its classification remains under separate CGC review processes. Enforcement actions, legal referrals, and public communications should continue to maintain this distinction with precision.

The Conclave's demonstrated preference for industrial and remote-zone staging continues to tax monitoring resources. The recovery of intact resupply materiel from Incident 77Q1-A provides the most technically useful intelligence haul since 75Q3 and will inform component-tracking activities in the coming quarter.

## Recommendations

1. **Expand automated sensor coverage** to the three satellite-only monitored sites within the 17-site inventory, prioritizing the two sites assessed at elevated Conclave activity risk. Resource request to be submitted under separate cover to the CGC Security Expenditure Committee.

2. **Increase ONE Network maritime patrol frequency** in the southern shipping corridor during the remainder of 77 PCE. The 77Q1-A interdiction confirms that corridor is an active Conclave transit route.

3. **Request formal Cyanite Industries security cooperation review** for Sarento-adjacent holdings, with a focus on access-point hardening and anomaly reporting protocols. This Directorate reiterates that this recommendation does not imply suspected complicity.

4. **Engage Sharpsea Bloc intelligence channels regarding Conclave activity.** Current information exchange with Bloc intelligence services on Conclave matters remains limited to ad-hoc bulletins with no regularized cadence, no shared threat taxonomy, and no mechanism for real-time escalation. The practical result is that CGC awareness of Conclave staging activity in Bloc-proximate territories lags significantly behind what coordinated sharing could provide. Formalizing this relationship presents political obstacles given existing CGC-Bloc tensions; nevertheless, the Directorate recommends that the CGC Secretariat explore whether a narrow technical information-sharing protocol — limited in scope to CYPHER-affiliated launch activity, with no broader intelligence reciprocity implied — could be proposed to Bloc counterparts on a provisional basis.

5. **Maintain monitoring inventory** at current 17-site coverage. No decommissioning of monitored sites is recommended pending further assessment of adaptive Conclave site-selection behavior.
