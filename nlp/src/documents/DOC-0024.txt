# RENHWA MEDIA
## INTERNAL MEMORANDUM — CONFIDENTIAL

**TO:** Renhwa Media Board of Directors
**FROM:** Park Soo-Hyun, Chief Executive Officer
**DATE:** 74-10-28 PCE
**SUBJECT:** Quarterly Board Update — Q3 FY74 PCE Performance Metrics
**CLASSIFICATION:** CONFIDENTIAL

---

### 1. Executive Summary

Q3 FY74 PCE produced measurable forward progress on the digital-first transformation initiative. Two of the three pilot projects returned strong early metrics, and audience data supports our thesis that Renhwa's content quality translates effectively to immersive distribution formats. The third pilot did not complete on schedule and is now tracking for Q4; this is addressed directly below. Overall, I view the quarter as a cautious but genuine step in the right direction.

---

### 2. Pilot Project Status

**Completion status:** Of the three digital-first pilot projects commissioned under the Q2 transformation mandate, two completed on schedule within the Q3 window. The third pilot was delayed into Q4 FY74 PCE due to staffing constraints in our immersive production unit. I want to be direct with the Board on this: the delay was operational, not creative, and reflects an ongoing gap in specialized talent that the People function is working to close. A revised Q4 delivery timeline has been submitted by the production lead and is appended to this memo.

I do not intend to minimize the delay. We will revisit resourcing assumptions for the remaining pilot ahead of the Q4 board session.

---

### 3. Pilot Performance Metrics

**Impression volume:** The two completed pilots generated a combined **340 million Edge-delivered impressions in their first month of distribution**, against a target of 200 million. This represents a 70% overperformance on our primary reach metric. The distribution was handled under a temporary licensing agreement with an immersive platform provider, the terms of which remain undisclosed per the agreement's confidentiality provisions.

We should not over-interpret a single month's data, but the overperformance was consistent across both pilots rather than concentrated in one, which adds confidence that the result reflects format fit rather than a one-off spike.

---

### 4. Production Cost Metrics

This section warrants careful reading. The two completed pilots were produced at an average cost of **1.4 times the industry average** for comparable immersive-format productions. For context, Renhwa's current company-wide production cost average stands at **2.3 times the industry average** — a figure that reflects legacy infrastructure overhead and production processes built for formats that are no longer our growth vectors.

The pilot cost figure was achieved through a combination of lean team structures, digital-first asset pipelines, and deliberate scope discipline on both productions. It should be understood as a proof-of-concept result, not a sustainable run-rate target without continued process investment. The People and Operations functions are currently mapping which pilot-model practices are transferable at scale.

---

### 5. Audience Retention Metrics

Audience retention is the metric I want the Board to focus on most closely this quarter. For the two completed pilots, **72% of viewers returned to engage with associated content within 7 days of their initial viewing** — compared to Renhwa's legacy content average of **38%** for equivalent 7-day return rates.

A near-doubling of return engagement is significant. It indicates that our audience, when presented with content in the format they are actively using, behaves very differently than in legacy distribution environments. This is the signal the transformation strategy is built around.

---

### 6. Outlook

Q4 priorities are: delivery of the third pilot, formal evaluation of the temporary distribution arrangement, and a resourcing review for the immersive production unit. I will present full three-pilot comparative metrics at the Q4 board session.

Questions may be directed to my office prior to the next session.
