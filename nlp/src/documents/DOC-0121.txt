## 1.0 Introduction and Document Scope

This document constitutes the authoritative foundational design specification for the Edge Neural Mesh Architecture (ENMA), Version 3.2. It describes the communication topology, visual cortex integration pathways, processing distribution model, and operational parameters governing the Edge platform's core nanobot infrastructure within the Haven Island Special Economic Zone and any licensed extension territories.

This specification was first drafted in 69 PCE and, as indicated in the revision history above, has undergone 14 major revisions since initial deployment. The current version reflects updated parameters arising from expanded Haven user density, revised Tier 3 synchronization protocols, and refinements to the avatar coherence subsystem introduced in late 74 PCE. Readers referencing earlier versions of this specification should note that sections 3.0 through 5.0 have been substantially revised since v2.7 and should not be treated as interchangeable.

This document is classified L3 and should not be distributed outside authorized personnel without approval from the Office of the Principal Systems Architect.

## 2.0 Architectural Overview: The Three-Tier Processing Model

The Edge neural mesh operates on a three-tier processing model that distributes computational load across three distinct infrastructure layers. Each tier is optimized for a specific category of processing task, differentiated primarily by latency requirements, data persistence demands, and the geographic scope of the interaction being managed.

**Figure 2.0-A (described):** Conceptual layered diagram. Tier 1 (local nanobot cluster, user-proximate) feeds upward to Tier 2 (ground relay station, Haven-distributed), which feeds upward to Tier 3 (CYPHER satellite uplink, orbital). Downward data flows follow the reverse path. No tier is bypassed under nominal operating conditions; failover logic permitting partial Tier 2 bypass is documented in Appendix C.

The three tiers are designated as follows:

- **Tier 1:** Local nanobot cluster (user-embedded)
- **Tier 2:** Ground relay station (Haven infrastructure)
- **Tier 3:** CYPHER satellite uplink (orbital processing network)

Detailed specifications for each tier are provided in Sections 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 respectively.

## 3.0 Tier 1: Local Nanobot Cluster Specification

### 3.1 Cluster Composition and Density

The foundational computational unit of the Edge neural mesh is the local nanobot cluster — the collection of individual nanobots resident within a given user's body at any operational moment. Cluster density is not uniform across the user population. Deployment scales dynamically with user body mass and augmentation level, such that local nanobot clusters consist of between 40,000 and 120,000 individual nanobots per user. Users at the lower bound of this range are typically those with minimal Somatic Augmentation history and lower body mass indices; users at the upper bound are those with significant augmentation levels and correspondingly greater body surface and tissue volume across which nanobots are distributed.

Nanobot positioning within the body is non-random. The majority of the cluster — typically 60 to 75 percent of resident units — concentrates within and adjacent to the visual cortex, the optic pathways, and the prefrontal association cortex. The remainder distributes across motor cortex adjacencies and peripheral sensory nodes, with a small reserve pool maintained in circulating lymphatic fluid for redistribution under load conditions.

### 3.2 Intra-Cluster Communication Topology

Nanobots within a local cluster communicate via short-range electromagnetic signaling operating in a frequency band proprietary to TEC. Intra-cluster mesh topology is self-organizing: each nanobot maintains active peer links with between 8 and 14 neighboring units, with the mesh reconfiguring automatically as nanobot positions shift during normal user movement. Cluster topology data is logged at the relay level (Tier 2) but is not transmitted to Tier 3 under nominal conditions.

Cluster coherence — the maintenance of a stable, high-fidelity intra-cluster mesh — is a prerequisite for all Tier 1 processing functions. Coherence degradation events (CDEs) are flagged to Tier 2 automatically and may trigger a relay-assisted cluster stabilization protocol. CDE incidence thresholds and stabilization procedures are documented in Appendix B.

## 4.0 Tier 2: Ground Relay Station Specification

### 4.1 Relay Infrastructure Distribution

Ground relay stations constitute the second processing tier of the ENMA and serve as the primary uplink and downlink interface between user-embedded nanobot clusters and the Tier 3 orbital network. Within Haven Island, relay stations are spaced no more than 1.4 kilometers apart to ensure continuous Tier 2 coverage across all populated and transit-accessible areas of the Special Economic Zone. Current Haven relay density exceeds this minimum specification in high-traffic sectors; the North Haven industrial corridor and East Haven research precincts maintain relay spacing of approximately 0.8 kilometers due to elevated user density and processing throughput requirements.

Relay stations are embedded in Haven's existing utility and transit infrastructure wherever feasible, with above-ground antennae concealed within standard municipal fixtures. Precise station locations are classified separately under TEC Infrastructure Reference TEC-INF-RLY-077 (L2).

### 4.2 Tier 2 Processing Functions

Tier 2 relay stations handle mid-latency processing tasks that exceed local cluster computational capacity but do not require the persistent identity and coherence management functions performed at Tier 3. Primary Tier 2 functions include:

- Aggregation and routing of intra-cluster state data to Tier 3
- Processing of medium-complexity environmental overlay elements (static and semi-static scene geometry, persistent object anchoring)
- Relay-assisted cluster stabilization (per Appendix B)
- Anonymized behavioral pattern telemetry (logging parameters defined in TEC-DATA-TEL-011, L2)
- Failover management and Tier 1 continuity support during CDE events

Tier 2 stations do not retain user identity data beyond the duration of an active session handoff. Identity persistence is exclusively a Tier 3 function (Section 5.0).

## 5.0 Tier 3: CYPHER Satellite Uplink and Orbital Processing

### 5.1 Satellite Network Integration

Tier 3 processing is performed across the CYPHER satellite network, the distributed orbital infrastructure that provides the Edge neural mesh with its highest-order computational resources. Uplink and downlink between Tier 2 relay stations and the CYPHER orbital array is managed through dedicated frequency allocations maintained under a joint technical agreement between TEC and the CYPHER network operations framework. Uplink handoff from Tier 2 occurs on a continuous basis under nominal conditions; no user action is required to initiate or maintain Tier 3 connectivity.

### 5.2 Tier 3 Processing Functions

Tier 3 satellite processing handles the functions that require persistent, cross-session, and cross-user data coherence. These functions cannot be performed at lower tiers due to their dependence on a continuously maintained global state. Tier 3 functions include:

- **Identity persistence:** Maintenance of the user's continuous Edge identity record across sessions, devices, and geographic locations within ENMA coverage zones
- **Avatar rendering coherence:** Ensuring that a user's avatar presentation — including all configured customization parameters — remains consistent as rendered by all other users' local clusters observing that avatar simultaneously
- **Cross-user interaction synchronization:** Coordination of shared-space interactions between multiple Edge users, including joint object manipulation, synchronized environmental events, and multi-user overlay states, such that all participants experience a coherent and temporally consistent interaction record

These three functions form the core of Tier 3's operational mandate and are the primary justification for the CYPHER network's role in ENMA. The latency tolerance for Tier 3 functions is substantially higher than Tier 1 — acceptable Tier 3 round-trip latency for identity and coherence operations is specified at under 340 milliseconds under standard network load, with degraded-mode tolerances documented in Appendix D.

## 6.0 Visual Cortex Integration and Tier 1 Latency Parameters

Visual cortex integration is the primary perceptual function of the Edge neural mesh and represents the most latency-sensitive operation within the ENMA. The rendering of visual overlay content — including avatar skins, environmental augmentation layers, navigational and informational annotations, and psychoactive affect modulation signals — is handled exclusively at Tier 1, using the computational resources of the user's local nanobot cluster.

Tier 1 processing handles latency-critical visual overlay rendering with a target latency of under 8 milliseconds from stimulus event to rendered output within the user's visual field. This threshold was established through perceptual research conducted during the 70–71 PCE development period and reflects the maximum delay at which visual overlay elements remain perceptually synchronous with real-world visual input for the median user. Users with atypically high neural processing speeds — a characteristic sometimes associated with certain Somatic Augmentation profiles — may perceive artifacts at latencies above 5 milliseconds; this subpopulation is documented in TEC-PERC-002 (L3).

Failure to achieve the 8-millisecond threshold results in perceptual desynchronization events (PDEs), which manifest to the user as visual flicker, overlay drift, or in severe cases, the characteristic disorientation associated with prolonged CDE sequences. PDE incidence is monitored continuously at Tier 2 and reported to Tier 3 for longitudinal user wellness tracking.

## 7.0 Interoperability Constraints and Known Limitations

This section notes current known architectural constraints relevant to systems engineering and infrastructure planning teams.

- ENMA coverage is presently confined to Haven Island and installations where licensed relay infrastructure has been deployed. Extension territories must replicate Haven relay density specifications (Section 4.1) to meet Tier 2 coverage guarantees.
- Tier 3 availability is contingent on CYPHER network operational status. CYPHER network degradation events and their impact on Tier 3 function availability are addressed in the ENMA Contingency Operations Specification (TEC-ARCH-CON-009, L2).
- Nanobot cluster density specifications (Section 3.1) are subject to revision as updated body-mass indexing data becomes available from ongoing deployment monitoring.
