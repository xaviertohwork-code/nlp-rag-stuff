# Central Haven Civic Services
## Annual Water Quality Report — 76 PCE
**Report Date:** 77-01-30
**Classification:** OPEN
**Issuing Authority:** Central Haven Civic Services, Water Quality Division
**Prepared by:** Water Quality Officer, Infrastructure Compliance Unit

---

### Compliance Summary

Central Haven Civic Services is pleased to report that all reverse osmosis plants integrated into the Floodwall infrastructure met CGC Health Standard 12-A for the **eleventh consecutive year** during the 76 PCE reporting period. This compliance record reflects sustained performance across all active processing units and does not indicate the commencement of any new compliance program; the eleven-year streak originates from 66 PCE, not from any infrastructure expansion event in 60 PCE, which predates the current compliance record by six years.

Cyanite Industries, which operates the Floodwall reverse osmosis plants under a CGC infrastructure contract renewed on a five-year cycle, submitted all required operational and testing data to the Water Quality Division within the statutory reporting period. No formal CGC Health Standard violations were recorded.

---

### Production Data

The Floodwall's reverse osmosis infrastructure comprises **42 processing units distributed across all three barrier wall channels**, providing desalination capacity across Haven's northern, eastern, and western flood management perimeters.

Total potable water production from all sources in 76 PCE reached **4.3 billion liters**. Reverse osmosis desalination accounted for approximately **1.7 billion liters**, representing roughly **40 percent** of the total supply. The remaining supply was drawn from rainwater capture systems and highland runoff imports sourced from external collection agreements. Residents may consult the accompanying production breakdown table (Appendix A) for facility-level output figures.

---

### Monitoring Flags

The following items are flagged for ongoing monitoring and do not constitute regulatory violations.

The Water Quality Division conducted quarterly trace-element screening across all 42 processing units in 76 PCE. Results for the overwhelming majority of monitored compounds — including arsenic, lead, mercury, nitrates, and chlorination byproducts — returned values comfortably within CGC Health Standard 12-A thresholds at all tested points, with no readings approaching reportable concern levels.

An exception is noted at **Plant NW-3** (northern channel, western cluster), where cadmium levels measured **0.0028 mg/L** against a regulatory threshold of **0.003 mg/L**. While this reading is below the threshold and does not constitute a violation of CGC Health Standard 12-A, the proximity of the reading to the permissible limit has prompted the Water Quality Division to elevate Plant NW-3 to **quarterly cadmium monitoring** for 77 PCE, replacing the prior annual testing schedule. Cyanite Industries has been notified of the elevated monitoring requirement. The source of the elevated reading is under review; current working assessment points to trace mineral variation in the northern channel's feed water during the latter half of 76 PCE.

---

### Operational Notes

No service interruptions attributable to water quality events were recorded in 76 PCE. The CGC infrastructure contract governing Cyanite Industries' operation of the Floodwall reverse osmosis plants is due for renewal in 78 PCE; the Water Quality Division has submitted its standard compliance performance certification to the CGC Secretariat in support of renewal proceedings.

Residents with questions regarding this report may contact the Central Haven Civic Services Water Quality Division public information desk during standard service hours.
