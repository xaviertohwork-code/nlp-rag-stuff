# Remembering the Night Haven Shook: Oral Histories from the 25th Anniversary Gala

**Renhwa Media | Long-Form** | 68-06-25

*By Kwan Yin-Lau, Staff Correspondent*

---

*Editor's Note: The four individuals quoted in this article provided their accounts on the condition of full anonymity. Their identities have been verified by Renhwa Media's editorial board and are known to this journalist alone. Given the events described herein — and the continued power wielded by the organisations responsible for them — we consider their request for anonymity not only reasonable but necessary. We publish their words as close to verbatim as editorial clarity permits.*

---

Forty years ago tonight, the Clairos Governance Council gathered in Haven to celebrate itself. The occasion was the 25th anniversary of the CGC's founding — a gala, by all accounts, of considerable opulence: formal dress, speeches prepared, crystal glasses raised to an organisation that had, in the span of a single generation, reordered the world. What followed has never been fully accounted for in any official record. Tonight, four people who were there speak about what they saw.

---

**"It went from champagne to carnage in less than two minutes."**

The first witness had attended in a logistical capacity, working the event floor. What they remember most is the dissonance — the abrupt collision of celebration and violence. "There was music still playing when the first unit came through the east corridor," they told this journalist. "Not music that had been stopped — music that continued for nearly a minute after, because whoever was running the sound hadn't been told. I think that is what haunts me most. They were destroying Wampa's people and their offices while something celebratory was still playing overhead." The assault on Wampa Robotics personnel and facilities, this witness insists, did not unfold as a response to any visible provocation. "It was coordinated. There was nothing accidental or reactive about it. These were people acting on a plan."

---

**"He was there. And then he wasn't."**

A second witness, who had seen Dr. Hiran Voss earlier in the evening at the reception, describes watching what they believe was his death — or something very close to it. "He was speaking to a small group near the far end of the hall when the chaos began. I lost sight of him in the confusion. Later — much later — people told me he had been killed. I cannot tell you I saw the moment itself. I can tell you I looked for him afterward and he was not there, that no one I spoke to saw him leave, and that no body was ever produced for public record. There was no independent verification, nothing that could be examined or contested. He simply was gone, and the official account said what it said, and that was the end of it."

---

**"They were not hiding who they were."**

The third account is perhaps the most forensically precise. This witness — who had positioned themselves near the building's central transit corridor during the confusion — describes watching the assault teams pass within meters of where they were sheltering. "They were not in unmarked gear," the witness says flatly. "I could read the insignia. Phyrexis. Cyanite. ONE. Multiple megacorporations, acting in concert, in the same corridor, coordinating with each other. I am a careful person. I did not make assumptions that night — I looked, and I read what I saw. Those were not ambiguous symbols. There was more than one megacorporation represented in that hallway, and they were moving together."

---

**"The silence after was its own kind of violence."**

The fourth witness arrived at the aftermath — they had been evacuated early and returned to the building's outer precincts the following morning. "What I remember is how quickly the official language arrived," they say. "By morning there were already people with prepared statements using words like 'security operation' and 'controlled circumstances.' Wampa was gone — not damaged, not diminished: *gone*, as a functioning entity, in the span of hours. And the CGC was already constructing the story it wanted told." This witness describes the days that followed as a kind of collective disorientation among people who had been present. "You had seen a thing, and you were being told the thing you saw was something else. Most people, eventually, accepted the correction. What else could they do?"

---

Forty years is a long time. The CGC that assembled that night to congratulate itself has since reshuffled, expanded, contracted, and reshaped Haven around its own interests with a thoroughness that its founders might have found satisfying. Wampa Robotics has not been reconstituted. Dr. Voss's writings circulate still — in dissident archives, in the reading groups of We The Opted, in the careful private collections of people who remember what it meant when an engineer-philosopher built something and the rest of the room decided it was easier to destroy him than to compete with him.

Renhwa Media covered these events at the time. We were present. We published what we could verify. In the years that followed, our ability to verify became constrained in ways our readers will understand without elaboration. Tonight, on the 40th anniversary of the night Haven shook, we offer these four voices as what they are: testimony that the official record has never had to answer to.

That, too, is a kind of verdict.
