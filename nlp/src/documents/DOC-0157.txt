# CGC SECRETARIAT POLICY BRIEF
## Assessment of Resolution 77-015 Compliance Readiness Across Haven Sectors

**CLASSIFICATION: RESTRICTED**
**Document Reference:** CGC-ORA-77-0157
**Date:** 77-04-22
**Prepared by:** Director Yelena Carstens, CGC Office of Regulatory Affairs
**Distribution:** CGC Member Representatives (All), CGC Secretariat Senior Staff

---

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This brief presents a sector-by-sector assessment of compliance readiness with Resolution 77-015 (Inter-Sector Transit Monitoring Requirements) as of the most recent audit date. As of 77-04-15, **19 of 42 designated crossing points** across all Haven sectors have had monitoring equipment successfully installed and verified as operational. This figure represents 45.2% of required installations, against a compliance deadline of 77-06-01. Given current installation rates and identified impediments detailed herein, the Office of Regulatory Affairs assesses that full compliance by the original deadline is not achievable. Director Carstens' recommendation regarding deadline revision is included in Section VII.

### SECTION I — NORTH HAVEN (Cyanite Industries Sector)

North Haven presents the only case of full compliance within the current assessment period. All **11 of 11 designated crossing points** are operational as of 77-04-15, with monitoring hardware installed, system integration tests completed, and staffing allocations confirmed at each checkpoint. Cyanite's existing industrial security infrastructure in the sector appears to have provided a significant procurement and logistics advantage; several monitoring installations were completed ahead of the originally submitted installation schedule.

North Haven's compliance documentation is complete and on file with the CGC Secretariat. No further action is required for this sector pending the broader system interoperability review noted in Section VI.

---

### SECTION II — EAST HAVEN (Genesis Labs Sector)

East Haven has **zero monitoring equipment installed** across all 6 designated crossing points as of 77-04-15. Genesis Labs has formally notified the CGC Secretariat that it will not proceed with installation pending the resolution of active legal challenges to the validity of Resolution 77-015 itself. Genesis Labs' filing, received 77-03-08, contends that the Resolution's data-retention provisions for transit monitoring records conflict with existing CGC privacy framework commitments applicable to medical and biometric data processed within East Haven, where Genesis's Somatic Clinic operations are concentrated.

The legal challenge is currently under review by the CGC Legal Affairs Panel. No installation timeline has been offered by Genesis Labs contingent on challenge failure.

---

### SECTION III — WEST HAVEN (ONE Network Enterprises Sector)

West Haven is the sector closest to full compliance among those that have begun installation. **6 of 9 designated crossing points** are operational as of 77-04-15. ONE Network Enterprises has provided a confirmed installation schedule for the remaining 3 crossing points, with completion expected no later than **77-05-10**. Supply logistics and contractor scheduling were cited as contributing to the short delay on those three installations.

Assuming the 77-05-10 target is met, West Haven will achieve full sectoral compliance well in advance of the original deadline, though integration with other sectors' monitoring systems will depend on interoperability progress described in Section VI.

---

### SECTION IV — SOUTH HAVEN (Phyrexis Group Sector)

South Haven has **2 of 8 designated crossing points** operational as of 77-04-15, representing the lowest installation rate among the three megacorp-administered sectors that have begun installation work. Phyrexis Group has attributed the shortfall to **supply chain delays affecting monitoring hardware procurement**, specifically citing extended lead times on sensor array components through its contracted equipment suppliers. Phyrexis has not provided a revised completion schedule to the Secretariat as of the date of this brief.

The Office of Regulatory Affairs notes that the absence of a revised schedule creates significant forecasting uncertainty for overall Haven compliance planning. Phyrexis is requested to submit an updated installation timeline to the Secretariat no later than 77-05-01.

---

### SECTION V — CENTRAL HAVEN (CGC Direct Administration)

Central Haven, administered directly by the CGC Secretariat rather than any individual megacorporation, has **0 of 8 designated crossing points** operational as of 77-04-15. The reason for non-compliance in this sector is **budget shortfalls within the CGC Secretariat's operational infrastructure allocation** for 77 PCE, not vendor contract disputes or legal challenges as may be assumed by analogy with other sectors. Funding approved for Central Haven monitoring installation at the time of Resolution 77-015's passage was subsequently reduced during CGC budget reconciliation in 77-01, leaving insufficient capital to proceed with procurement.

The Office of Regulatory Affairs notes that Central Haven is the sole sector for which the compliance gap is attributable directly to CGC-level administrative failure rather than corporate action or external cause.

---

### SECTION VI — INTEROPERABILITY CONCERNS

Even for sectors that achieve installation targets, a system-level concern remains unresolved: the monitoring hardware deployed by Cyanite Industries (North Haven), ONE Network Enterprises (West Haven), and Phyrexis Group (South Haven) are procured through different vendor relationships and operate on distinct data formatting standards. Cross-sector transit monitoring — the stated operational purpose of Resolution 77-015 — requires that data generated at one sector's crossing points be readable and flaggable in adjacent sectors' monitoring systems. As of this brief, no unified data protocol has been agreed among the participating megacorporations. This interoperability gap affects all installed equipment and must be resolved regardless of when remaining installations are completed.

---

### SECTION VII — RECOMMENDATION

Given the combination of installation shortfalls, the unresolved East Haven legal challenge, the Central Haven funding gap, and the outstanding interoperability protocol issue, the Office of Regulatory Affairs concludes that full operational compliance with Resolution 77-015 by the current deadline of 77-06-01 is not achievable.

**Director Carstens recommends that the CGC formally extend the compliance deadline by 90 days, to 77-09-01**, and that the extension motion be accompanied by a mandatory interoperability standards working group convening within 30 days of the motion's passage. A separate funding supplemental request for Central Haven infrastructure is being prepared under separate cover.
