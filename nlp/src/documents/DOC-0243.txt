# Sim Jiahong Absent from Latest CGC Assembly; TEC Sends Deputy

**RENHWA MEDIA — HAVEN POLITICAL DESK**
*77-04-28 | OPEN*

---

The Edge Corporation's Chief Executive Officer Sim Jiahong was again absent from the CGC General Assembly on 77-04-25, with Chief Operating Officer Devika Oranyan stepping in to represent the megacorporation at the session. Oranyan, who has emerged in recent months as TEC's de facto public face at CGC proceedings, handled the session with characteristic poise, navigating a busy agenda that included preliminary discussions on Haven port-district logistics reform and a procedural review of BCO enforcement reporting standards.

The absence was the talk of the assembly floor before the session had even formally convened.

---

## Attendance Record

Sim last appeared in person at a CGC General Assembly in the second quarter of 76 PCE — a gap that has grown long enough that it is no longer possible to dismiss as scheduling inconvenience. At the 77 PCE annual opening session in January, he was present only by holographic proxy, a choice that drew quiet comment at the time. The Q1 General Assembly on 77-04-25 marks the third consecutive CGC assembly session he has failed to attend in person since that 76 PCE Q2 appearance, continuing a pattern that is attracting increasing attention from the other member corporations and from observers of CGC affairs.

Whether his holographic appearance in January constitutes meaningful participation, or merely a flag planted to forestall harder questions, is a matter on which opinion within the assembly hall appears divided.

---

## 'Procedural Complications'

The muted diplomatic frustration that has been building around Sim's absences found voice, carefully unnamed, in remarks shared with this correspondent after the session closed.

"Mr. Sim's absence creates procedural complications," one CGC official told Renhwa Media, declining to be identified by name or corporate affiliation. The official offered no further elaboration, but the remark captures a sentiment that has become increasingly common in the corridors outside the assembly chamber — that a megacorporation CEO who cannot or will not appear in person is a different kind of interlocutor than one who can be pinned down across a table.

Oranyan, for her part, gave no indication that she considered herself anything other than TEC's full representative for the day's business. Measured, thorough, and notably more diplomatic in register than Sim tends to be when he does appear, she has developed a reputation in CGC circles as someone who smooths the edges that Sim occasionally sharpens deliberately.

---

## Speculation and Denial

In the absence of any official explanation from TEC for Sim's continued non-attendance, speculation has filled the vacuum. The most dramatic theory circulating in Haven's political and financial circles holds that the Old 3 Firms — Phyrexis Group, Cyanite Industries, and ONE Network Enterprises — may have made attempts on Sim's life, and that his withdrawal from public settings reflects security concerns rather than mere preference.

TEC has flatly denied the assassination rumors. No TEC executive confirmed or lent credence to the claims at the 77-04-25 assembly or in any subsequent communication to this correspondent. The denial is consistent with TEC's posture on previous iterations of the same rumor, which have circulated in various forms since Sim's absences began to accumulate.

Whether the denial is believed is another matter. The Old 3 Firms, for their part, have not commented.

---

What is driving Sim Jiahong's reclusiveness — security, strategy, health, or something else entirely — remains, as of this writing, an open question. What is not in doubt is that Devika Oranyan is becoming very good at answering it on his behalf.

*— Renhwa Media Political Correspondent*
