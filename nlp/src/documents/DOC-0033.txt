## SECTION 2: BIOGRAPHICAL OVERVIEW

Subject is a Sharpsea Bloc native with formative professional experience in the regional maritime logistics sector. Her career trajectory is notably atypical for a media-sector CEO: she has no substantive background in entertainment, content production, or media operations prior to her Renhwa appointment. Her selection by Renhwa's board is assessed as deliberate — a turnaround and re-accession mandate requiring operational and political skills that no internally available candidate possessed.

Subject carries the instincts of a logistics operator into a content-industry role. She is not known for ideological commitments; her public statements are consistently instrumentalist in character, oriented toward institutional outcomes rather than principles. From Cyanite's perspective, this makes her less predictable, not more. She will not be constrained by entertainment-industry conventions.

## SECTION 3: CAREER HISTORY

**2099–2113 CE — Founder and Chief Executive, Tidemark Logistics (Sharpsea Bloc)**
Subject founded Tidemark Logistics, a mid-sized regional shipping firm operating within the Sharpsea Bloc, and ran the company for 14 years. The firm operated primarily across outer Bloc island territories, with route exposure to both Zonnon and Kashikari-adjacent lanes. In 2113 CE, Tidemark was acquired by a Kashikari family consortium. Terms of the acquisition have not been disclosed publicly; subject departed the firm upon completion of the transaction.

**2113–2115 CE — Consulting (Haven)**
Following the Tidemark sale, Park relocated to Haven. She held two interim consulting engagements in succession — neither role has been publicly identified by title or client. Duration of each assignment is unknown; combined tenure in consulting is estimated at approximately two years.

**2115 CE — Recruited to Renhwa Board; Named CEO**
Park was recruited to Renhwa Media's board of directors following her consulting period, subsequently named CEO in 2115 CE. Her appointment followed a period of significant internal panic at Renhwa over declining market share. No formal search process has been publicly confirmed.

## SECTION 4: MANAGEMENT STYLE ASSESSMENT

Subject is described by former Tidemark personnel as demanding, methodical, and intolerant of ambiguity in reporting lines. She is press-shy by the standards of Haven's corporate culture — her public appearances are infrequent and tightly controlled. Known to make decisions quickly once she assesses that sufficient information is available, but will delay action substantially if she judges the information environment inadequate.

No reported history of delegation failures or internal mutinies. Regarded as operationally capable. Her relative unfamiliarity with Haven's corporate social infrastructure — the Quicksilver circuit, the Old 3 Firm relationship networks — may constrain her short-term influence, though this is unlikely to persist.

## SECTION 6: STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT

**Primary Objective:** CGC readmission for Renhwa Media. *Confidence: High.*

Subject was appointed to execute a turnaround, and the only turnaround outcome that satisfies Renhwa's board is a return to the CGC. All other observable strategic decisions — Haven relocation, board recruitment, public positioning — are consistent with this objective. This assessment is not in question.

**Cyanite-Specific Competitive Exposure:** Subject's 14-year history operating Tidemark Logistics gives her detailed operational knowledge of Kashikari-controlled shipping lanes. These lanes are central to Cyanite's raw material transit from extraction sites to Sarento and Haven. A Renhwa CEO with this background is a qualitatively different adversary than a conventional entertainment-industry figure. She understands our logistics dependencies. She understands which disruptions cost us most. This exposure is material and should be factored into any Cyanite response to Renhwa's re-accession efforts.

Her Bloc contacts, if mobilized, could further complicate Cyanite's regional commercial relationships in ways that a Haven-native CEO could not engineer.

The CGC's voting dynamics would shift materially against Cyanite's interests. Director-General Oyelaran is advised to treat this scenario as a contingency warranting pre-emptive preparation regardless of confidence rating.
