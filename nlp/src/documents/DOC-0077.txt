# ONE NETWORK ENTERPRISES
## INTERNAL MEMORANDUM

**CLASSIFICATION: CONFIDENTIAL**
**Document Reference:** ONE-GA-76-1008-C
**Date:** 76-10-08 PCE
**To:** Office of the CEO; Chief Legal Officer; Chief Commercial Officer
**From:** Government Affairs Directorate
**Subject:** TEC Commercial Encroachment in West Haven Port Zone — Situation Assessment and Recommended Response

---

### 1. SITUATION SUMMARY

This memorandum is issued to advise senior leadership on an escalating encroachment situation requiring prompt strategic attention.

Over the past fourteen months, The Edge Corporation (TEC) has expanded its commercial operations — specifically Edge-adjacent retail infrastructure, nanobot seeding nodes embedded in commercial lease arrangements, and TEC-affiliated vendor contracting — into **three commercial blocks directly adjacent to West Haven's primary port terminals**. The affected blocks are designated in CGC administrative records as West Haven Commercial Zones 7, 8, and 11 (WH-CZ7, WH-CZ8, and WH-CZ11). These zones sit at the operational fringe of ONE's primary commercial shipping terminal cluster and passenger ferry docks, the core commercial and logistics infrastructure ONE has operated from West Haven since the earliest years of the Haven Island Special Economic Zone.

TEC's presence in these blocks is not incidental. The deployment pattern — retail frontage providing TEC brand visibility, service nodes generating nanobot saturation in high-traffic transit corridors, and exclusive vendor agreements crowding out ONE-preferred port logistics suppliers — is consistent with deliberate commercial displacement rather than organic growth.

---

### 2. BACKGROUND: THE SECTOR GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

The sector allocation system governing Haven's operational geography was established under **CGC Resolution 52-007**, adopted in 52 PCE (2094 CE), as the original framework for assigning each member corporation a primary operational sector within Haven Island. Under that framework, West Haven was designated as ONE Network Enterprises' primary sector, consistent with our pre-existing port infrastructure investment and logistics mandate.

Government Affairs must advise leadership clearly on one material constraint: **the sector allocation borders are not legally binding**. Resolution 52-007 does not confer exclusive territorial title, zoning restrictions enforceable against other CGC members, or any mechanism preventing another corporation from establishing a commercial presence within another member's designated sector. The sector system is an informal administrative framework, not a legally cognizable property arrangement. There are no hard borders between sectors, and Havenites and corporate entities alike move and operate freely across them.

This limitation is significant and shapes the available response options discussed below.

---

### 3. FINANCIAL EXPOSURE

Despite the above, the commercial consequences of unchecked TEC encroachment are material. ONE's internal commercial modeling, prepared by the Port Revenue Analytics division in 76-09 PCE, estimates that the port-adjacent commercial revenue stream — encompassing vendor concessions, logistics service contracts, passenger terminal commercial leases, and freight-adjacent retail operations in the West Haven port zone — generates approximately **132 billion Phi Credits annually**.

The encroachment in WH-CZ7, WH-CZ8, and WH-CZ11 presently threatens a portion of this revenue through vendor displacement and reduced foot-traffic capture. If TEC's expansion continues at current pace and is permitted to consolidate into adjacent zones, Government Affairs assesses that the full 132 billion Phi Credit revenue base should be considered at risk within a three-year horizon.

---

### 4. STRATEGIC OPTIONS AND RECOMMENDED ACTION

Government Affairs has evaluated three response postures: informal diplomatic engagement with TEC's Government Relations office; unilateral commercial countermeasures (accelerated lease acquisition in contested blocks); and formal CGC grievance filing.

We recommend **filing a formal CGC grievance under Resolution 52-007's sector encroachment provisions**. While we have set out above that the sector allocation framework does not establish legally binding borders — and TEC's legal team will certainly exploit this in any proceeding — Resolution 52-007 nonetheless provides the most credible procedural mechanism available to ONE for registering this dispute at the CGC level and building a formal record. A filed grievance compels TEC to respond through CGC channels, creates documentation for any subsequent escalation, and signals to other CGC members (notably Phyrexis, which has its own interest in stable sector arrangements) that ONE will not absorb encroachment without response.

Informal engagement is assessed as unlikely to produce durable results given TEC's demonstrated expansion posture. Unilateral commercial countermeasures carry litigation risk and, if perceived as aggressive, could disadvantage ONE in the grievance proceeding.

The Government Affairs Directorate requests authorization to prepare and file the grievance by 76-11-15 PCE.
