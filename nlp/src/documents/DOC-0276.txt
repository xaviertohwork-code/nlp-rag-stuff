# CORDIAL ENTENTE
## Annotated Signatory Record and Ratification History

**Document Reference:** CGC-LEGAL-0071-ENTENTE
**Classification:** OPEN
**Issuing Authority:** CGC Administrative Secretariat
**Date of Instrument:** 29-09-22 PCE (2071-09-22 CE)
**Document Status:** Ratified and in Force

---

## PREAMBLE

WHEREAS the member corporations of the Clairos Governance Council convened in the twenty-ninth year of the Post-Cascade Era, having collectively witnessed and participated in the events of 28 PCE, which resulted in the cessation of operations of a former member corporation and the irreversible transformation of the Council's composition;

WHEREAS the coordination demonstrated in those events, while undertaken in the name of collective security, has given each remaining member corporation cause to reflect upon the vulnerability of the current cooperative framework and the dangers of unchecked unilateral action pursued under the cover of collective mandate;

WHEREAS the remaining member corporations, mindful of the destabilizing consequences that open military conflict between megacorporations would visit upon Haven, upon the residents dependent upon the Council's governance, and upon the continued viability of the commercial civilization each corporation has an interest in preserving;

WHEREAS it is the shared judgment of the undersigned that a formal instrument prohibiting open warfare between member corporations serves the long-term commercial and institutional interests of all parties, notwithstanding and indeed because of the circumstances that necessitate its creation;

NOW THEREFORE the undersigned, being the full and complete membership of the Clairos Governance Council as constituted on this date, do hereby enter into the following Cordial Entente, effective as of the date of the final signatory's execution below.

---

## ARTICLE I — PROHIBITION OF OPEN MILITARY CONFLICT

**Section 1.1.** No signatory corporation shall direct its standing military forces, contracted military forces, or formally commissioned security apparatus to engage in acts of open warfare against any other signatory corporation, its personnel, its formally designated infrastructure, or its governing bodies.

**Section 1.2.** "Open warfare" is defined, for purposes of this instrument, as any sustained, publicly attributable, and formally ordered military campaign conducted under the operational authority of a signatory corporation's command structure against another signatory corporation's command structure.

**Section 1.3.** This prohibition is absolute with respect to the conduct defined in Sections 1.1 and 1.2 and admits of no exception save those enumerated in Articles IV and V below.

---

## ARTICLE II — DEFINITIONS

**Section 2.1.** "Signatory Corporation" refers to any megacorporation that has executed this instrument and whose execution has been recorded in the Signatory Record appended hereto.

**Section 2.2.** "Standing Military Forces" refers to uniformed personnel under direct corporate employment whose primary designated function is armed conflict or armed deterrence.

**Section 2.3.** "Formally Commissioned Security Apparatus" refers to security units operating under written executive authorization of the relevant signatory's executive leadership.

**Section 2.4.** "Open Warfare" shall be understood in strict accordance with the definition provided in Article I, Section 1.2, and shall not be construed to encompass activities falling outside that definition by reason of their covert character, indirect attribution, or commercial framing.

---

## ARTICLE III — SCOPE OF APPLICATION

**Section 3.1.** This instrument applies to all signatory corporations and their wholly owned subsidiaries operating under the signatory's corporate charter.

**Section 3.2.** This instrument applies within the Haven Island Special Economic Zone, within all territories in which a signatory corporation maintains formal operational presence, and upon the high seas and air corridors where signatory corporations maintain logistical operations.

**Section 3.3.** This instrument applies to operations conducted under the signatory's own authority. It does not, by its terms, govern the conduct of third parties whose actions a signatory may have occasion to influence, fund, or coordinate with, except where such coordination rises to the level of formal military command as defined in Article I.

---

## ARTICLE IV — SCOPE LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUDED ACTIVITIES

**Section 4.1.** Nothing in this instrument shall be construed to prohibit, restrict, or otherwise regulate the following categories of activity, which the signatories expressly reserve as within the ordinary conduct of commercial enterprise:

**(a)** Economic competition of any kind, including predatory pricing, market foreclosure, acquisition of competitor assets or personnel, disruption of commercial operations through lawful commercial means, and the use of financial instruments to constrain the commercial viability of a competing signatory corporation;

**(b)** Intelligence collection, counterintelligence operations, surveillance, signals monitoring, and the gathering, analysis, or use of information pertaining to a signatory corporation's operations, personnel, or strategic intentions, provided such activities do not rise to the level of open warfare as defined in Article I;

**(c)** Support — whether financial, material, logistical, or advisory — provided to third parties not themselves bound by this instrument, including governments, non-state actors, commercial enterprises, advocacy organizations, and informal civic groupings, provided that such support is not accompanied by a formal command relationship that would constitute a commissioned security operation under Article I;

**(d)** Security operations conducted within a signatory corporation's own territory or designated operational zone for the purpose of protecting the signatory's personnel, assets, and infrastructure from third-party threats;

**(e)** Commercial litigation, regulatory challenge, lobbying, public advocacy, media relations, and any other activities conducted through the formal mechanisms of the CGC or applicable legal instruments.

**Section 4.2.** The exclusions enumerated in Section 4.1 are illustrative and not exhaustive. The signatories acknowledge that the prohibition of Article I is intentionally narrow and that the categories of activity reserved in this Article are broad, reflecting the signatories' collective judgment that commercial competition — including its adversarial forms — is the appropriate mechanism for resolving disputes between megacorporations.

---

## ARTICLE V — ENFORCEMENT AND NON-ATTRIBUTION EXCEPTION

**Section 5.1.** Enforcement of this instrument is voluntary and reciprocal. No external enforcement body is established. Any signatory alleging a breach shall present its allegation to the CGC in plenary session; the responding signatory shall be afforded full opportunity to rebut.

**Section 5.2.** Operations whose authorship or authorization cannot be formally attributed to a signatory corporation's executive command structure, as demonstrated by contemporaneous documentary evidence, shall not constitute a breach of this instrument regardless of circumstantial inference or analytical conclusion.

**Section 5.3.** Allegations of breach shall be assessed under a standard of direct and manifest evidence. Presumptive, probabilistic, or reputationally sourced attributions shall not satisfy this standard.

**Section 5.4.** The CGC may, by unanimous vote of all signatories excluding the alleged breaching party, issue a formal finding of breach. No sanction mechanism is established by this instrument; consequences of a breach finding shall be determined by the remaining signatories on a case-by-case basis.

---

## ARTICLE VI — AMENDMENT

**Section 6.1.** This instrument may be amended by unanimous written consent of all then-current signatory corporations, executed in accordance with the same formalities as the original instrument.

**Section 6.2.** No amendment shall take effect until it has been deposited with the CGC Administrative Secretariat and published in the CGC open record.

---

## ARTICLE VII — SUCCESSION AND NEW MEMBERSHIP

**Section 7.1.** This instrument binds each signatory corporation as a legal entity. In the event of a signatory corporation's dissolution, merger, acquisition, or transformation of legal identity, its obligations and rights under this instrument shall be addressed by the remaining signatories in plenary session, and no automatic succession of obligation shall be presumed.

**Section 7.2.** Corporations that accede to CGC membership subsequent to the date of this instrument shall not be deemed bound by its terms solely by virtue of their CGC membership. Whether and upon what terms any future member corporation becomes a party to this instrument shall be a matter for negotiation among the then-current signatories at the time of such accession, and the absence of any express agreement shall not be construed as either inclusion or exclusion.

**Section 7.3.** The obligations of this instrument are personal to the signatories named in the Signatory Record. Nothing in this instrument creates obligations for non-signatory parties.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — EFFECTIVE DATE

**Section 8.1.** This instrument enters into force upon execution by all signatories named in the Signatory Record below, effective as of 29-09-22 PCE (2071-09-22 CE).

## SECRETARIAT NOTE ON POPULAR RECEPTION

The CGC Administrative Secretariat notes, for purposes of the historical record appended to this filing, that public and commercial reception of this instrument upon publication has been markedly divided. Among the megacorporations' commercial partners and dependent populations, the Entente has been welcomed as evidence that the Council's member corporations retain the capacity for institutional self-restraint. Among independent commentators, dissident communities, and the surviving independent press, the instrument's name has been received as ironic in character, its language critiqued as establishing a prohibition narrow enough to permit virtually all forms of commercial, intelligence, and proxy competition between its signatories, and its enforcement mechanism noted as voluntary, self-referential, and functionally unverifiable. The Secretariat records these observations without endorsing them, in accordance with its obligation to provide a complete contemporaneous account of the instrument and its reception.
