# Personal Field Journal
**[Recovered document — Cyanite Industries Site 7-Sarento Outskirts]**
**Classification: GREEN**

---

## 75-11-19

Another 14-hour shift. Feet are shot. Everything smells like sulfur and machine grease and I can't remember the last time I slept a full night without the processing drums waking me up.

Extraction numbers are down again. Foreman Dech says it's the geology shifting east of the primary bore, says we'll compensate by pushing the secondary lines harder. Everybody knows what that means — same quota, more ground to cover, same 14 hours. Not a single extra credit for it.

Speaking of. Payslip came through on the terminal today. 1,200 Phi Credits. Same as every month. I keep thinking it'll look bigger once it arrives. It never does.

---

## 75-11-20

**Equipment Status**

RP-14 is still down. Six days now and still no repair parts from logistics. Six days. Dech filed the request the same afternoon it seized up and we've heard nothing. The unit handles roughly a third of our coarse ore processing throughput so we're redistributing load to RP-11 and RP-16 which were already running hot before any of this. I give it another week before one of those goes too.

I asked Maret in the maintenance bay whether she'd heard anything from Sarento depot. She laughed. Not a funny laugh.

The dust is worse without RP-14 running its filtration cycle properly. My lungs feel like wet gravel by end of shift.

---

## 75-11-21

**Security / General**

Quiet today, which I am not complaining about. Two weeks back there was a scare — movement spotted in the hills to the northeast, maybe a kilometer out, looked like a Canian insurgent scouting party from what the security detail could make out. The whole compound went into lock-and-hold for the better part of an afternoon. Nothing came of it, no shots fired, just the usual waiting around feeling useless. But it puts you on edge. You start noticing every shadow on the ridgeline during morning shift.

Wouldn't mind a walk to clear my head after this week, but you don't go anywhere near the perimeter without shift supervisor approval. Compound rule, written or otherwise. So I sit in the bunk and stare at the ceiling.

RP-14 still showing red on the board. Day six.

---

## 75-11-22

Nothing to report except exhaustion. 14-hour day, same as always. Ate something that was labeled as protein stew. Closed my eyes for a few minutes after and when I opened them it was already time to go back.

Thought about how 1,200 Phi Credits a month works out per hour. I did the math twice. I don't recommend it.

The hills were quiet tonight at least.

— *End of recovered entries*
