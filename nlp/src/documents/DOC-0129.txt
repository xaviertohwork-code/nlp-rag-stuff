# INCIDENT REPORT IR-76-0441
## Sector-Wide Relay Cascade Failure — East Haven
**Authoring Organisation:** TEC Incident Response Division
**Classification:** L3
**Date of Incident:** 76-08-17
**Date of Report:** 76-08-24
**Report Reference:** IR-76-0441

---

## 1. INCIDENT SUMMARY

On 76-08-17, East Haven experienced a sector-wide partial Edge outage resulting from a cascade relay failure that originated at relay stations in the immediate vicinity of Helix Tower. The failure was not related to any external actor or physical infrastructure damage. The cascade was triggered by an internally scheduled firmware update that conflicted with a third-party data exchange protocol operating on shared relay infrastructure. The outage affected approximately 2.3 million Edge users across East Haven for a period exceeding forty minutes. This report documents the full timeline of the event, its impact, root cause determination, and corrective recommendations.

---

## 2. TIMELINE

**14:32** — Incident onset. Twenty-three relay stations clustered in the Helix Tower vicinity simultaneously rebooted. The concurrent reboot of all twenty-three stations is confirmed as the origination point of the cascade failure. Helix Tower proximity is the common factor; no anomaly was detected at Meridian Hub or any other East Haven relay cluster prior to or during the initial failure event.

**14:33–14:36** — Automated cascade propagation. As affected relay stations attempted to re-synchronise with adjacent nodes, load redistribution exceeded threshold tolerances across neighbouring relay groups. Cascading reboots began propagating northward and eastward through the sector mesh.

**14:38** — Emergency fallback rendering activated sector-wide by automated protocols, six minutes after initial failure detection. Fallback rendering provided degraded but partially functional Edge coverage to users whose primary relay nodes were offline.

**14:45** — TEC Incident Response Division formally convened. Initial diagnostic sweep identified firmware build 76.8.14-r2 as the common variable across all rebooted stations.

**15:02** — Root cause confirmed (see Section 4). Manual rollback procedure authorised.

**15:19** — Full service restored across East Haven following the completion of the manual rollback of firmware build 76.8.14-r2. All 547 relay stations in the sector confirmed operational.

---

## 3. IMPACT ASSESSMENT

The cascade ultimately affected **312 of East Haven's 547 relay stations** before propagation was arrested. The remaining 235 stations continued functioning throughout the event, providing partial coverage that the fallback rendering system supplemented.

Approximately **2.3 million Edge users** in East Haven experienced degraded service during the outage window. Reported user impacts ranged in severity:

- **Minor degradation:** Avatar rendering artifacts, latency spikes, intermittent nanobot synchronisation lag
- **Moderate degradation:** Persistent rendering failures, loss of Edge overlay continuity, inability to access commercial content tiers
- **Severe degradation:** Complete Edge dropout, full loss of immersive environment access

The highest density of complete dropout was recorded in the residential and commercial corridors directly adjacent to Helix Tower, consistent with the failure's origin point. Secondary impact concentrations were observed along the sector's northeastern transit corridors as cascade propagation spread.

No physical infrastructure damage was recorded. Fallback rendering, activated at 14:38, successfully mitigated complete service loss for an estimated 60–65% of affected users.

---

## 4. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

Root cause has been definitively identified as a **conflict between firmware build 76.8.14-r2** — a scheduled TEC maintenance update deployed to East Haven relay stations on the morning of 76-08-17 — **and a Genesis Labs Somatic Clinic data exchange protocol running concurrently on shared relay infrastructure**.

The Genesis Labs protocol, used to synchronise patient biometric and Somatic treatment telemetry between the East Haven Somatic Clinic complex and Genesis Labs' primary research facilities, operates on relay bandwidth channels that overlap with the handshake verification sequence introduced in build 76.8.14-r2. When the firmware update initialised its verification sweep, the Genesis Labs protocol's data exchange packets were interpreted as malformed authentication requests. The relay stations' fault-response logic triggered a protective reboot cycle.

Because the Helix Tower relay cluster serves as the primary routing hub for the Genesis Labs protocol in East Haven, all relay stations actively handling Genesis Labs data exchange packets at 14:32 rebooted simultaneously — twenty-three stations in total. This simultaneous reboot, rather than a staggered failure, is the proximate cause of the cascade's severity.

To be unambiguous: the conflict arose because a third-party protocol — operated by Genesis Labs on infrastructure shared under the standard East Haven co-tenancy arrangement — was not disclosed to TEC's firmware engineering team as part of the pre-deployment compatibility review. Build 76.8.14-r2 passed internal compatibility testing because the Genesis Labs protocol was not included in the test environment. This is a process failure with direct external contributing factors.

---

## 5. RESOLUTION

TEC Incident Response initiated a manual rollback of firmware build 76.8.14-r2 across all East Haven relay stations following root cause confirmation at 15:02. Full service was restored by **15:19**. The rollback procedure reverted all affected stations to build 76.7.29-r1, which has no known conflict with the Genesis Labs Somatic Clinic data exchange protocol. Build 76.8.14-r2 has been flagged as suspended pending protocol conflict remediation.

Genesis Labs has been formally notified of the protocol conflict under the terms of the East Haven shared infrastructure agreement.

---

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS

**R-1 — Mandatory third-party protocol disclosure prior to firmware deployment.** All entities operating data exchange protocols on shared TEC relay infrastructure must provide full protocol specifications to TEC's firmware engineering team no fewer than fourteen days prior to any scheduled firmware update. Non-disclosure should be treated as a breach of co-tenancy terms.

**R-2 — Infrastructure segmentation for high-density third-party protocol zones.** The Helix Tower relay cluster should be evaluated for physical segmentation from the broader East Haven mesh, such that a simultaneous reboot event affecting stations serving a single co-tenancy operator cannot propagate into the general relay network. This segmentation architecture should be assessed for implementation at all sector hubs where a single third-party operator accounts for more than 15% of relay bandwidth utilisation.

**R-3 — Pre-deployment compatibility testing environment to include all active co-tenancy protocols.** The gap identified in this incident — the absence of Genesis Labs' protocol from the test environment — must be treated as a systemic deficiency, not an isolated oversight. The compatibility testing regime should be updated accordingly before build 76.8.14-r2 or any successor build is redeployed.

**R-4 — Review of fallback rendering activation threshold.** Fallback rendering was activated at 14:38, six minutes after initial failure detection. While this response was within standard parameters, Incident Response Division recommends reviewing whether the activation threshold can be reduced to three minutes for cascade events originating in high-density residential sectors.
