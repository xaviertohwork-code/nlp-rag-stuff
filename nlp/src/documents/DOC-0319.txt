# THE EDGE CORPORATION
## INTERNAL MEMORANDUM

**CLASSIFICATION: L3 — AUTHORIZED ONLY**
**TO:** Senior Leadership, Deployment Operations Division
**FROM:** Deployment Operations, Edge Network Infrastructure
**DATE:** 77-02-05
**RE:** Edge Nanobot Deployment Metrics — 76 PCE Annual Summary

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The 76 PCE deployment cycle represents another strong year of Edge network growth across Haven. Total active users closed the year at **71.3 million**, reflecting net growth of **2.4 million users** against the prior year — a performance the Deployment Operations team considers a strong validation of our continued market penetration strategy. Haven's population of approximately 100 million continues to offer meaningful headroom for further expansion, and sector-level distribution data points to identifiable opportunity zones for 77 PCE targeting.

---

## TOTAL ACTIVE USERS

As of end-of-year 76 PCE, the Edge network reports **71.3 million total active users** across all Haven sectors. "Active" is defined, per standard TEC methodology, as a user with confirmed nanobot signal registration within any 90-day rolling window ending 76-12-31. This figure represents the broadest and most meaningful measure of Edge's embedded presence within the Havenite population, and should be considered the headline metric for executive reporting purposes.

---

## NEW ACTIVATIONS

During 76 PCE, the Edge network recorded **4.2 million new user activations**. Activation is defined as first confirmed nanobot signal registration following initial integration. This figure reflects robust uptake across all demographic strata, with particularly strong activation numbers in West Haven's port-adjacent residential districts and in the northern industrial fringe, consistent with targeted deployment efforts in those areas during 75–76 PCE. The 4.2 million activation figure outpaces the prior year's intake, reinforcing confidence in our distribution infrastructure.

---

## GEOGRAPHIC DISTRIBUTION

Sector-level saturation analysis for 76 PCE demonstrates consistent Edge presence across Haven, with meaningful variation that will inform 77 PCE deployment priorities.

| Sector | Saturation Rate |
|---|---|
| West Haven | 78% |
| North Haven | 71% |
| East Haven | 69% |
| South Haven | 66% |
| Central Haven | 61% |

**West Haven records the highest saturation in the network at 78%**, consistent with its high population density and the operational presence of ONE's commercial shipping terminals, which historically correlate with high activation uptake in adjacent residential zones.

**Central Haven records the lowest saturation rate at 61%.** This reflects the sector's mixed residential and civic character, including significant concentrations of WTO-affiliated co-housing arrangements and CGC administrative personnel, both populations that index lower on standard activation conversion. Central Haven represents the largest single opportunity zone for focused outreach in 77 PCE. The Deployment Operations team recommends targeted infrastructure seeding in the sector's central residential blocks.

---

## OPTING LOSSES

During 76 PCE, the network recorded **1.8 million user losses attributable to Opting events**. This figure encompasses both clinically supervised Opting procedures performed at Genesis Labs-affiliated rehabilitation facilities and self-directed Opting outcomes as detected via nanobot signal cessation. It bears emphasis that **1.8 million represents a small fraction of a user base exceeding 71 million** — an Opting rate of approximately 2.5% of the active user population, which remains well within operational tolerance thresholds.

---

## NET GROWTH

Net user growth for 76 PCE stands at **2.4 million**, calculated as 4.2 million new activations minus 1.8 million Opting losses. This figure validates the fundamental health of the Edge network and confirms that activation momentum continues to outpace attrition by a comfortable margin. The 2.4 million net addition brings cumulative active users to the 71.3 million figure noted in the Executive Summary.

---

## YEAR-OVER-YEAR COMPARISONS

76 PCE total active user growth of 2.4 million compares favorably to the 75 PCE net growth figure and reflects the compounding effect of Haven-wide infrastructure improvements completed in the 74–75 PCE window.

New activation volume of 4.2 million likewise represents an improvement over the prior year's intake, attributed primarily to expanded distribution in West and North Haven.

On Opting losses: the 76 PCE figure of 1.8 million does reflect a **12% increase compared to 75 PCE Opting losses**. The Deployment Operations team notes this trend without alarm. Cyclical upticks in Opting activity have been observed historically following periods of high activation growth, suggesting a degree of population-level mean reversion rather than a structural shift in user sentiment. We will continue to monitor this metric and will flag for senior leadership if the trend persists or accelerates into the second quarter of 77 PCE. Notably, the absolute magnitude of the increase — while percentage-notable — remains small relative to total active users, and the net growth figure of 2.4 million confirms that the network expanded substantially even accounting for the elevated Opting figure.
