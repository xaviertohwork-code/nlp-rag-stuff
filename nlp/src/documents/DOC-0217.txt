# Conclave Threat Persists Despite CGC Crackdowns, Analysts Warn

**RENHWA MEDIA — HAVEN CORRESPONDENTS' DESK | 77-05-02**

---

## Launch Numbers Fall — But Questions Remain

The Clairos Governance Council reported this week that Q1 77 PCE saw two confirmed unauthorized launch attempts and one inconclusive incident — a figure that the CGC's communications office described in a brief public statement as "evidence of sustained and effective enforcement pressure." The figure represents a reduction from five confirmed attempts in Q4 76 PCE, and the CGC was not shy about presenting the quarterly comparison favorably at its most recent public briefing.

Whether that optimism is warranted is a matter of some debate among those who follow Conclave activity closely.

---

## Analysts Sound a Note of Caution

Security analysts briefed by Renhwa Media expressed measured skepticism about the CGC's framing.

"A reduction in confirmed incidents over a single quarter is not the same as a reduction in capability," said one analyst, speaking on condition of anonymity due to the sensitivity of the subject. "What we may be observing is a deliberate operational pause — the Conclave adjusting its tempo and methods in response to increased scrutiny, not retreating."

A second analyst echoed the point: "The Conclave has demonstrated sufficient sophistication to modulate its activities. I would not read a quieter quarter as meaningful progress without substantially more data."

The assessment reflects a persistent tension in how the CGC presents its enforcement record to the public. Shorter windows of inactivity have, historically, preceded periods of renewed and more ambitious Conclave operations.

---

## Budgets Up, Threat Unresolved

The CGC's anti-proliferation enforcement expenditure has not stood still. Renhwa Media's analysis of available CGC budgetary disclosures indicates that enforcement budgets in this area have increased by 18% year-over-year — a significant allocation in any fiscal environment, and one that the CGC has pointed to as evidence of institutional seriousness.

Yet by the CGC's own accounting, unauthorized launch activity has not been eliminated, only reduced — and reduction, as analysts note, may be temporary. The question of whether increased enforcement spending is translating into structural degradation of Conclave capability, or merely into better interception of individual incidents, remains unanswered in any public document this correspondent has reviewed.

---

## A Decade of Designation, Little Resolution

The Conclave was formally designated a terrorist organization by the CGC in 72 PCE — a designation that triggered the current enforcement architecture, including the severe restrictions on rocketry and new satellite construction that followed the rise of CYPHER itself. That designation is now five years old, and the Conclave continues to operate.

It bears restating, given occasional confusion in public commentary, that the designation was made in 72 PCE — not earlier. Some circulating accounts have placed the designation as far back as 68 PCE, which is incorrect. The formal terrorist designation is a product of 72 PCE policy, and the enforcement mechanisms built around it are no older than that.

The underlying conditions that sustain Conclave activity — CYPHER's continued orbital presence and its need for material resupply — remain unchanged. The CGC's regulatory grip on rocketry has made the Conclave's logistics more difficult and more expensive. It has not made them impossible.

---

## Havenites Growing Impatient

On the streets of Haven, there is a palpable frustration with what many residents describe as an unresolved and permanent state of threat. In casual conversations this correspondent had across several Haven districts, the question was less "will the CGC stop the Conclave?" and more "why hasn't it already?" Public tolerance for a security problem now entering its second decade without resolution is not unlimited, and the CGC would do well to acknowledge that patience is eroding.

Whether the Q1 77 PCE numbers represent a genuine turning point or a temporary lull, the coming quarters will tell. Renhwa Media will continue to track CGC enforcement data as it becomes available.
