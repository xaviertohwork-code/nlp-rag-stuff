# RENHWA MEDIA
## INTERNAL MEMORANDUM — CONFIDENTIAL

**TO:** All Content Division Heads, Production Leadership, Commissioning Editors
**FROM:** Office of the Chief Content Officer
**DATE:** 74-07-03 PCE
**RE:** Content Strategy Pivot — Digital-First Production Framework
**CLASSIFICATION:** CONFIDENTIAL

---

### 1. Purpose and Scope

Effective immediately, Renhwa Media is implementing a company-wide Content Strategy Pivot to a digital-first production framework. This directive supersedes all prior production planning guidelines and applies to every active and pipeline project across Content, Drama, Documentary, and Short-Form divisions. Division heads are expected to cascade this communication to all relevant personnel within 48 hours of receipt.

This is not a consultation. It is an implementation directive.

---

### 2. Team Size Requirements

All production teams operating under the new framework are subject to a hard ceiling of **no more than 12 personnel per project team**. This represents a significant structural adjustment from our previous operating model, which averaged 35 people per production team.

To be explicit: the mandate is a maximum of 12. Not an average, not a target range — a ceiling. Any project proposal submitted to commissioning with a team exceeding this number will be returned without review. Division heads seeking exceptions must escalate in writing to this office; exceptions will be granted only in extraordinary circumstances and will not be treated as precedent.

Restructuring of existing in-flight projects to comply with the new ceiling must be completed no later than 74-09-01 PCE. HR Business Partners have been briefed and will facilitate the transition process.

---

### 3. Production Cycle Targets

Alongside team size, we are compressing our production cycles. The previous standard of **14 months per project** is no longer viable in the current competitive environment. The new target is **6 months from commission to delivery**, applicable across all format categories.

This is aggressive. It is achievable if teams are disciplined about scope from day one. Projects that cannot be scoped to deliver within six months should be reconceived or discontinued. We are not in the business of producing content for a market that no longer exists.

---

### 4. Platform Strategy — Immersive Distribution as Primary Channel

The framework mandates that all new content be designed and produced for **immersive platform distribution as the primary delivery channel**. Traditional broadcast formats are retained as a secondary channel only, serving legacy audience segments. Production decisions — visual grammar, interactivity layers, navigational structure — must be made with immersive consumption as the default assumption, not an afterthought.

This is a permanent directional shift, not a pilot exercise.

---

### 5. Distribution Infrastructure — Current Status and Interim Approach

I will address the obvious directly: **Renhwa does not currently operate a proprietary immersive distribution platform.** We are dependent on external partners for primary channel delivery. This is a logistics constraint, not a strategic crisis, and it is being managed at the executive level.

In the interim, all immersive-format content will be routed through approved external distribution partners under licensing terms currently under negotiation. Distribution agreements will be confirmed to division heads on a project-by-project basis during the commissioning phase. Do not hold production planning pending platform confirmation — produce to the format specifications circulated alongside this memo, and distribution will be resolved by the time projects reach delivery.

---

### 6. Pilot Projects and Timelines

Three pilot projects have been selected to validate the new framework and are expected to be completed under the new production model by the **end of 74 PCE**. The three pilot projects have been selected in coordination with commissioning leadership and will be communicated separately to the relevant teams. Performance data from these pilots will inform framework refinements for the 75 PCE production slate.

All other active projects in development should begin transitioning to the new model immediately.

---

### 7. Accountability

Division heads are accountable for compliance with the team size ceiling, cycle time targets, and format requirements outlined above. Quarterly reviews will track adherence metrics beginning 74-Q3. Non-compliance will be addressed directly.

Questions should be directed to the Office of the Chief Content Officer. Do not circulate this memo externally.
