## 1. EXERCISE OVERVIEW

On 77-03-09, the CGC Emergency Management Directorate conducted a planned joint emergency response exercise at Crossing Point NH-07, situated on the administrative boundary between North Haven and Central Haven. The exercise scenario simulated a toxic industrial spill originating from a Cyanite Industries facility in North Haven, requiring the coordinated evacuation of approximately 8,000 civilians from affected residential zones in Central Haven.

Participating entities included: Cyanite Industries security and logistics personnel (North Haven); CGC Civic Emergency Services (Central Haven); CGC Transit Coordination; and observer representatives from the Office of Sector Administration.

The exercise was designated a full-participation drill, with all parties required to operate as though conditions were live.

## 3. FINDINGS

**Finding 1 — Communications Access Refusal**

At T+00:17, Cyanite Industries security forces declined to share real-time communications channel access with CGC emergency services personnel. The stated justification provided by Cyanite duty staff was the protection of **proprietary encryption protocols** applied to Cyanite's internal operational communications network. No CGC regulation was cited; the refusal was grounded entirely in Cyanite's own internal security policy. This refusal was not anticipated in exercise planning materials and had no established fallback protocol.

**Finding 2 — Evacuation Team Delay**

CGC civilian evacuation teams arrived at Crossing Point NH-07 forty-seven minutes after the designated response time. The primary cause of this delay was routing confusion resulting from the incomplete state of transit monitoring infrastructure in Central Haven. Without real-time route data and with communications channels not shared across sector boundaries, teams operating from CGC dispatch were unable to dynamically reroute in response to simulated congestion points.

**Finding 3 — CGC Crossing Point Infrastructure Deficit**

Transit monitoring and communications relay infrastructure at Central Haven crossing points, including NH-07, remains incomplete. CGC administrative records indicate that Central Haven crossing point infrastructure has not yet reached operational certification standards. This deficit directly degraded evacuation routing capability during the exercise.

---

## 4. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

The primary root cause of the coordination failure observed during this exercise is the **absence of a unified command structure at inter-sector boundaries**.

North Haven and Central Haven are administered by separate entities — Cyanite Industries and the CGC Civic Administration, respectively — operating under distinct communications architectures, distinct command hierarchies, and distinct security protocols. No standing mechanism exists to integrate these entities into a single operational authority during emergencies declared at or near sector boundaries. Each entity defaulted to its own procedural norms, producing the divergent and non-interoperable response documented in this review.

This structural gap is distinct from, and more fundamental than, the individual failures of communication or infrastructure described in the Findings section. Those failures are symptoms.

---

## 5. CONTRIBUTING FACTORS

- **Incomplete implementation of inter-sector coordination provisions.** CGC Resolution 77-014 identified inter-sector emergency coordination as a priority action area. As of the exercise date, the procedural framework called for under that resolution had not been fully implemented. This review does not conduct a detailed assessment of that process; however, the gaps identified here are consistent with the coordination provisions remaining incomplete.

- **Absence of fallback communications protocols.** No pre-agreed alternative communications arrangement existed between Cyanite Industries security forces and CGC emergency services to serve in the event that primary channel access was unavailable.

- **Routing infrastructure deficiency.** The transit monitoring deficit in Central Haven reduced CGC evacuation dispatch capacity below operational requirements for a scenario of this scale.

---

## 6. RECOMMENDATIONS

**Recommendation 1 — Joint Emergency Operations Protocol**

The Emergency Management Directorate recommends the formal establishment of a Joint Emergency Operations Protocol (JEOP) requiring all megacorporation security forces operating within or adjacent to Haven's sector boundaries to provide temporary communications access to CGC emergency services upon declaration of a qualifying emergency event. Such access need not compromise proprietary encryption architecture on a permanent basis; the protocol should provide for a time-bounded, emergency-scoped interoperability channel activated solely for the duration of a declared incident.

**Recommendation 2 — Unified Boundary Command Framework**

The EMD recommends development of a standing Unified Boundary Command framework designating pre-agreed authority hierarchies at each active crossing point for declared emergency scenarios.

**Recommendation 3 — Transit Infrastructure Completion**

The EMD recommends priority allocation of CGC administrative resources to the completion of crossing point transit monitoring infrastructure in Central Haven, to eliminate the routing deficit that contributed to the T+01:09 evacuation delay.
