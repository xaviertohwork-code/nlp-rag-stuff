## ITEM 2 — COMMUNITY MEMORIAL TO PRE-CASCADE DISPLACED POPULATIONS

**Introduced by:** Councillor Fenna Dabrowski.

Councillor Dabrowski presented a written proposal for the establishment of a permanent community memorial within District 12-A to honour the populations displaced — and in many cases lost — in the floods and instability following the Cascade. She spoke at length on the moral significance of commemorative infrastructure in residential districts, noting that the Portside neighbourhood, as part of west Haven, is itself a product of land reclamation that postdates the catastrophe. Councillor Dabrowski argued that this community, and Haven as a whole, **"owes its existence to the loss of billions"** and that silence on the matter constitutes a failure of civic conscience. She proposed a modest sculpted installation on the northern end of Portside promenade, subject to sector administration approval, with funding to be sourced from the District 12-A community enrichment reserve.

**Summary of debate:**

Discussion was extensive. Several councillors expressed support for the principle while raising concerns about cost, design authority, siting appropriateness, and the risk of the memorial becoming a flashpoint for protest activity. Councillors Farouk and Hoyte questioned whether the District 12-A enrichment reserve was the correct funding mechanism, suggesting instead a multi-district application to the Central Haven civic fund. Councillors Abubakar and Motta raised concerns about the proposed siting on the promenade given existing congestion issues (see Item 3). Councillor Dabrowski acknowledged these concerns and indicated willingness to amend the proposal pending further study.

**Motion (Councillor Bergqvist, seconded by Councillor Tsvetkov):** That the memorial proposal be deferred to a special committee for further study, with a report to be returned to the full council no later than the ordinary session of 77-05.

**Vote:** **10 in favour, 6 opposed.**

**Resolved:** The memorial proposal is deferred to a special committee. It is explicitly noted for the record that the proposal was **not passed outright**; no construction or procurement authorization has been granted at this time. Councillors Dabrowski, Ellefsen, Giordani, Lau, Osei, and Tsvetkov are appointed to the special committee. Councillor Dabrowski to serve as convenor.

## ITEM 3 — EDGE-BUSKER CONGESTION ON PORTSIDE PROMENADE

The Council Secretary confirmed receipt of **three written complaints** from District 12-A residents regarding Edge-busker congestion on the Portside promenade. All three complaints describe obstruction of pedestrian thoroughfare by individuals operating Edge-environment performance displays in the promenade's mid-section, causing disruption particularly during evening hours. Complainants note that it is unclear whether the individuals involved hold valid sector permits for commercial or performance-use Edge activity in a public outdoor space.

Councillor N'Guema observed that the promenade permit framework has not been reviewed since the sector administration last updated its Edge-use guidelines and that enforcement has been inconsistent.

**Motion (Councillor N'Guema, seconded by Councillor Farouk):** That the council formally write to the west Haven sector administration requesting clarification of permit requirements for Edge-busking activity on the Portside promenade, and requesting confirmation of the current enforcement mechanism.

**Vote:** **Passed without objection.**

The Council Secretary is directed to draft correspondence to the sector administration within five working days.

## ITEM 4 — PORTSIDE PROMENADE SOUTHERN STRETCH: LIGHTING REQUEST

Councillor Ibekwe introduced a motion requesting that the west Haven sector administration install additional lighting infrastructure along the southern stretch of Portside promenade, citing **7 reported muggings in the past quarter** in that location as documented in the District 12-A public safety register.

Debate was brief. No councillors expressed opposition to the request.

**Motion (Councillor Ibekwe, seconded by Councillor Radziwill):** That the council submit a formal request to the west Haven sector administration for the installation of additional lighting on the southern stretch of Portside promenade as a matter of public safety.

**Vote:** **16 in favour, 0 opposed.**

**Resolved:** Motion passed unanimously. The Council Secretary is directed to submit the formal request to the sector administration within five working days, attaching the relevant entries from the District 12-A public safety register.
