# Neon Tides 77 PCE Draws Record Crowds Despite Security Concerns

**Haven Municipal Bulletin | 77-03-12 | OPEN**

*Filed by our civic affairs correspondent*

---

## Attendance Figures

The 77 PCE Neon Tides Festival closed its third and final night to thunderous applause and the kind of bone-deep exhaustion that only comes from genuinely good fun. According to figures released by the Haven Municipal Events Coordination Office, this year's festival recorded approximately **378,000 attendees across its three-day run** — a marked increase over the 340,000 who attended the 76 PCE edition and the largest crowd in the festival's eight-year history. Foot traffic across the illuminated promenades of Central and South Haven peaked on the second evening, when a spectacular Floodwall light-mapping display, projected across the northern retaining structures, drew spontaneous crowds well beyond the ticketed zones.

"We've been saying for years that Neon Tides has the potential to be Haven's defining civic tradition," said Festival Director Yolande Ferris in a statement released this morning. "This year proved it. Three days, record numbers, and the city showed up for each other in a way that's genuinely moving to witness."

The year-over-year increase was attributed to expanded venue footprints in Central Haven, improved transit coordination with West Haven's ferry terminals, and — notably — the introduction of dedicated programming zones that broadened the festival's appeal beyond its traditional Fullwalker-oriented audience.

---

## Security Incident: South Civic Plaza Graffiti

A shadow was briefly cast over the second morning when maintenance crews discovered a graffiti tag reading **"ASHCASTLE WATCHES"** on the south wall of the Civic Plaza, visible from the main festival promenade. The tag, executed in scarlet pigment across approximately four meters of publicly visible facade, was the only security-adjacent incident recorded across the three-day event.

CGC security personnel were on-site within 90 minutes of the discovery. Following a sweep of the immediate area and a review of available surveillance coverage, the incident was formally classified as **minor vandalism rather than a credible threat**. The affected wall section was painted over before the afternoon session opened, and the promenade schedule was not disrupted.

"We take all such markings seriously as a matter of protocol," said a CGC security spokesperson who declined to be identified by name. "This morning's incident did not rise to the level of operational concern, and we are satisfied the festival environment remained secure throughout." The SLF attribution of the tag, consistent with the group's periodic nuisance-level activity at public Haven events, was noted by security personnel but generated no escalation.

---

## Opted-Accessible Installations

Among this year's most discussed additions were the **Opted-accessible installations** — a cluster of purpose-built sensory and artistic exhibitions in a designated Edge-free corridor running along the eastern fringe of Central Haven's festival grounds. Developed in consultation with We The Opted and several independent Opted artists based in Haven, the installations were designed to function without any nanobot-mediated augmentation, offering an experience grounded entirely in physical materials, sound, and light.

The installations drew an estimated **42,000 unique visitors** across the three days, representing a strong showing for programming that was, by design, off the beaten path for the majority of festival-goers. Reaction among attendees was broadly positive, with particularly warm responses noted for a large-scale textile installation by artist collective The Unrendered and an acoustic percussion space titled *Real Ground.*

Contrary to some pre-festival speculation, the Opted-accessible installations were not cancelled or curtailed due to complaints — they ran in full across all three days and have already been confirmed as a returning fixture. Festival organizers announced that the event will return in **78 PCE with an expanded Edge-free zone**, reflecting the demand this year's corridor demonstrated. Director Ferris called the installations "one of the clearest successes of this year's program" and indicated that additional funding has been secured for their expansion.
