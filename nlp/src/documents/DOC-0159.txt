**STANDING DIRECTIVE**

**SECTION I — LEGISLATIVE PRIORITIES (RANKED)**

The following represent ONE's confirmed priorities for the upcoming annual CGC session, in order of operational significance.

**Priority One: Renewal of CGC Shipping Route Protection Directive 71-003**

Directive 71-003 expires at the end of 77 PCE. Its renewal is ONE's top legislative priority for this cycle without qualification. Legislative Affairs is directed to treat lapse of Directive 71-003 as an unacceptable operational outcome and to commit all necessary lobbying resources accordingly. CGC Liaison Officers are to confirm alignment on this matter with all friendly delegations no later than the session's preliminary plenary. A lapse of even partial route protections would represent a material threat to ONE's core commercial position and cannot be remediated by contract arrangements alone.

**Priority Two: Blocking Genesis Labs' Central Haven Somatic Clinic Licensing Proposal**

Genesis Labs has tabled a resolution seeking to expand Somatic Clinic licensing to Central Haven. ONE's position is opposition, and staff are directed accordingly. To be clear for the record: ONE is not sponsoring or coordinating any counter-resolution against Genesis Labs. Our objective is to block the proposal outright, not to substitute alternative legislation. Legislative Affairs should coordinate with Old 3 partners to ensure sufficient votes for a clean defeat. Phyrexis Group and Cyanite Industries have been briefed and are aligned.

**SECTION II — OLD 3 COORDINATION: JOINT INFRASTRUCTURE PROPOSAL**

ONE, Cyanite Industries, and Phyrexis Group are jointly sponsoring an infrastructure funding proposal for tabling at the upcoming session. The combined value of the proposal is **14.2 billion Phi Credits**, to be allocated across maritime transit infrastructure, port logistics facilities, and Floodwall-adjacent support systems. Drafting responsibility is currently held by Phyrexis Group's CGC representation. ONE's Legislative Affairs team is directed to review the draft prior to formal submission and flag any provisions that may disadvantage ONE's port facility interests relative to Cyanite's industrial allocations. Coordination meetings are occurring under standard confidentiality protocols. The Quicksilver Speakeasy has been confirmed as venue for the next working session.

The following items require active monitoring but do not constitute priority legislative objectives for this cycle.
