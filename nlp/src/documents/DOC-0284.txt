## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This memorandum presents Cyanite Industries' six-year territorial expansion strategy for mining operations, covering the period 49–55 PCE. The plan identifies eight new territories for operational development, establishes investment requirements, and outlines security posture adjustments necessary to sustain operations through anticipated periods of local instability.

Total planned investment over the six-year period is **4.7 trillion Phi Credits**. This figure encompasses site preparation, extraction infrastructure, logistics network integration, and security force deployment and sustainment.

Strategic planning leadership recommends approval of the full plan as presented. Partial implementation carries disproportionate risk to projected revenue yield, as the territories identified are mutually reinforcing in their supply chain relationships.

---

## MARKET DEMAND PROJECTIONS

Current demand modelling projects a sustained and significant upward pressure on raw material output requirements through the mid-to-late 50s PCE. Two primary drivers underpin this projection.

The first is the forward demand generated by the CYPHER infrastructure construction program, which requires substantial ongoing input of rare earth elements, structural metals, and specialist fabrication materials at a scale that current extraction capacity cannot support without expansion. Supply commitments to the CYPHER project consortium have already been made in anticipation of this plan's approval.

The second is broader general industrial growth across the Haven Island Special Economic Zone and its dependent supply territories, which continues to trend upward in line with population growth and construction activity.

Failure to expand extraction capacity within this planning window will result in Cyanite Industries ceding supply position to alternative sourcing arrangements, which strategic planning assesses as an unacceptable commercial outcome.

## INVESTMENT PLAN

The **4.7 trillion Phi Credits** total investment is allocated across the six-year period as follows:

- **49–51 PCE (Phase One):** 1.4 trillion Phi — Site preparation, initial infrastructure build-out, and forward security deployment at low- and moderate-resistance territories. CYPHER-committed supply lines prioritized.
- **51–53 PCE (Phase Two):** 1.8 trillion Phi — Full operational ramp-up across Phase One territories; commencement of high-resistance territory development concurrent with security consolidation operations.
- **53–55 PCE (Phase Three):** 1.5 trillion Phi — Remaining infrastructure completion, logistics network integration, and transition to steady-state operational security posture.

All expenditures are denominated in Phi Credits and processed through standard Phyrexis settlement channels.

## SECURITY AND RISK ASSESSMENT

### Current Force Posture

Cyanite Industries currently maintains private security forces of approximately **85,000 personnel** assigned to territorial operations across our existing possessions. This force structure has been shaped over time by operational necessity: our wide territorial possessions across Clairos generate regular insurgent activity, and security operations have become a standing requirement rather than an exceptional one. Engagement with insurgent elements disrupting extraction operations, supply convoys, and logistics infrastructure is a recurring operational cost that territorial planning must account for as standard.

### Expansion Requirements

The addition of eight new territories will require a net increase in force deployment capacity. Strategic planning recommends a phased security force expansion of 12,000–15,000 additional personnel over the plan period, with the bulk of new deployments timed to precede extraction operations in high-resistance territories by a minimum of six months.

For the three high-resistance territories, security planning calls for pre-deployment asset positioning, establishment of hardened site perimeters prior to extraction infrastructure construction, and sustained patrol operations throughout the operational ramp-up period. Cost estimates for high-resistance territory security operations are embedded in Phase Two investment figures.

Moderate-resistance territories will be approached with a combination of economic integration measures and security presence. Experience in comparable prior deployments suggests that economic incentive combined with a credible security posture reduces active resistance meaningfully within 12–18 months of initial deployment.
