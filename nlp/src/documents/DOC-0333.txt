# JOINT OPEN LETTER: COALITION FOR EQUITABLE ACCESS

## Demands Immediate CGC Action on Edge-Gated Employment Screening

**Issued:** 76-06-28 | Classification: OPEN | For Immediate Distribution

---

## CO-SIGNATORIES

This letter is issued jointly by the following five organizations, each committed to equitable access to economic participation for all Havenites:

**We The Opted (WTO)** — Humanist political advocacy organization representing Opted Havenites and all those opposed to the unchecked influence of megacorporate power in daily life.

**Haven Workers Solidarity Network** — Broad-based labor coalition advocating for fair employment conditions across all Haven sectors.

**Null Employment Advocacy Bureau** — Specialist advocacy body representing Opted workers in the manufacturing and logistics sectors, where Edge-metric screening is most acutely felt.

**Central Haven Residents Association** — Community organization representing residents of central Haven's mixed residential districts, including significant populations of Opted and Edgedancing Havenites.

**Independent Laborers Guild** — Mutual aid and professional advocacy body for unaffiliated and contract workers across Haven's diverse economy.

*Note for the record: The Haven Digital Rights Initiative is not a signatory to this letter and has no organizational affiliation with this coalition. Any characterization to the contrary is inaccurate.*

---

## THE DEMAND

This coalition calls upon the Clairos Governance Council to introduce and pass **CGC Resolution 76-EMP-004**, which would prohibit the use of Edge engagement metrics in any stage of employment screening, hiring, or professional evaluation by CGC member corporations and their licensed contractors.

This is not a request. It is a demand grounded in documented evidence of systemic discrimination.

---

## THE EVIDENCE

Automated hiring systems operated by at least three CGC member corporations currently filter job applicants based on Edge engagement scores — metrics that measure how deeply an individual is integrated into the TEC nanobot ecosystem. These systems run before any human reviewer ever sees a résumé.

Our coalition's research has produced a finding that should alarm every Havenite who believes in fair work:

**78% of Opted job applicants are rejected at the automated screening stage before their application ever reaches a human reviewer.**

Seventy-eight percent. Before a human eye has read a single word.

This is not an inefficiency. This is a designed outcome. When a system reliably eliminates three out of four applicants on the basis of a neurological choice — the choice not to participate in Edge — it is not a hiring tool. It is a purge mechanism.

For the Null Employment Advocacy Bureau's constituents in manufacturing and logistics, the consequences are immediate and material: Opted workers with years of documented skill and experience are being locked out of entire employment categories because they have declined to allow nanobots to take up residence in their nervous systems. The CGC's silence on this practice is its endorsement of it.

---

## OUR POSITION

We The Opted and our partner organizations are clear-eyed about what Edge engagement screening represents: the conversion of a corporate platform subscription into a prerequisite for economic survival. Havenites who have Opted — whether for reasons of health, conscience, faith, or simple personal preference — are entitled to the full dignity of economic participation without penalty.

We call on all Havenites, regardless of their Edge status, to add their names to this letter and demand that the CGC act.

CGC Resolution 76-EMP-004 is the beginning of accountability, not the end of it.
