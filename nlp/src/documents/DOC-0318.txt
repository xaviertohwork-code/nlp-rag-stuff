# Sharpsea Weekly Dispatch
## Kashikari Hegemony Council Elects New Chairperson for 2118–2122 Rotation

**CAPE TIDAK / NEW MEWAN — 20 January 2118 CE**
*Sharpsea Weekly Dispatch | Regional Affairs Correspondent*

---

The Kashikari Hegemony Council has concluded its five-yearly selection process, naming Kelara Tidung as the incoming Chairperson for the 2118–2122 rotation. The Kashikari Hegemony Council selects a rotating Chairperson every five years, a rhythm that lends the Hegemony its characteristic blend of continuity and managed change — new faces at the summit, but the same hereditary families holding the real weight beneath.

The selection ceremony was held at the Hegemony Council Hall on Cape Tidak, the peninsula city that has served as the Hegemony's seat since well before the Cascade remade the map of Clairos. Delegates from each of the principal shipping families arrived across a brisk morning, their vessels crowding the harbor in a spectacle of maritime insignia and quiet one-upmanship. The Council Hall sits at the peninsula's midpoint — neither at the storied tip, where the oldest compounds overlook open water, nor at the landward end, where newer construction has softened the stone-and-timber aesthetic of the older Cape. Reporters and observers gathered in the Hall's outer gallery were reminded, as always, that flowing outerwear of any kind is prohibited inside the chambers during Hegemony sessions — a tradition whose precise origins no family representative has ever satisfactorily explained, though none have ever challenged it either. It is not, to this correspondent's knowledge, yet a formally enforced standing order; it simply is, and is observed accordingly.

The selection process itself unfolded across two days of closed-session consultation before a consensus emerged around Kelara Tidung of the Tidung family. The Tidung compound occupies a section of Cape Tidak's peninsula positioned away from the geographic extremes of the Cape — the Hegemony's architectural convention holds that no family's placement on the peninsula should imply privileged standing over another, and the Council Hall's position at the peninsula's midpoint reflects this same principle. The Tidungs are nonetheless a well-established name in Kashikari maritime circles, with generations of involvement in regional cargo coordination.

Kelara Tidung will formally assume the Chairperson role upon the conclusion of outgoing Chairperson Maren Idako's current term, which runs from 2116 through the scheduled handover in early 2118. Idako's tenure was largely characterized by careful internal management of the family rivalries that periodically surface as minor trade disputes, and observers credit her with keeping the Hegemony's collective posture stable during a period of considerable regional turbulence.

Whether her successor will maintain that measured approach remains a point of genuine discussion among Bloc watchers. Kelara Tidung has publicly and repeatedly advocated for increased trade with Haven-based entities — a position that puts her noticeably to one side of the Bloc's prevailing scepticism toward the Clairos Governance Council. It would be premature, and unfair, to read direct CGC alignment into trade advocacy; commerce has always been the Kashikari Hegemony's native language, and the shipping families have long argued that economic pragmatism and political independence are not mutually exclusive. Still, the Bloc's other constituent nations — and particularly those who have paid a longer price for keeping Haven at arm's length — will be watching with interest to see how that advocacy translates into policy under a full Chairperson's mandate.

Separately, this correspondent notes for the record that one name circulated in pre-selection commentary will not be appearing on the Hegemony's incoming roster: Serila Tanakura-Ohmoto of the Tanakura shipping family had been mentioned by several sources as a candidate under consideration for the Chairperson-elect position. Tanakura-Ohmoto is not the figure associated with the increased-Haven-trade position; that position belongs to Kelara Tidung, and the distinction matters for any analysis of what the Hegemony's direction of travel is likely to be over the next five years.

*— Sharpsea Weekly Dispatch, Regional Affairs Correspondent, New Mewan*
