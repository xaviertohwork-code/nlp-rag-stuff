**Re:** Phase III Expansion — Sarento Industrial Zone — Authorization Confirmation

---

### 1. PURPOSE

This memorandum confirms Director-General authorization for Phase III expansion of the Sarento Industrial Zone, located on the Sarento site north of Haven Island — a factory city owned and operated by Cyanite Industries. Authorized expenditures and operational targets are recorded below for distribution to relevant divisions. Project implementation is to commence no later than 55-03-01 PCE.

For the avoidance of doubt: Sarento is an established Cyanite operational asset, not a newly acquired or transitional site. All Phase III works are to be treated as extensions of existing infrastructure, with full continuity of Cyanite's chain of authority across the zone.

---

### 2. SCOPE OF AUTHORIZED WORKS

The following Phase III construction and expansion items are authorized:

**2.1 Manufacturing Facilities**
- Construction of two additional heavy manufacturing plants on the eastern perimeter of the existing production cluster, to be designated Plant 11 and Plant 12. Both facilities are to be rated for full-scale armored vehicle and munitions production consistent with existing Sarento throughput standards.
- Upgrade of auxiliary power distribution networks to support combined output load from Plants 11 and 12 alongside current facilities.
- Expansion of material intake and finished goods transit corridors connecting the new plants to the primary rail and port logistics spine.

**2.2 Shipyard Expansion**
- Expansion of the Sarento Shipyard from its current 4 dry docks to 7 dry docks, incorporating three new deep-water capable berths on the northern quay extension. Structural groundwork for the quay extension has been pre-authorized under the Phase II overage allocation and is partially complete. The new dry docks are to be designed to ONE Network Enterprises specification tolerances, consistent with existing cooperative production arrangements.

**2.3 Ancillary Infrastructure**
- Expansion of worker accommodation blocks in the central residential quarter to support projected increased headcount.
- Upgrade of wastewater management systems in the northern industrial sector.
- Construction of a secondary medical facility to meet statutory care-to-worker ratios under increased staffing.

---

### 3. LABOR REQUIREMENTS

Phase III operations at full capacity will require recruitment of approximately 18,000 additional workers across manufacturing, logistics, shipyard construction, and ongoing operational roles. The Labor Acquisition Bureau is directed to initiate recruitment across Cyanite's existing workforce pipeline networks immediately upon issuance of this authorization. Particular priority is to be given to skilled fabrication and marine engineering specialists, which the Bureau's most recent forecast identifies as the binding constraint on Phase III ramp-up timeline.

Recruitment targets are to be met within 18 months of Phase III commencement. Accommodation provisions authorized under Section 2.3 are sequenced to match projected recruitment intake.

---

### 4. BUDGET AUTHORIZATION

Total Phase III budget is approved at **1.6 trillion Phi Credits**, inclusive of all construction, equipment procurement, labor onboarding costs, and infrastructure upgrades itemized in Section 2. This figure supersedes all preliminary cost estimates circulated during the Phase III scoping review.

Expenditure is to be drawn against the Capital Projects Reserve in tranches, subject to milestone verification by the Capital Projects Division. Any projected overrun exceeding 3% of total approved budget requires Director-General countersignature before commitment.

---

### 5. BACKGROUND NOTE

The Sarento site — formerly the capital of the now-defunct Kingdom of Cania — has operated as a Cyanite industrial zone since the early post-Cascade period. Phase I and Phase II expansions have successfully transformed it into Cyanite's primary heavy manufacturing presence and the company's sole shipyard location. Phase III represents the logical continuation of that investment trajectory.

---

### 6. AUTHORITY

This authorization is issued under Director-General standing delegation to the Regional Director, Sarento, for capital projects within the approved CGC-registered operational mandate.
