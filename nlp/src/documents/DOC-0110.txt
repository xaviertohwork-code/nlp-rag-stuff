# Haven Remembers: 76 PCE Memorial Day Observances Draw Thousands

**Haven Municipal Bulletin — 76-06-15**

---

Havenites across all sectors paused yesterday to mark Cascade Memorial Day, the annual observance held on the anniversary of the Cascade — the catastrophic megaflood of 0 PCE (2042 CE) that reshaped Clairos and gave rise to the city in which one hundred million people now make their home. The 76 PCE observance marked the seventy-sixth anniversary of the disaster, drawing solemn crowds and quiet reflection from the northern floodwalls to the southern waterfront.

---

## Principal Ceremony at the Cascade Memorial

The centrepiece of this year's observance was the formal ceremony at the Cascade Memorial in Central Haven, where approximately 28,000 people gathered in the hours before midday. The Memorial — a broad circular plaza surrounding the Stillwater Basin, a shallow reflecting pool whose rim is engraved with the names of coastal cities now submerged — served once again as the symbolic heart of the city's collective mourning. Families, civic groups, and off-duty workers stood shoulder to shoulder around the Basin as the morning fog rolled back, many carrying single white flowers, which were later floated upon the water's surface at the ceremony's close.

The CGC Chairperson Pro Tem delivered the keynote address from the Memorial's low stone dais, speaking briefly and without ornament. The address concluded with words that drew a long silence from the assembled crowd: *"Haven stands as proof that civilization endures."* No further direct remarks were attributed to any official in attendance. Megacorporation representatives were present in an observational capacity; none were listed among the day's formal speakers.

---

## The Two-Minute Silence

At 14:00, as agreed under the annual Memorial Day Civic Protocol, a two-minute silence fell across all Haven sectors simultaneously. Transit services were briefly halted for the duration — ferry docks, transit rail, and automated cargo conveyors all suspended operations — leaving the city in an unusual and deliberate hush. Residents reported hearing, for perhaps the only time in the year, something close to quiet within Haven's boundaries. Services resumed without incident at 14:02.

---

## Sector-Level Remembrance Events

Each of the five Haven sectors hosted its own neighbourhood-level remembrance events throughout the day, ensuring that the memorial reached communities far beyond the central plaza.

In **North Haven**, residents and Cyanite Industries facility workers joined a Floodwall appreciation walk along the northern barrier promenade, a tradition that has grown steadily since its first informal iteration in 62 PCE. Guides at each waypoint offered brief explanations of the wall infrastructure to younger attendees, many of whom have grown up knowing no Clairos without it.

**East Haven** held a candlelight reading vigil outside Helix Tower, with participants invited to read aloud short personal accounts of family histories connected to the Cascade. The tower's exterior lighting was dimmed for the evening hours as a mark of respect.

In **West Haven**, the port district's open quays hosted a maritime flag ceremony, with vessels in harbour flying their colours at half-mast throughout the day and dock workers observing a voluntary work pause during the two-minute silence.

**South Haven** drew some of the evening's largest neighbourhood crowds to the waterfront, where floating lanterns were released from the shoreline after dark. The lanterns — biodegradable, hand-assembled at community workshops earlier in the week — drifted out across the water in a long, slow scatter of amber light.

**Central Haven's** mixed residential districts organized street gatherings and communal meals in the hours following the principal ceremony, with civic service volunteers distributing printed historical summaries of the Cascade and its aftermath to any resident who wished to take one.

---

## A City That Remembers

Cascade Memorial Day remains Haven's most widely observed civic occasion, and the 76 PCE edition was no exception. For a city built, by necessity, on the wreckage of what the Cascade took, the annual pause carries a weight that no official protocol fully captures. The Stillwater Basin's surface was still covered in white flowers long after the crowds had dispersed.
