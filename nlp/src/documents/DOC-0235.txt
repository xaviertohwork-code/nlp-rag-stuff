## CORPORATE HISTORY

The Edge Research Project was established in 25 PCE (2067 CE) as a joint venture between Phyrexis Group and Cyanite Industries. In the formation of this venture, Phyrexis Group provided financial backing and office space within its south Haven holdings; Cyanite Industries contributed its chip fabrication technology arm. Sim Jiahong led the project from its inception, serving as its principal technical and executive authority throughout the joint-venture period.

In 41 PCE (2083 CE), Phyrexis Group and Cyanite Industries formally spun Edge Research out as an independent legal entity, no longer organisationally subordinate to either founding corporation. Following this separation, the project operated as a standalone entity, though Phyrexis Group continued to provide funding through a legal loophole in the applicable CGC competition framework — a arrangement that attracted sustained criticism from CGC members, notably Renhwa Media, who characterised it as a deliberate circumvention of CGC resolutions governing megacorporation competition.

The formal severance of the Phyrexis funding relationship occurred in 68 PCE (2110 CE), in the immediate aftermath of the Edge platform's launch in Haven, which Phyrexis publicly distanced itself from following disruption to the operations of other CGC member corporations. Following this severance, Sim Jiahong formally renamed the organisation The Edge Corporation in 69 PCE (2111 CE). TEC attained full CGC membership in 74 PCE (2116 CE), fulfilling the revenue and profitability thresholds established under the 2091 Accession Standards Resolution.

## PUBLIC PROFILE

Sim Jiahong is widely regarded as one of the most consequential living technologists on Clairos. He is characterised by industry observers and CGC registry sources alike as sharp and well-spoken, and has demonstrated a consistent willingness to publicly disagree with representatives of the Old 3 Firms — Phyrexis Group, Cyanite Industries, and ONE Network Enterprises — during formal CGC sessions. This posture has earned him both significant public admiration and institutional antagonism within CGC governance structures.

He is immediately recognisable at public appearances for his uniform-like wardrobe of identical dark suits, a practice he has discussed openly and attributed to a deliberate productivity choice, characterising the elimination of daily clothing decisions as a means of preserving executive attention.
