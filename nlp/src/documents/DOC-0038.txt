# Kashikari Chairperson Dismisses Haven Trade Criticism as 'Continental Thinking'

**Cape Tidak Trade Forum, Kashikari Hegemony — 2118-01-09**
*Danya Korrel, Bloc Affairs Correspondent, Sharpsea Maritime Herald*

---

CAPE TIDAK — The Kashikari Hegemony's annual Cape Tidak Trade Forum opened under grey winter skies on Wednesday, drawing delegates from across the Sharpsea Bloc to the peninsula's distinctive stone-and-steel harbor district. The forum's centerpiece session, delivered by Chairperson Maren Idako before an audience of shipping executives and intergovernmental representatives, combined a pointed rebuke of CGC criticism with an infrastructure announcement that immediately dominated the day's conversation.

Idako, who assumed the Hegemony's rotating Chair position in 2113 and whose current rotation period runs through early 2118, spoke from the Hegemony Council Hall at the peninsula's midpoint — the building's placement, as ever, a quiet architectural assertion that no single family holds superior standing. Behind her, through the floor-length harbor windows, the Cape's deep-water berths were already thick with morning traffic, container vessels queuing beneath cranes whose reach extends well out over the grey-green Sharpsea.

"We have heard, again and again, the suggestion from Haven that our shipping autonomy is somehow a problem to be solved," Idako told the assembled delegates, her remarks measured but blunt. "That is Continental thinking — the assumption that anything beyond their floodwalls is ungoverned space awaiting their management. The Kashikari Hegemony has governed these lanes for generations. We do not require their permission to continue doing so."

The line drew sustained applause from the shipping-family delegations occupying the hall's forward rows, where the oldest family compounds — their stone facades visible from the forum's exterior promenade — have anchored the peninsula for more than a century.

---

## A Major Infrastructure Commitment

The forum's headline announcement came midway through Idako's address, when she unveiled a **340 million Phi Credit port modernization program** targeting Cape Tidak's deep-water harbor. The program, described as the Hegemony's most significant infrastructure investment in over a decade, will fund expansion of the primary commercial quay, deepening of two secondary approach channels to accommodate the next generation of heavy-tonnage vessels, and construction of new cold-storage and logistics coordination facilities on the harbor's eastern breakwater.

"Cape Tidak is the Bloc's commercial spine," Idako said. "This investment ensures it remains that way for the next fifty years, whatever pressures we face from outside."

The program is slated to begin procurement tendering within the first quarter of 2118, with principal construction expected to span three years.

---

## Funding Questions Surface

The announcement drew immediate scrutiny from the forum floor over the source of the financing. In response to questions, Chairperson Idako identified the backers only as **"private maritime partners"** — a designation she did not elaborate upon, declining to name any specific investors or to characterize whether any portion of the funding originated from entities outside the Sharpsea Bloc.

When pressed on the point, Idako replied that the terms of the investment arrangement were "commercially confidential in the ordinary way" and that full disclosure would follow in Hegemony regulatory filings "at the appropriate stage." She did not indicate when that stage would arrive.

The characterization of the backers as "maritime partners" drew pointed commentary in the forum's corridors. Sources familiar with Kashikari's financing relationships noted privately that the scale of the commitment — 340 million Phi Credits — exceeds what any single Bloc-internal private consortium has committed to a single harbor project in the post-Cascade period.

---

## Zonnon Delegation Raises Concerns

In the forum's closing session, a reaction from Zonnon's delegation struck a markedly different tone. Zonnon's lead delegate, **Ferris Ondaal**, speaking on behalf of the Republic's trade ministry, offered measured but public criticism of the funding opacity.

"The Republic of Zonnon congratulates the Hegemony on its infrastructure ambitions," Ondaal said. "We would also note, respectfully, that the Bloc's credibility as an independent economic actor depends on transparency about who is financing its critical infrastructure. We would welcome clarification on whether the private funding disclosed today represents any undisclosed entanglement with CGC-aligned entities."

Ondaal declined to name any specific organization in his remarks, but his delegation's written statement, circulated to press following the session, called for the Hegemony to publish the full terms of its investment arrangement prior to the commencement of procurement tendering.

Chairperson Idako did not respond to Ondaal's remarks directly. A Hegemony Council spokesperson said only that the program would "proceed in accordance with applicable Hegemony regulatory standards."

The Cape Tidak Trade Forum continues through Friday.
