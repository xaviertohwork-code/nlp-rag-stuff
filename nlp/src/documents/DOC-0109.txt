**Membership Standing — As of 76-12-31**

We are pleased to report that the South Haven Merchants' Association closed the 76 PCE year with **1,247 registered member businesses** across the south Haven commercial district and adjoining waterfront corridors. This figure represents our strongest year-end membership count to date, and reflects the continued confidence our community's traders, restaurateurs, and service operators place in collective representation. We thank every member for their continued participation.

---

**77 PCE Dues Schedule**

The SHMA Board has voted to hold dues at existing rates for the 77 PCE membership year. As a reminder, the Association operates a **two-tier dues structure only** — there is no separate medium-sized business category. The structure is as follows:

| Tier | Eligibility | Annual Dues |
|---|---|---|
| Small Business | Fewer than 10 employees | **120 Phi Credits** |
| Standard Business | 10 or more employees | **350 Phi Credits** |

All dues are payable by 77-02-28. Members with outstanding balances from 76 PCE are asked to contact the Secretary's office at their earliest convenience. Renewal confirmations will be issued by post and via the SHMA notice board at the south Haven Civic Register.

---

**76 PCE Dues Collection**

Total dues collected across the 76 PCE membership year came to approximately **218,000 Phi Credits**. The Board extends its sincere gratitude to all members who paid promptly. Full financial statements for 76 PCE will be circulated ahead of the Annual General Assembly, scheduled for 77-02-15.

**76 PCE Advocacy Activities Summary**

The past year was an exceptionally productive one for SHMA representation, and your Board is proud to share the following highlights.

**Waterfront League Sponsorship.** For the second consecutive year, the SHMA served as the primary sponsor of the **South Haven Waterfront League**, committing **85,000 Phi Credits** to support the League's programming, pitch maintenance, and community events throughout 76 PCE. The Waterfront League remains one of the most visible expressions of the south Haven community's identity, and our sponsorship continues to generate considerable goodwill among residents and visiting traders alike. The Board considers this investment a cornerstone of SHMA's community presence and intends to renew it in 77 PCE.

**Pedestrian Promenade Extension.** After nearly three years of sustained advocacy by the SHMA's Infrastructure and Planning Subcommittee — including two formal petitions, four public hearings, and a jointly submitted survey of merchant and resident foot-traffic data — the **CGC Civic Infrastructure Committee approved the extension of south Haven's pedestrian promenade by 1.2 kilometers** in 76-09. This is, without question, the Association's most significant planning victory in recent memory. The extended promenade will directly connect our waterfront commercial strip to the broader south Haven market district, increasing pedestrian flow to member businesses and improving the public character of our neighborhood. Construction is expected to commence in early 77 PCE, and we will keep members informed of timelines and any temporary disruptions.

On behalf of the Board, thank you for your membership and your trust. South Haven's commercial community is strongest when we act together, and 76 PCE demonstrated exactly what collective advocacy can achieve.
