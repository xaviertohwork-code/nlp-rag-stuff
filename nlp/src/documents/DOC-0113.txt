# West Haven Sector Administration
## Portside Business District — Quarterly Vacancy and Leasing Report
**Period:** Q4 76 PCE (76-10-01 to 76-12-31)
**Issued:** 77-01-22
**Prepared by:** Property Officer, West Haven Sector Administration
**Classification:** OPEN

---

### 1. Overview

Commercial vacancy in the Portside Business District rose to **8.7%** in Q4 76 PCE, compared with 7.1% recorded in Q3 76 PCE. The increase of 1.6 percentage points represents the largest single-quarter movement in the district in the past two years. The uptick is primarily attributable to a single consolidation event by one major occupant, detailed in Section 3 below. Underlying leasing demand across the broader Portside tenant base remained stable throughout the quarter.

---

### 2. Leasing Activity

New leases executed during Q4 totalled **9,800 square meters** across **17 tenants**. The tenant mix was weighted toward logistics support services, freight coordination offices, and a smaller number of maritime insurance and compliance consultancies. Lease lengths ranged from 12 to 36 months, with the majority falling in the 24-month band.

The average lease rate achieved in Q4 was **42 Phi Credits per square meter per month**, consistent with the trailing four-quarter average and reflecting no material pressure on headline rates despite the elevated vacancy environment. Rate stability is noted as a positive indicator for district fundamentals.

---

### 3. ONE Network Enterprises Consolidation

The principal driver of the Q4 vacancy increase was the departure of ONE Network Enterprises from **three mid-size office blocks** in the central Portside zone. ONE vacated a combined **14,200 square meters** of space as part of an internal consolidation programme. The West Haven Sector Administration has been advised that this consolidation reflects internal operational restructuring; no further detail has been provided to this office and no external-facing explanation has been issued by ONE Network Enterprises. The three vacated blocks are now formally relisted with the Sector Administration's commercial leasing registry and are available for immediate occupation.

It should be noted for the record that the 17 new leases executed during the quarter — totalling 9,800 square meters — were signed across a range of Portside sub-zones and were not concentrated in the ONE-vacated blocks. Absorption of the ONE vacancies is addressed in the Outlook section below.

---

### 4. Outlook

The Sector Administration projects that commercial vacancy in the Portside district will return to approximately **7.5% by Q2 77 PCE**, contingent on continued inbound interest from logistics and freight-adjacent operators. Preliminary expressions of interest received since quarter-close indicate that the ONE-vacated blocks are attracting disproportionate attention from logistics firms seeking consolidated floor plates in the immediate port precinct, which is consistent with Portside's established positioning as the primary logistics-services corridor in west Haven.

No significant lease expiries are scheduled in Q1 77 PCE that would materially affect the vacancy trajectory. The Sector Administration will monitor absorption of the ONE vacancies closely and issue an interim update should conditions deviate materially from the Q2 projection.
