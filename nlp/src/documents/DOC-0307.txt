## Daughter of Cyanite Chief Joins Opted Protest Movement

*Ada Oyelaran, 31, publicly renounces the Edge and steps into a new fight — one that puts her squarely against her father's empire*

---

**By Haven Herald Correspondent**

Ada Oyelaran, daughter of Cyanite Industries Director-General Takeshi Oyelaran, publicly Opted in 73 PCE at age 31, announcing her decision in a brief statement delivered outside a Genesis Labs Opting rehabilitation clinic in Central Haven on Tuesday morning. Within hours, she had formally affiliated herself with We The Opted, the humanist advocacy group that has been steadily growing its voice on the streets of Haven since its founding earlier this year.

What distinguished the announcement from the dozens of public Optings that take place in Haven each week was not merely the name attached to it. Standing before a small gathering of press and supporters, the 31-year-old made a point of introducing herself not simply as a private citizen making a private choice. "I am Ada Oyelaran, daughter of the Director-General of Cyanite Industries," she said, reading from a prepared statement, "and I am Opted." The choice of words was deliberate, her representatives later confirmed — a rhetorical signal unmistakable to anyone familiar with how megacorporation dynasties operate in Haven.

---

### WTO Response

We The Opted founder Mira Delacroix-Vey issued a statement welcoming Ada to the movement within the same afternoon. "We welcome Ada Oyelaran with open arms," Delacroix-Vey wrote in the release distributed to Haven media outlets. "Every Opted knows what it costs to make this choice honestly and publicly. Ada has made that choice, and she has made it honestly."

It is worth noting that the welcoming statement came from WTO founder Mira Delacroix-Vey herself — not, as some preliminary social-Edge commentary erroneously suggested, from any other WTO figure. Delacroix-Vey, who founded We The Opted earlier this year following her own personal history with corporate oversight, was unambiguous in her personal endorsement.

Delacroix-Vey added that Ada's presence "speaks to something that cannot be suppressed: the growing awareness, across every level of Haven society, that the Edge is not a gift freely given."

---

### Context: A New Benchmark

**ON MIRA DELACROIX-VEY'S OPTING**

*Mira Delacroix-Vey, the founder of We The Opted, herself publicly Opted in 68 PCE after departing from a mid-level compliance role at Phyrexis Group. She has said publicly that witnessing internal financial irregularities during her time at Phyrexis was the precipitating event. Her Opting in 68 PCE was widely covered and considered a landmark moment for Haven's nascent Opted community at the time.*

This publication's editors note that Ada Oyelaran's announcement carries a significance that extends beyond its political dimensions. The Haven Herald has characterized this as the highest-profile Opting since Mira Delacroix-Vey's own Opting in 68 PCE — a span of five years during which no comparable public figure had made a declaration of this visibility.

---

### Cyanite's Response

Cyanite Industries, when contacted by the Haven Herald for comment, declined to address the substance of Ada Oyelaran's statement or her affiliation with We The Opted. A spokesperson's reply, delivered in full, read as follows:

*"This is a personal family matter."*

The response, which arrived less than ninety minutes after the Haven Herald's inquiry was submitted through Cyanite's media relations office, was notably terse even by the standards of a corporation not known for loquacious press engagement. No follow-up clarification was offered, and no spokesperson was made available for interview.

---

### Background

Ada Oyelaran holds a degree in industrial engineering and spent several years in private-sector roles prior to her Opting. Sources familiar with her background suggest she brings technical knowledge of Cyanite's operational practices — knowledge, observers note, that may prove considerably more difficult to manage than a standard WTO organizer's public criticism.

Takeshi Oyelaran has served as Cyanite's Director-General for eighteen years. His office did not respond separately from the corporate media statement.

*— Haven Herald Correspondent*
