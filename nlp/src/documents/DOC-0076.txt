# We The Opted Rally Draws Record Crowd to Central Haven Plaza

**RENHWA MEDIA — HAVEN CORRESPONDENT DESK**
*Tarek Binali, Senior Correspondent | 77-03-18 PCE (2119-03-18 CE) | OPEN*

---

CENTRAL HAVEN — An estimated 8,000 people filled Central Haven Plaza on Thursday, making the gathering the largest We The Opted rally on record and a striking demonstration of the movement's expanding reach beyond its Opted core into Haven's broader disaffected population.

The crowd began assembling before dawn, occupying the civic esplanade between the CGC administrative buildings and the mixed residential districts that form Central Haven's backbone. By midmorning, the plaza was visibly at capacity, with overflow crowds spilling into the surrounding streets. Attendees wore the movement's characteristic grey armbands, though a notable portion of the crowd bore no identifying markers at all — a signal, organizers suggested beforehand, that the day's message was meant for Havenites who had not yet publicly aligned themselves with WTO.

---

## "They Work in the Dark So You Can't See"

The keynote address was delivered by We The Opted founder Mira Delacroix-Vey, who drew extended applause with a pointed demand for structural accountability. "Every vote the CGC casts shapes this city, shapes your life, shapes what you're allowed to breathe and eat and earn," she told the crowd, her voice carrying across the packed plaza. "And not one of those votes is visible to you. We are calling for the full and unconditional public disclosure of CGC voting records — every resolution, every member position, every abstention. They work in the dark so you can't see. We intend to turn the lights on."

Delacroix-Vey, a former Phyrexis compliance officer who Opted in 2110 after witnessing internal fraud, has positioned WTO in recent months as a vehicle not only for Opted rights but for broad civic reform. Thursday's crowd appeared to reflect that strategic expansion — the demographic range visible was wider than at previous rallies, with workers from port districts and industrial fringe neighborhoods joining the movement's traditional constituents.

---

## Oyelaran: "My Father Knows What Happens Down There"

The afternoon's most pointed address came from Ada Oyelaran — identified by organizers, and by the speaker herself, as the daughter of Cyanite Industries Director-General Takeshi Oyelaran — who delivered a detailed account of labor conditions in Cyanite's mining towns across Clairos.

"These are not abstract statistics," she told the crowd, her engineering background evident in the precision of her language. "I have the maintenance logs. I have the injury rates. I have the testimony of workers who were told that organizing was a terminable offense. My father knows what happens down there. The question is whether the rest of Haven is going to be allowed to know."

Oyelaran, who Opted publicly in 2115 and has since risen to Senior Organizer within WTO, was careful to present her criticisms as technical and documentable rather than personal, a style her supporters argue makes her considerably harder to dismiss. The crowd received her remarks with sustained applause.

To be clear on the day's division of labor: it was Delacroix-Vey who delivered the keynote calling for CGC voting record transparency, while Oyelaran's address focused specifically on labor conditions in Cyanite's mining operations. The distinction matters — WTO is simultaneously pursuing a transparency agenda and a labor-rights agenda, and Thursday's programme made the case that the two are inseparable.

---

## Drones at the Perimeter

Several attendees noted the presence of TEC surveillance drones circling the rally's outer perimeter throughout the afternoon session, visible against the grey Haven sky above the plaza's eastern edge.

At the time of publication, the CGC had not issued any official response to the rally or to the demands raised by its speakers.

Whether that silence constitutes a position is a question Central Haven Plaza appeared, on Thursday, to have already answered for itself.
